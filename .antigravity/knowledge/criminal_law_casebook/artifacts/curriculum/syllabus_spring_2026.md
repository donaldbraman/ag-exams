# Spring 2026 Syllabus Summary

## Analytical Frameworks
The course emphasizes three perspectives:
1. **Historical Change**: Evolution of law over time.
2. **Status & Equality**: Law as a site of group status competition and protection.
3. **Empirical Evidence**: Effectiveness of doctrine based on criminology and social science.

## Units & Topics
- **Unit 1: Foundations**: Theories of punishment, institutional actors (Legislatures, Prosecutors, Courts/Juries).
- **Unit 2: General Principles**: Actus Reus (Voluntary act, omissions), Mens Rea (MPC states, strict liability).
- **Unit 3: Specific Offenses**: Sexual Assault, Homicide (Degrees, Felony Murder, Provocation).
- **Unit 4: Defenses**: Self-Defense, Necessity, Duress, Insanity.
- **Unit 5: Inchoate & Group Crimes**: Attempts, Accomplice Liability, Conspiracy, RICO.
