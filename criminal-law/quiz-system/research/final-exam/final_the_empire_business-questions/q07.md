**Q7.** Assume that, whether or not a valid enterprise currently exists, Jack and Kevin agreed to form an operation where they would commit extortion and drug distribution. Are they guilty of RICO conspiracy?

(a) Yes, because an agreement to associate with and participate in a future enterprise through a pattern of racketeering activity constitutes a fully completed RICO conspiracy offense. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because they each had agreed to personally complete at least two specific predicate acts of racketeering within a ten-year period as mandated by the statute.
(c) No, because the criminal enterprise must be fully formed and presently operating before a legal conspiracy to conduct its illicit affairs can be formally charged against them.
(d) No, because Jack merely directed the organizational activity and did not personally or physically participate in the collection of extortion payments or the actual delivery of drugs.
(e) No, because a single overarching agreement cannot concurrently support both a RICO conspiracy charge and a substantive criminal charge for the underlying predicate racketeering offenses.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because under *Salinas* and *Rich*, a RICO conspiracy under § 1962(d) requires only an agreement to further an endeavor that, if completed, would satisfy all elements of a substantive RICO offense. It can even be an agreement to form a *future* enterprise. (b) is wrong because under *Salinas*, a RICO conspirator need not personally commit (or even agree to personally commit) two predicate acts; the agreement to further the enterprise is sufficient. (c) is wrong because under *Rich*, an agreement to form a future enterprise fully supports a conspiracy conviction. (d) is wrong because directing an enterprise's affairs from the top (as Jack did) is classic RICO participation; physical commission of the street-level crimes is not required. (e) is wrong because conspiracy and the completed substantive offenses do not merge; a defendant can easily be convicted of both.
