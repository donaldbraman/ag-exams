**Q5.** Dominic is charged with "using" a firearm during and in relation to a drug trafficking crime under 18 U.S.C. § 924(c) based on the exchange. Is he guilty?

(a) Not guilty, because a person who receives a firearm in exchange for drugs does not "use" the firearm within the meaning of the federal statute. <!-- correct -->
(b) Not guilty, because the AR-15 rifles were not actively deployed or brandished as dangerous weapons during the course of the drug transaction.
(c) Guilty, because trading drugs to acquire a firearm constitutes active employment of the weapon as currency in a narcotics transaction.
(d) Guilty, because Dominic constructively possessed the firearms in connection with a drug trafficking offense by taking them into his vehicle.
(e) Guilty, because receiving firearms during a drug transaction satisfies the statute's separate "carrying" prong as a strict matter of law.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under *Watson v. United States*, a defendant who trades drugs to acquire a gun does not "use" the firearm under § 924(c); only the party tendering the gun uses it as currency. (b) fails because under *Smith v. United States*, a gun CAN be "used" as currency in a trade without being brandished, provided the defendant is the one tendering it. (c) fails because it states the exact opposite of *Watson*. (d) fails because the question specifically asks about the "use" prong, and mere constructive possession does not constitute "use." (e) fails because receiving a gun is not automatically "carrying" it without evidence of physical transport on the person or in a vehicle.
