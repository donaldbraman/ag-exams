**Q3.** Is Laura guilty of constructively possessing the two kilograms of cocaine in the breakroom?

(a) Yes, because she had actual knowledge of the contraband's presence in a shared employee space and failed to immediately report it to the relevant authorities.
(b) Yes, because she made an incriminating statement acknowledging the presence of the drugs, which automatically satisfies the legal requirement for exercising dominion and control.
(c) No, because she was merely a salaried employee of the corporation, and only business owners or managers can constructively possess contraband found in shared corporate workspaces.
(d) No, because her mere awareness of the drugs does not establish that she had the power and intent to exercise dominion and control over the locked bag. <!-- correct -->
(e) No, because establishing constructive possession legally requires the defendant to be physically present in the room when the contraband is ultimately discovered by law enforcement.

**Answer:** (d)

**Explanation:** (d) is correct because constructive possession requires proof that the defendant (1) knew of the contraband, and (2) had the power and intent to exercise dominion and control over it (*Jenkins*, *White*). Laura lacked access to the locked bag and explicitly disclaimed control, so her awareness alone is legally insufficient. (a) is wrong because failing to report known contraband does not equate to exercising dominion and control over it. (b) is wrong because an incriminating statement proving awareness ("I know what's in that bag") does not automatically prove intent to control. (c) is wrong because employees can constructively possess items in a workspace if they actually have sufficient control; corporate ownership is not required. (e) is wrong because physical presence at the time of discovery is not required for constructive possession.
