# Scenario: Pinnacle Waste Syndicate

### Facts

1. The FBI's investigation established that Carmine owned "Pinnacle Waste," a lawfully chartered corporation with a central dispatch center, a fleet of trucks, and a corporate billing department.
2. Carmine and his capo, Dominic, used Pinnacle's dispatch center to coordinate illicit extortion collections from local businesses, and used the billing department to launder the extortion proceeds.
3. Carmine and Dominic agreed to establish a new, separate narcotics syndicate the following year.
4. Carmine directed Marcus, the Pinnacle dispatcher, to route the drug-carrying trucks around state weigh stations. Marcus replied, "I know you're moving drugs, but I don't want a cut of that money. I just want my regular salary." Marcus then routed the trucks as directed.
5. Two months later, Marcus attempted to quit. Carmine told Marcus, "If you ever stop routing my trucks, I will track you down and put you in a compactor." Marcus continued routing the trucks for four more months without contacting authorities.
6. Dominic personally handled the first major drug acquisition. He met a supplier and gave the supplier 2 kilograms of a controlled substance, receiving five unregistered AR-15 rifles in return to arm his extortion crew.
7. Chemical testing later established that the 2 kilograms were 100% pure fentanyl. Dominic had no idea the substance was fentanyl, genuinely believing it to be heroin.
8. Dominic and his armed crew visited a restaurant to collect a delinquent extortion debt. Dominic brandished his new AR-15, aimed it at the cashier, and explicitly demanded that the cash register be emptied into a bag. The restaurant owner, Victor, unexpectedly pulled a concealed revolver and fired a shot at Dominic, missing. Dominic returned fire, killing Victor.
9. Following Victor's death, Dominic decided to preemptively eliminate a rival gang leader, Elias. Dominic purchased a sniper rifle, drove to Elias's house, and set up a concealed firing position across the street. As Elias walked out his front door, Dominic tracked him through the rifle scope. Just as Dominic moved his finger to the trigger, the FBI suddenly surrounded his position and arrested him.
10. The FBI subsequently executed a search warrant at Dominic's residence. In the master bedroom, agents found a 9mm handgun in a locked nightstand drawer containing only his wife's belongings. Dominic stated to the agents, "I knew my wife still kept that 9mm in her drawer, but I don't have the key and I never touch it."
11. Dominic is charged under 18 U.S.C. § 922(g) (felon-in-possession) based on the 9mm. Ten years prior, Dominic had been convicted of state commercial fraud, a nonviolent offense, and sentenced to probation. Dominic genuinely believed this prior conviction was a misdemeanor, but under state law, it was classified as a felony punishable by up to two years in prison.
