## Scenario: Empire Waste Syndicate

### Facts

1. The investigation established that Empire Waste Management LLC is a legitimate, state-licensed waste disposal corporation. Jack is the sole owner and CEO; Laura is the salaried dispatcher; Kevin is a route supervisor.
2. In January, Jack verbally directed Kevin to use his waste collection routes to collect weekly extortion payments from ten local businesses and to deliver wholesale quantities of cocaine to local dealers.
3. Laura observed Kevin loading brick-shaped packages wrapped in duct tape into Empire Waste trucks. She knew these were drugs, but she continued to dispatch his routes solely because she wanted to keep her $80,000-per-year lawful salary.
4. Kevin stored a locked duffel bag containing two kilograms of cocaine in the shared Empire Waste employee breakroom.
5. Laura told Kevin: "I know what's in that bag, get it out of the breakroom." Kevin replied: "I am the only one with the lock combination, just ignore it." Laura never touched the bag.
6. On October 1, Kevin met with a rival syndicate member. Kevin handed over 100 grams of cocaine and received a loaded 9mm handgun in exchange.
7. Kevin possessed the 9mm handgun continuously thereafter.
8. In 2018, Kevin pled guilty to "commercial fraud" in state court and received a sentence of two years' probation. The maximum statutory penalty for the offense was 18 months in prison, making it a qualifying felony under federal law.
9. During the 2018 proceedings, Kevin's defense attorney sent him an email stating: "This conviction is classified as a misdemeanor. You are not a felon." The investigation confirmed Kevin genuinely believed this email.
10. On November 1, Jack and Kevin signed a written agreement to launch a new syndicate called "Tri-State Waste" the following year to control illegal gambling. No operations or overt acts for Tri-State have begun yet.
11. On November 15, Jack and Kevin agreed that Kevin would drive to Matthew's restaurant that night to break the windows to pressure Matthew into paying his missed protection money.
12. Kevin drove to the restaurant with the 9mm handgun in his pocket. He parked, put on a ski mask, grabbed a crowbar, and stepped out of his car, 50 feet from the restaurant doors.
13. Matthew, anticipating an attack, saw the masked Kevin approaching with the crowbar. Matthew stepped out the back door armed with a shotgun.
14. Upon seeing the shotgun, Kevin immediately dropped the crowbar, turned around, and sprinted away down the street.
15. Matthew yelled, "I have to end this permanently so you cartel goons stop bleeding my business dry!" Matthew's hands were trembling and his face was flushed red. He shot the fleeing Kevin in the back from 40 feet away, killing him instantly.
