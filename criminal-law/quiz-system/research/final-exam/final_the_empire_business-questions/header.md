# Scenario: Pinnacle Waste Enterprise

### Facts

1. The FBI's investigation established that Carmine owned "Pinnacle Waste," a lawfully chartered corporation with a dispatch center, a fleet of trucks, and a corporate billing department.
2. Carmine and his capo, Dominic, used Pinnacle's dispatch center to coordinate illicit extortion collections from local businesses, and used the billing department to launder the extortion proceeds.
3. Looking to expand, Carmine and Dominic agreed to form a new side-operation to distribute narcotics, explicitly agreeing to use Pinnacle trucks to transport the drugs in the future once they secured a reliable supply.
4. Carmine directed Marcus, the Pinnacle dispatcher, to route the drug-carrying trucks around state weigh stations. Marcus replied, "I know you're moving drugs, but I don't want a cut of that money. I just want my regular salary." Marcus then routed the trucks as directed.
5. Two months later, Marcus attempted to quit. Carmine told Marcus, "If you ever stop routing my trucks, I will track you down and put you in a compactor." Marcus continued routing the trucks for four more months without contacting authorities.
6. Dominic personally handled the first major drug acquisition. He met a supplier and traded 2 kilograms of a controlled substance in exchange for five unregistered AR-15 rifles to arm his extortion crew.
7. Chemical testing later established that the 2 kilograms Dominic traded were 100% pure fentanyl. Dominic had no idea the substance was fentanyl, genuinely believing it to be heroin.
8. As part of the Pinnacle extortion ring, Dominic and his armed crew entered Victor's restaurant to collect a "protection payment." When Victor resisted by throwing a heavy ledger at Dominic's face, Dominic immediately drew his weapon and shot Victor dead.
9. Following Victor's death, Dominic decided to preemptively eliminate a rival gang leader, Elias. Dominic purchased a sniper rifle, drove to Elias's house, parked, and was sitting in his car with the rifle loaded, waiting for Elias to emerge, when the FBI arrested him.
10. Following Dominic's arrest, the FBI executed a search warrant at the home Dominic shared with his wife. Inside the wife's personal bedside drawer—which contained only her personal belongings—agents found a 9mm handgun legally registered to her.
11. During the search, Dominic stated to the agents, "I told my wife to get rid of that 9mm last week. I knew it was still in the house."
12. Dominic had a single prior criminal conviction for state-level commercial fraud, a nonviolent offense. Dominic genuinely believed this prior conviction carried a maximum sentence of six months, though state law actually made it punishable by up to two years in prison.

### Stem 1: Enterprise and Accomplice Liability

Draft a memo evaluating the enterprise structure of Pinnacle Waste based on the FBI investigation. Also, analyze Marcus's criminal liability given his statements and actions during the transport routing.

### Stem 2: Weapons and Narcotics Charges

Focus on Dominic's weapons and narcotics charges. Analyze his drug-for-gun exchange, the fentanyl discrepancy, and the firearm found in his wife's drawer. Finally, evaluate the charges stemming from his status as a prior offender, addressing both his knowledge of the sentence exposure and the constitutional limits of the statute.

### Stem 3: Homicides and Attempt

Finally, analyze the homicides and attempted homicides. Determine the specific defenses Dominic might raise for Victor's death, whether Dominic's actions toward Elias constitute a criminal attempt, and whether Carmine can be held liable for Dominic's actions.
