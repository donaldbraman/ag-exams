# Scenario: Empire Waste Management

### Facts

1. Empire Waste Management LLC is a state-licensed waste disposal corporation. Jack is the sole owner and CEO; Laura is the salaried dispatcher; Kevin is a route supervisor.
2. Jack verbally directed Kevin to use his waste collection routes to collect weekly extortion payments from local businesses and to deliver wholesale quantities of cocaine. Kevin agreed.
3. Laura observed Kevin loading brick-shaped packages wrapped in duct tape into Empire Waste trucks. She knew these were drugs, but she continued to dispatch his routes solely to keep her $80,000-per-year lawful salary.
4. Kevin stored a locked duffel bag containing two kilograms of cocaine in the Empire Waste employee breakroom.
5. Laura told Kevin: "I know what's in that bag, get it out of the breakroom." Kevin replied: "I am the only one with the lock combination, just ignore it." Laura never touched the bag and had no physical access to it.
6. On October 1, Kevin met with a rival syndicate member. Kevin handed over 100 grams of cocaine and received a loaded 9mm handgun in exchange.
7. Kevin possessed the 9mm handgun continuously thereafter.
8. In 2018, Kevin pled guilty to "commercial fraud" in state court. The maximum statutory penalty for the offense was 18 months in prison, making it a qualifying felony under federal law.
9. During the 2018 proceedings, Kevin's defense attorney sent him an email stating: "This conviction is classified as a misdemeanor under state law. You are not a felon." Kevin read and genuinely believed this email.
10. On November 15, Matthew, a local restaurant owner, refused to pay his weekly extortion payment. Jack told Kevin: "Break his windows tonight and scare him into paying." Kevin agreed, stating, "I'll take care of it tonight."
11. Kevin drove to Matthew's restaurant, parked directly in front with the engine running, and grabbed a crowbar and ski mask from his passenger seat. He consciously disregarded the substantial and unjustifiable risk that his highly visible, armed arrival would provoke a violent, potentially deadly confrontation.
12. Before Kevin could put on the mask or open his door, Matthew emerged from the restaurant. Recognizing Kevin as the enforcer, Matthew yelled, "I'm not paying you!" and shattered Kevin's driver-side window with a baseball bat.
13. Kevin immediately drew the 9mm handgun he had received in the prior trade and shot Matthew, killing him instantly.

### Stem 1: Initial Operations

We are preparing to charge Jack, Kevin, and Laura in a multi-count federal indictment based on the ongoing waste management operation and Kevin's gun possession. I need you to evaluate the viability of the charges based on the established facts. Pay special attention to the enterprise structure, possession boundaries, and whether Kevin's reliance on his old lawyer defeats the gun charge.
