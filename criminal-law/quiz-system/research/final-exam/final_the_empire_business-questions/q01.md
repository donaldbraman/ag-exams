**Q1.** Is Empire Waste Management LLC a valid RICO enterprise under these facts?

(a) Yes, because a legitimate corporation can serve as a RICO enterprise when its ordinary business structure, such as its collection routes, is repurposed to conduct racketeering activity. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because any business owned by a single individual automatically constitutes an association-in-fact enterprise, regardless of its legal status or whether it engages in interstate commerce.
(c) No, because Empire Waste Management LLC is a validly state-licensed corporation, and under the statute, a legally chartered business cannot constitute an inherently criminal organization.
(d) No, because Jack and Kevin are merely employees of the corporation and a RICO defendant cannot be employed by or associated with an enterprise that is a separate legal entity.
(e) No, because the extortion and drug distribution operations lacked a formal hierarchy, an internal command structure, or written rules governing the execution of the criminal schemes.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because under RICO, a legitimate corporation can itself constitute the enterprise when its ordinary business structure (here, the waste collection routes) is repurposed to conduct or facilitate racketeering activity. (b) is wrong because single-owner businesses do not automatically constitute associations-in-fact, and the corporate entity itself is the charged enterprise here. (c) is wrong because courts have consistently held that lawful entities can serve as the enterprise; an inherently criminal organization is not required. (d) is wrong because RICO explicitly targets defendants who are employed by or associated with an enterprise, meaning the enterprise can be a separate legal entity from the defendants. (e) is wrong because *Boyle* established that an enterprise need not have a formal hierarchy or written rules.
