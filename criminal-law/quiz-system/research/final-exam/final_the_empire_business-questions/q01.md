**Q1.** Carmine is charged with conducting the affairs of a RICO enterprise under 18 U.S.C. § 1962(c). Can Pinnacle Waste constitute a valid RICO enterprise under the statute?

(a) Yes, because a legitimate corporation can serve as a RICO enterprise when its ordinary business structure is repurposed to conduct racketeering activity. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because an association-in-fact enterprise requires an inherently criminal organization without a lawful charter to satisfy the statutory requirements.
(c) No, because the hub-and-spoke structure of the corporation lacks horizontal relationships among the legitimate employees working in the departments.
(d) Yes, because any business owned by a person who commits two or more predicate acts automatically becomes a RICO enterprise by law.
(e) No, because the extortion and money laundering offenses must be committed against the corporation itself rather than conducted through it.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under RICO, a legitimate corporation can itself constitute the enterprise when its ordinary business structure (like Pinnacle's dispatch and billing) is repurposed to conduct or facilitate racketeering activity. (b) fails because an enterprise need not be an inherently criminal organization. (c) fails because Pinnacle is a formal corporate enterprise, not an informal hub-and-spoke association-in-fact. (d) fails because ownership alone, without conducting affairs *through* the enterprise, is insufficient. (e) fails because § 1962(c) explicitly targets conducting the enterprise's affairs *through* racketeering, not merely victimizing the enterprise.
