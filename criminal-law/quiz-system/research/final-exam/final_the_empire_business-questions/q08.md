### Stem 2: The November 15 Incident

We just received the police file regarding a November 15 incident at Matthew's restaurant, which ended in Matthew's death. I need you to evaluate the homicide, attempt, and accessorial liability charges against Kevin and Jack stemming from this escalation.

---

**Q8.** Are Jack and Kevin guilty of conspiracy to commit extortion regarding the November 15 plan?

(a) Yes, because Jack's explicit directive and Kevin's immediate affirmative response formed a mutual bilateral agreement to commit an unlawful extortionate act against Matthew's business. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because Jack, acting as the corporate CEO of Empire Waste, is held strictly liable for any violent crimes committed by his employees during their working hours.
(c) No, because Kevin had not yet successfully broken the restaurant windows or obtained any extortion money from Matthew before the deadly confrontation unexpectedly occurred.
(d) No, because their brief conversation was merely a preliminary negotiation that crucially lacked the specific tactical details regarding exactly how the nighttime extortion would be executed.
(e) No, because a corporate boss issuing a unilateral order to a subordinate employee inherently lacks the mutual intent required to establish a valid bilateral conspiratorial agreement.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because conspiracy requires an agreement between two or more persons to commit a criminal act, accompanied by the specific intent to achieve the unlawful objective. Jack's command and Kevin's response ("I'll take care of it") perfectly establish this bilateral agreement. (b) is wrong because corporate officers are not strictly liable for employee crimes without proof of their own involvement or agreement. (c) is wrong because conspiracy is complete upon the agreement (and in some jurisdictions, an overt act), regardless of whether the target crime is successfully completed. (d) is wrong because the agreement was complete and unambiguous; they did not need to finalize exact tactical details for the conspiracy to legally form. (e) is wrong because an order and its acceptance by a subordinate fully satisfies the mutuality requirement for a bilateral agreement.
