**Q2.** Is Laura guilty as an accomplice to Kevin's drug distribution?

(a) Yes, because she knew Kevin was distributing drugs, and her act of dispatching the trucks provided the necessary logistical assistance to his ongoing criminal operation.
(b) Yes, because retaining her lawful $80,000 salary provided her with a continuous financial stake in the successful outcome of Kevin's illicit drug distribution enterprise.
(c) No, because her sole motive was to maintain her lawful salary, meaning she lacked the specific purpose required to facilitate Kevin's underlying drug distribution offense. <!-- correct -->
(d) No, because an employee cannot be held liable as an accomplice for the criminal acts of a supervisor unless the employee physically participates in the crime.
(e) No, because she did not verbally agree to participate in the drug distribution, which is a necessary prerequisite to establish derivative liability for a subordinate employee.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** (c) is correct because under the federal standard for accomplice liability (and most states), the defendant must act with the purpose of promoting or facilitating the offense. Mere knowledge that assistance will aid a crime, coupled with a desire to keep a lawful salary (without a stake in the criminal venture itself), is insufficient. (a) is wrong because knowledge without purpose does not satisfy the mens rea for accomplice liability in this context. (b) is wrong because receiving a fixed, pre-existing lawful salary does not constitute a "stake in the outcome" of the criminal venture. (d) is wrong because physical participation is not required; providing logistical assistance would satisfy the actus reus if she had the requisite purpose. (e) is wrong because accomplice liability does not require a prior verbal agreement, unlike conspiracy.
