**Q2.** Assume that, whether or not Pinnacle Waste is an enterprise, Carmine is charged with RICO conspiracy under § 1962(d) for the agreement to form the new drug distribution side-operation. Is he guilty?

(a) Guilty, because an agreement to associate with and participate in a yet-to-be-formed racketeering enterprise can constitute a completed RICO conspiracy offense. <!-- correct -->
(b) Guilty, because he has already personally committed at least two predicate acts of racketeering through his management of the extortion scheme.
(c) Not guilty, because the enterprise did not exist at the time of the agreement, meaning there was no entity to conduct affairs through.
(d) Not guilty, because a RICO conspiracy requires the conspirators to have actually secured the illicit supply necessary to execute their criminal purpose.
(e) Not guilty, because applying conspiracy liability to an unformed enterprise violates the fundamental requirement that the enterprise have sufficient longevity.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under *United States v. Rich*, an agreement to form a future enterprise can constitute a completed RICO conspiracy offense. The enterprise need not actually exist at the time of the agreement. (b) fails because a RICO conspirator need not personally commit two predicate acts (*Salinas*). (c) fails because *Rich* explicitly allows future-tense enterprises for conspiracy liability. (d) fails because conspiracy is an inchoate offense that does not require the object to be fully achievable or completed at the time of agreement. (e) fails because the longevity requirement applies to the structure of an actual association-in-fact enterprise, not to the inchoate agreement to form one.
