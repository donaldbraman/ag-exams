**Q3.** Assume Deacon is charged with depraved heart murder for Chloe's fatal overdose in a jurisdiction following the *Beckwitt* standard. Which of the following is the most likely outcome?

(a) Guilty, because formulating and distributing a drug batch known to be highly likely to cause fatal overdoses satisfies the requirement that conduct be reasonably likely, if not certain, to cause death. <!-- correct -->
(b) Not guilty, because depraved heart murder requires that the defendant act with the specific purpose of causing a fatality, a mental state which Deacon clearly lacked during the distribution.
(c) Guilty, because the distribution of illicit narcotics is an inherently dangerous felony that automatically establishes extreme indifference to human life without any further showing of subjective or objective risk.
(d) Not guilty, because Chloe voluntarily choosing to inject the drugs constitutes an independent intervening cause that entirely breaks the chain of proximate causation under the strict depraved heart standard.
(e) Guilty, because any conscious disregard of a substantial and unjustifiable risk of death is legally sufficient to elevate an unintentional killing to the level of depraved heart murder.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Under Maryland's standard in *Beckwitt*, depraved heart murder requires conduct that is "reasonably likely, if not certain, to cause death," which is a higher threshold than standard recklessness. Distributing drugs known to be highly likely to cause fatal overdoses meets this elevated threshold. (b) is wrong because depraved heart murder is an unintentional homicide standard; it does not require a specific purpose to kill. (c) is wrong because this describes the felony murder rule, not the depraved heart standard, which requires an independent evaluation of the conduct's dangerousness. (d) is wrong because a drug user's voluntary ingestion is generally considered a foreseeable result of drug distribution, not a chain-breaking intervening cause. (e) is wrong because "conscious disregard of a substantial and unjustifiable risk" is the standard for manslaughter, not depraved heart murder.
