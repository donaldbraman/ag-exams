# Scenario: Apex Freight and the Warehouse Operation

### Facts

1. Marcus Vance is the CEO of Apex Freight, a registered commercial trucking corporation with 50 employees. Vance utilizes Apex’s legitimate payroll system, dispatch software, and warehouse to coordinate both legal auto-parts shipments and illegal narcotics distribution across state lines.
2. Vance hired Elias Thorne as the warehouse manager. Vance told Thorne, "We move off-book oxycodone in the blue crates. Route them safely, and I'll give you a 20% bump on your legitimate salary." Thorne agreed and consistently routed the crates. Thorne despises the drug trade and never handles the drugs himself, but he routes them to secure the extra money to pay off his mortgage.
3. Vance provided Darius Locke, a security driver, with a loaded 9mm handgun for the delivery routes. Locke was previously convicted of a state felony for financial fraud and sentenced to two years of probation. Upon sentencing, Locke's state probation officer explicitly told him, "Because you only got probation, this is classified as a misdemeanor, not a felony." Based on this, Locke openly carries the 9mm.
4. Vance secretly packed the crates with 400 grams of pure fentanyl, but left the original 'Oxycodone' labels on the crates, which Locke never opened.
5. During a delivery stop, Locke traded three blue crates of fentanyl to a local street dealer in exchange for an AR-15 rifle, stating he needed "heavier firepower for these runs."
6. Later that afternoon, Locke’s truck was blocked by a rival crew, with one member pointing a shotgun out of the car window in his direction. Panicking, Locke fired the AR-15 blindly out his open window to scare them off. One bullet passed through a store window, striking and killing a clerk inside.
7. Following the convenience store shooting, local police traced the truck's commercial license plate back to Apex Freight and executed a multi-agency search warrant on the main warehouse. During the raid, Thorne was found sitting at his dispatch desk. Agents located the AR-15 rifle inside a locked employee locker in the shared breakroom 50 feet away. Thorne, as manager, held the only master key on his desk. When questioned, Thorne stated, "I know Locke's rifle is in there, but I refuse to touch it."
8. The investigation revealed that two weeks before the raid, Locke agreed with an outside crew to rob the Apex safe. Locke drew a map of the security cameras and purchased black ski masks. However, the day before they were supposed to acquire a blowtorch to breach the safe, Locke texted the crew: "I'm out. Too much police heat around the warehouse lately," and threw his ski mask away.
