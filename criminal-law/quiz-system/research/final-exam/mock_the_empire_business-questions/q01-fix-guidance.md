**Q1.** Can Apex Freight serve as the "enterprise" for a RICO prosecution against Marcus Vance?

(a) Yes, because Vance utilized Apex's legitimate business structure and infrastructure to facilitate the illicit distribution of narcotics. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because a registered commercial corporation with a primary legal purpose cannot satisfy the association-in-fact requirement.
(c) No, because the government must prove the enterprise possessed an exclusively criminal purpose rather than a mixed legal-illegal operation.
(d) Yes, but only if the prosecution establishes that the fifty employees were fully aware of and consented to the narcotics distribution.
(e) Yes, because any business entity that hires a convicted felon automatically triggers the enterprise element under federal racketeering statutes.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** A legitimate corporation can constitute a RICO enterprise when its ordinary business structure (like payroll, software, and warehouses) is repurposed to conduct or facilitate racketeering activity. (b) is wrong because an enterprise does not need to be an informal "association-in-fact"; legal entities like corporations expressly qualify under the statute. (c) is wrong because the lawfulness of the corporation's formal charter or its mixed operations does not shield it. (d) is wrong because a unified enterprise structure does not require every employee to be aware of the racketeering activity. (e) is wrong because hiring a felon is not an enumerated predicate act and does not automatically convert a business into a RICO enterprise.
