# Scenario: The Warehouse Operation

Leo, Artie, Silas, and Dante met in a diner. They pooled $40,000 to rent an industrial warehouse, purchase chemical reactors, and acquire firearms, explicitly agreeing to manufacture Schedule II synthetic opioids. Dante maintained exclusive possession of the only key to the warehouse's reinforced steel safe, where he stored the finished product each night. 

During an active chemical synthesis, the primary reactor malfunctioned and its temperature gauge redlined. Artie, the chemist, calculated that the volatile mixture would detonate within twelve minutes, yielding a blast radius that would level the warehouse and an adjacent occupied residential block. Leo, his face flushed and jaw clenched as the redlining gauge threatened his entire product line, drew a 9mm pistol and pressed the barrel to Artie's temple. "Steal the nitrogen tanker from the lot next door and flood our reactor, or I will put a bullet in your brain right now," Leo stated. 

Benny, a local mechanic hired to fix the lab's generator, witnessed the threat. Sweating profusely, he tossed Artie his master set of hotwiring tools and yelled, "I don't care about your lab, I just want to survive!" 

Meanwhile, outside the active lab, Vargas, a rival dealer, approached the loading dock heavily armed to hijack the drug shipment. Silas, the operation's perimeter guard who had a documented medical history of severe schizophrenia, stopped pacing. His eyes widened and he began muttering to himself as Vargas approached. Silas dropped to his knees, pointed his rifle at Vargas, and screamed, "Go back to hell, fire-demon!" Silas fired three shots, killing Vargas. Upon arrest, Silas told police he was proud to have slain a monster made of smoke to protect the castle.

Inside, Artie successfully flooded the reactor with the nitrogen, preventing the explosion. Silas's gunshots drew passing police, who raided the warehouse, seized the drugs from the safe, and arrested Artie, Benny, Silas, and Leo. Dante was not present at the warehouse. 

Later that evening, Hector, an enforcer for Vargas's gang, kicked open Dante's apartment door. Hector sat heavily on Dante's living room couch and placed a revolver on his lap. "My crew is five minutes away. When they get here, we are executing you for what happened to Vargas," Hector said, leaning back and resting his hands behind his head. Dante visually confirmed the apartment's rear fire-escape window was unlocked and unobstructed, providing a safe and immediate avenue of retreat. Dante panicked, certain that Hector was drawing the weapon to kill him right then.
