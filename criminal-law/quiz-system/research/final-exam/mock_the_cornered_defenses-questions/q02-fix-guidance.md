## Issue 1 — audit

**Q2.** Assume Benny is charged as an accomplice to Artie's theft of the tanker. Under the traditional standard requiring the purpose to promote or facilitate the offense, is Benny guilty?

(a) Guilty, because his explicit desire to survive the explosion demonstrates that he acted with the conscious object to actively facilitate the successful theft. <!-- correct -->
(b) Guilty, because providing specialized tools to a known criminal operation automatically satisfies the strict intent requirement for traditional common law accomplice liability.
(c) Not guilty, because his express statement that he did not care about the lab negates the required specific intent to facilitate the underlying theft.
(d) Not guilty, because he merely possessed knowledge that Artie would commit the theft, which remains legally insufficient for traditional common law accomplice liability.
(e) Not guilty, because his involvement was strictly limited to providing physical tools he already possessed for completing his legitimate mechanical repair contract.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** To be liable as an accomplice, a defendant must assist the principal with the specific purpose (conscious object) of promoting or facilitating the crime. Although Benny did not care about the drug lab's success, he explicitly threw the tools because he "wanted to survive." Since his survival depended entirely on Artie successfully stealing the tanker to flood the reactor, Benny acted with the conscious object that the theft succeed. (b) is incorrect because providing tools with mere knowledge of their illicit use is generally insufficient under the traditional purpose standard (*Gladstone*). (c) is incorrect because Benny's indifference to the lab does not negate his specific purpose to facilitate the *theft*, which he needed to occur to live. (d) is incorrect because Benny had more than mere knowledge; he had a direct stake in the theft's success (his life). (e) is incorrect because providing ordinary tools with the specific purpose to facilitate a crime perfectly satisfies the actus reus of accomplice liability.
