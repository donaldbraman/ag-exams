**Q5.** Assume Arthur is prosecuted for depraved heart murder. Which of the following provides the strongest doctrinal basis for sustaining this specific charge?

(a) Arthur's active participation in dragging the unconscious Marcus into dense brush and abandoning him manifested an extreme indifference to human life. <!-- correct -->
(b) Arthur's status as a former chemistry professor meant his failure to perform a professional rescue constituted an intent to cause death.
(c) The sale of any illicit controlled substance constitutes an inherently dangerous felony sufficient to establish implied malice under the traditional rule.
(d) Arthur's initial attempt to dial 911 before being stopped by Chloe proves he had a conscious object to cause Marcus's demise.
(e) Marcus's death was a highly extraordinary result that satisfies the objective foreseeability standard required for second-degree depraved heart murder convictions.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Depraved heart murder requires recklessness manifesting extreme indifference to the value of human life. Dragging an unconscious, overdosing person into dense brush and abandoning them to die without medical intervention easily meets this elevated standard of gross recklessness. (b) fails because professional status does not automatically convert an omission into an intentional murder. (c) fails because this describes the structure of felony murder (implied malice via a predicate felony), not the gross recklessness required for a depraved heart murder conviction. (d) fails because attempting to dial 911 demonstrates a desire to save the victim, which directly contradicts having a conscious object (intent) to cause death. (e) fails because depraved heart murder requires subjectively reckless conduct highly likely to cause death, not merely an extraordinary result.
