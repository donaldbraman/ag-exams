**Q8.** Assume Silas is charged with conspiracy to distribute synthetic drugs. Can the prosecution establish the agreement element without an explicit verbal or written contract between Silas and Arthur?

(a) The agreement element is satisfied because a conspiratorial agreement can be inferred from their coordinated conduct of renting a warehouse and synthesizing drugs together. <!-- correct -->
(b) The agreement element fails because conspiracy requires direct evidence of an explicit verbal or written contract between the parties outlining the criminal enterprise.
(c) The agreement element is satisfied because Silas's mere presence in the motel room during the transaction creates a conclusive presumption of an agreement.
(d) The agreement element fails because Silas's subordinate relationship as a graduate student legally precludes him from forming a voluntary and binding bilateral agreement.
(e) The agreement element is satisfied because Wharton's Rule automatically establishes an agreement whenever two individuals participate in committing any underlying criminal offense.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. A conspiracy requires an agreement to commit a crime, but courts rarely require an explicit, formal contract. An agreement can be inferred from concert of action. Silas and Arthur's coordinated behavior—renting a warehouse and working together for three weeks to synthesize the drug—is strong circumstantial evidence of a mutual agreement.
(b) is incorrect because the law explicitly allows conspiratorial agreements to be implicit and inferred from circumstantial conduct.
(c) is incorrect because mere presence at the scene of a crime is insufficient to prove participation in a conspiracy.
(d) is incorrect because a subordinate academic or employment relationship does not negate legal capacity to enter into a criminal conspiracy.
(e) is incorrect because Wharton's Rule prevents conspiracy charges for crimes that logically require two people (like dueling), rather than establishing agreements.
