**Q4.** Assume that Arthur had a legal duty to act. Can the prosecution establish that Arthur's conduct in blocking the 911 call and locking the door was the factual cause of Julian's death?

(a) Arthur's interference is a factual cause because Julian would have had a 95% chance of survival but for Arthur blocking the 911 call. <!-- correct -->
(b) Arthur's interference is not a factual cause because the toxic chemical byproduct was the sole physical mechanism causing Julian's acute cardiac arrhythmia.
(c) Arthur's interference is a factual cause because the concurrent sufficient causes doctrine treats any delay in emergency treatment as an automatic but-for cause.
(d) Arthur's interference is not a factual cause because the prosecution cannot prove with absolute certainty that Julian would have survived without the delay.
(e) Arthur's interference is a factual cause because blocking a medical emergency call is a strict liability offense that completely removes the causation requirement.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Factual cause requires a showing that the result would not have occurred "but for" the defendant's conduct. Because medical testimony established a 95% survival rate with prompt intervention, Arthur's acts of knocking away the phone and locking the door were a necessary condition for Julian's death at that time.
(b) is incorrect because but-for causation does not require the defendant's act to be the sole physical mechanism, only a necessary antecedent.
(c) is incorrect because the concurrent sufficient causes doctrine applies only when two independent lethal forces act simultaneously.
(d) is incorrect because the law does not demand 100% absolute certainty; proving a 95% probability of survival without the interference easily satisfies the standard.
(e) is incorrect because causation remains an element that must be proven regardless of whether an act is otherwise unlawful.
