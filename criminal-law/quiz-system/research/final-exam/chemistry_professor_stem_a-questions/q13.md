**Q13.** Assume the facts of Stem 2: Vance held the only key to the lockbox under Thorne's passenger seat. Can the state establish that Thorne constructively possessed the 500 grams of narcotics found inside?

(a) Yes, because Thorne's knowledge of the contents, his regular proximity to the lockbox, and his joint participation in the illicit enterprise establish his intent and power to exercise control. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because Vance retained exclusive physical control of the only key, rendering it legally impossible for Thorne to establish the absolute dominion required for a constructive possession conviction.
(c) Yes, because mere presence in a vehicle containing illegal contraband automatically establishes constructive possession for all passengers under the universal elements of the modern dangerous-items heuristic doctrine.
(d) No, because Thorne was merely an employee in Vance's operation, meaning constructive possession is strictly limited to the primary owner of the vehicle where the illegal items are discovered.
(e) Yes, because Thorne possessed a key to the passenger door, which explicitly creates an irrebuttable presumption of exclusive ownership over all personal containers located anywhere within the vehicle's cabin.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because constructive possession requires intent and power to control the item, which can be established by joint participation in a criminal enterprise, knowledge of the contents, and proximity to the contraband. (b) is wrong because exclusive physical control is not required; constructive possession can be joint. (c) is wrong because mere presence is constitutionally insufficient to prove possession without further connecting links. (d) is wrong because subordinates and co-conspirators can exercise joint constructive possession. (e) is wrong because while a key to the door is strong evidence of access, it creates a factual inference for the jury, not an irrebuttable presumption.
