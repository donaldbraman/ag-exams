**Q6.** Assume the jury is considering whether to convict Vance of gross-negligence involuntary manslaughter rather than depraved-heart murder for abandoning Gable. What is the fundamental doctrinal difference between these two standards in this context?

(a) Manslaughter requires subjective awareness of the risk, while depraved-heart murder relies entirely on what an objective, reasonable person would have known at the time.
(b) Manslaughter requires Vance to have acted with the purpose of causing harm, while depraved-heart murder requires only that he should have known of the substantial risk.
(c) Manslaughter involves a gross deviation from the standard of care a reasonable person would observe, while depraved-heart murder requires recklessness manifesting an extreme indifference to human life. <!-- correct -->
(d) Manslaughter applies when the defendant's acts are inherently dangerous in the abstract, whereas depraved-heart murder applies when they are dangerously executed in their specific commission.
(e) Manslaughter requires the victim's death to be highly extraordinary and unpredictable, while depraved-heart murder requires the death to be reasonably foreseeable to the average person.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** (c) is correct because the fundamental line between the two grades of unintentional homicide is that manslaughter requires a gross deviation from a reasonable standard of care, while depraved-heart murder requires a higher threshold of recklessness manifesting extreme indifference to human life. (a) fails because both can involve subjective awareness depending on the jurisdiction, and depraved-heart is not purely objective. (b) fails because neither requires the purpose to cause harm; both deal with unintentional outcomes. (d) fails because the abstract vs. facts-as-committed distinction applies to the felony-murder rule, not the manslaughter/murder line. (e) fails because "highly extraordinary" is a standard for proximate causation, not the distinction between manslaughter and murder mental states.
