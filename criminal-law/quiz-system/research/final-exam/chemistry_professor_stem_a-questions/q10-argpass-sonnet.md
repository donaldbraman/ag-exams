**Q10.** Assume the DA charges Chloe as an accomplice to Arthur's distribution of Compound Y to Marcus. To secure a conviction, what mens rea must the prosecution prove regarding Chloe's facilitation?

(a) That she had the true purpose of promoting or facilitating the distribution of the drugs when she brought Marcus to the van.
(b) That she was merely aware her actions in bringing Marcus to the van would practically result in a drug transaction occurring.
(c) That she acted with gross criminal negligence regarding the substantial likelihood that Arthur would distribute the drugs to the waiting buyer.
(d) That she possessed the specific intent to cause Marcus's subsequent fatal overdose when she actively facilitated the illicit chemical sale.
(e) That she knew her actions were specifically prohibited by the jurisdiction's Controlled Substances Act and intended to violate that statute.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Accomplice liability requires that the defendant act with the true purpose of promoting or facilitating the target offense. By bringing the buyer and standing watch, Chloe demonstrated purpose to facilitate the drug distribution. (b) fails because mere knowledge or awareness that a crime will occur is generally insufficient for accomplice liability. (c) fails because gross negligence is a standard for involuntary manslaughter, not the purpose required for accomplice liability to a specific intent or distribution crime. (d) fails because the target offense is drug distribution, not murder; she only needs purpose regarding the distribution itself. (e) fails because ignorance of the law is generally no excuse; the prosecution need not prove she knew the specific statute she was violating, only that she intended to facilitate the distribution act.
