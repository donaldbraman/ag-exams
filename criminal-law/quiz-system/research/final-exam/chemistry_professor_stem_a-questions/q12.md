**Q12.** Assume the prosecution charges Silas with possession of the 50 grams of drugs found in his trunk. Which of the following establishes Silas's constructive possession of the drugs?

(a) Silas had constructive possession because he was aware of the drugs' presence and had exclusive control over them by holding the only set of car keys. <!-- correct -->
(b) Silas lacked constructive possession because Arthur was the person who physically placed the drugs in the trunk, breaking the chain of possession.
(c) Silas had constructive possession because he participated in synthesizing the drugs, which creates an irrebuttable presumption of ongoing actual possession.
(d) Silas lacked constructive possession because his explicit refusal to distribute any more of the drug legally negated his intent to exercise control.
(e) Silas had constructive possession because driving the vehicle makes him strictly liable for any contraband found anywhere within the car's interior.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Constructive possession requires both awareness of the contraband's presence and the ability to exercise dominion and control over it. Silas knew the 50 grams of drugs were in his trunk, and by holding the only set of car keys, he possessed exclusive control over the area where the drugs were stored.
(b) is incorrect because who physically placed the items is irrelevant so long as the defendant subsequently maintains awareness and control.
(c) is incorrect because prior synthesis of the drugs does not create an irrebuttable presumption of ongoing present possession.
(d) is incorrect because possession requires only the intent to exercise control over the item, not necessarily the intent to distribute it.
(e) is incorrect because driving a vehicle does not create strict liability for hidden contraband if the driver lacks awareness of it.
