**Q12.** Silas is charged as an accomplice to Arthur's manufacturing of the Schedule I substance. Silas argues he only provided the glassware and did not personally synthesize the drugs. Under the common law rule for accomplice liability (*Lauria*/*Gladstone*), does Silas have the required mens rea to be an accomplice to manufacturing?

(a) Yes, because he knew exactly what Arthur was going to do with the glassware, and mere knowledge of the principal's criminal intent is always sufficient for accomplice liability.
(b) Yes, because his agreement to receive a 20% cut of the sales gives him a financial stake in the venture, elevating his knowledge to a true purpose that the crime succeed. <!-- correct -->
(c) No, because providing perfectly legal and unscheduled goods like glassware can never serve as the actus reus for accomplice liability, regardless of the provider's mental state.
(d) No, because Silas was not physically present during the chemical synthesis, which entirely defeats the strict requirement of "presence plus" for accomplice liability.
(e) No, because Silas only intended to assist with the distribution phase, not the manufacturing phase, legally severing his accomplice liability for the prior synthesis.

**Answer:** (b)

**Explanation:** Under the common law (as seen in *People v. Lauria*), an accomplice must have the *purpose* to promote or facilitate the crime. While providing legal goods with mere knowledge of their illicit use is usually insufficient, the provider's knowledge elevates to purpose when they acquire a "stake in the venture" (e.g., charging an inflated price or taking a cut of the profits). Because Silas took a 20% cut of the sales, he had a stake in the venture, satisfying the purpose requirement. (a) is incorrect because mere knowledge is generally insufficient. (c) is incorrect; providing legal goods *can* be the actus reus if the requisite purpose is present. (d) is incorrect; physical presence is not required if the accomplice aided beforehand. (e) is incorrect because supplying the manufacturing tools for a cut of the proceeds demonstrates a purpose to aid the manufacturing itself.
