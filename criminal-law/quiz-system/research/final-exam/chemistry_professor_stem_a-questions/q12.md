**Q12.** Silas is charged as an accomplice to Arthur's manufacture of the Schedule I substance. Silas argues he did not participate in the synthesis and only bought glassware. Under the traditional mens rea standard for accomplice liability, is Silas liable for the manufacture?

(a) Yes, because Silas provided the glassware knowing it would be used for synthesis and retained a 20% financial stake, demonstrating a purpose to facilitate the crime. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because providing any material assistance with mere knowledge that a crime will occur automatically satisfies the mens rea for accomplice liability under the common law.
(c) No, because Silas's participation was limited to logistics, and accomplice liability requires the defendant to be physically present during the commission of the actus reus.
(d) No, because a supplier of legally obtainable goods like glassware can never be held liable as an accomplice to a crime committed with those goods.
(e) No, because Silas's financial stake in the sales only establishes liability for the distribution phase, not for the upstream manufacturing process that Arthur performed alone.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because under the standard established in *Peoni* and *Lauria*, providing goods with knowledge of their illicit use combined with a financial stake in the venture (a 20% cut) demonstrates a specific purpose to facilitate the crime, satisfying accomplice mens rea. (b) fails because mere knowledge that a crime will occur is generally insufficient for accomplice liability; the defendant must have the purpose to facilitate it. (c) fails because accomplice liability does not require physical presence during the actus reus; providing essential materials beforehand is sufficient. (d) fails because suppliers of legal goods can be liable if they demonstrate a purpose to facilitate the crime, such as by charging inflated prices or taking a cut of the illicit profits. (e) fails because providing specialized glassware directly facilitates the manufacturing process, making him an accomplice to the synthesis itself, not just the later distribution.
