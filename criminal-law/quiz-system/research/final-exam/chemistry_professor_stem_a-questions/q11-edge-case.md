**Q11.** Assume Chloe is convicted of conspiracy to distribute Compound Y. The DA seeks to hold her liable for Marcus's death under the *Pinkerton* doctrine. What is required to sustain this charge?

(a) The death must have been a reasonably foreseeable consequence of the drug distribution conspiracy and committed in furtherance of it. 
(b) Chloe must have personally performed an overt act that was the direct but-for cause of Marcus's subsequent fatal overdose and death.
(c) The prosecution must prove that Chloe specifically intended for Marcus to die as a direct result of the conspiratorial agreement.
(d) Chloe must have possessed a depraved heart and extreme indifference to human life at the time the initial conspiracy was formed.
(e) The death must have occurred before Chloe committed any overt acts, ensuring it fell entirely within the scope of the agreement.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Under the Pinkerton doctrine, a co-conspirator is liable for substantive offenses committed by other members if those offenses are reasonably foreseeable and committed in furtherance of the conspiracy. (b) fails because Pinkerton liability is vicarious; the defendant does not need to personally commit any act causing the target harm. (c) fails because Pinkerton extends liability for unintended but foreseeable consequences; specific intent for the homicide is not required. (d) fails because Pinkerton uses objective foreseeability, substituting the conspiracy agreement for the mental state of the substantive offense (like depraved heart). (e) fails because Pinkerton liability applies to acts committed during the ongoing conspiracy, regardless of when the defendant's specific overt acts occurred.
