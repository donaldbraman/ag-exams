**Q10.** Prior to meeting Julian, Arthur was carrying the 500 grams of the substance in his backpack. Silas freely reached into Arthur's backpack to retrieve the two grams. Does Silas have constructive possession of the remaining 498 grams?

(a) Yes, because Silas had knowledge of the substance and the ability and intent to exercise dominion and control over it, despite Arthur's physical custody. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because in any drug conspiracy, every member automatically has actual physical possession of all narcotics held by any other member of the enterprise.
(c) No, because constructive possession requires the defendant to exercise exclusive control over the specific premises or container where the contraband is physically located.
(d) No, because Arthur was wearing the backpack, and the law does not recognize joint constructive possession of items that are physically carried by another person.
(e) No, because Silas was only legally entitled to a 20% cut of the sales, meaning his dominion and control was strictly limited to a fractional share.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because constructive possession requires knowledge of the contraband and the power and intention to exercise dominion and control. Silas demonstrated this by freely reaching into Arthur's backpack to retrieve the drugs, establishing joint constructive possession. (b) fails because conspiracy membership alone does not automatically establish physical or constructive possession without proof of dominion and control. (c) fails because constructive possession does not require exclusive control; joint possession by multiple individuals is common and legally recognized. (d) fails because dominion and control can extend to items carried by a co-conspirator if the defendant has the recognized authority and ability to access them. (e) fails because a fractional financial stake does not conceptually divide the physical possession of the contraband; Silas exercised control over the communal stash.
