**Q9.** The state seeks the death penalty against Thorne for felony murder. Applying the *Tison* standard, can Thorne be sentenced to death as an accomplice to the felony?

(a) Yes, because Thorne was a major participant in the felony who demonstrated reckless indifference to human life by verbally recognizing the danger but ultimately driving away.
(b) Yes, because Thorne was physically present at the scene of the crime, which automatically satisfies the *Tison* requirement for major participation and justifies the death penalty.
(c) No, because Thorne did not personally kill Gable, and the Eighth Amendment strictly prohibits imposing the death penalty on any non-killer accomplice regardless of their participation.
(d) No, because yelling "He's dying, call 911!" shows that Thorne did not act with the reckless indifference to human life required by *Tison*, even if a major participant. <!-- correct -->
(e) No, because Thorne was merely a street-level dealer and therefore automatically qualifies as being on the remote outer fringes of the joint venture, barring capital punishment.

**Answer:** (d)

**Explanation:** (d) is correct because the Tison standard requires the accomplice to act with reckless indifference to human life; Thorne's desperate plea to call 911 demonstrates a regard for Gable's life, negating the reckless indifference necessary for the death penalty. (a) fails because yelling for help contradicts the requirement of reckless indifference, even if he ultimately followed orders to drive away. (b) fails because mere physical presence does not automatically satisfy the "major participant" or "reckless indifference" prongs of Tison. (c) fails because the Eighth Amendment allows the death penalty for non-killers if they meet the Tison criteria. (e) fails because his role in bringing buyers and facilitating the transaction means he was more than just on the remote outer fringes.
