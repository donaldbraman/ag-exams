# Scenario: Terminal Chemistry

Dr. Arthur Pendelton, a former chemistry professor facing terminal cancer and financial ruin, agreed to synthesize a controlled substance to generate cash for his family.

Arthur partnered with his former student, Chloe Vance. They agreed to split the profits. Chloe acquired a decommissioned mobile veterinary clinic (a large van) to serve as a mobile laboratory, kept the only set of keys to the van, and arranged all buyer meetings.

Arthur manufactured the drugs and kept the bulk supply in a locked safe inside the van. Arthur was the only person who knew the combination to the safe.

The jurisdiction's Controlled Substances Act makes it a felony to manufacture or distribute Compound Y. The statute is silent regarding a mens rea requirement, but it carries a maximum penalty of 15 years in prison.

Arthur believed he had successfully synthesized a legal, unregulated analogue of Compound Y because he misread a complex chemical diagram. Chemical testing later confirmed the substance he synthesized was identically Compound Y.

On October 12, Chloe brought their first buyer, Marcus Thorne, to the van. While Chloe stood watch by the door, Arthur personally handed Marcus a small packet of the drug in exchange for $500.

Marcus immediately ingested the drug in front of them. Within two minutes, Marcus collapsed on the floor of the van, seizing and foaming at the mouth.

Arthur recognized that Marcus was suffering a fatal overdose and pulled out his cell phone to dial 911.

Chloe grabbed Arthur's arm and said, "No! If the cops come, we go to prison. Grab his legs." Together, they dragged the unconscious Marcus out of the van, left him concealed in dense brush, and drove off in the van.

Marcus died in the brush. The medical examiner concluded that Compound Y was the biological cause of death, and established that if 911 had been called when Marcus initially collapsed, emergency responders would have arrived with Naloxone in time to save his life.

### Stem 1: Arthur's Charges

We need to figure out exactly what homicide and drug charges we can make stick against Arthur Pendelton. I'm specifically concerned about how his mistake regarding the chemical synthesis interacts with the drug statute, and whether his failure to call 911 elevates this to murder.

### Stem 2: Chloe's Culpability

Now let's look at Chloe Vance. She didn't synthesize the drugs or personally hand them over, but she provided the van, stopped Pendelton from calling 911, and helped hide the body. The DA wants to charge her with possessing the bulk drugs, conspiracy, being an accomplice to the distribution, and murder.
