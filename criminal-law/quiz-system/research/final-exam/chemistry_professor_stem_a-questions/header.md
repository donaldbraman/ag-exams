## Scenario: The Motel Synthesis

### Facts

1. Arthur Pendelton, a tenured chemistry professor with terminal liver cancer, partnered with his former graduate student, Silas Vance, to raise experimental treatment money.
2. Arthur synthesized 500 grams of a chemical that is actually a Schedule I controlled substance. 
3. Arthur knew the exact molecular structure he was synthesizing, but he genuinely misread the regulatory database and believed the structure was an unscheduled, fully legal "nootropic" supplement.
4. Silas did not participate in the synthesis but purchased the specialized glassware for Arthur, knowing what Arthur would use it for, in exchange for a 20% cut of the sales.
5. Arthur and Silas met a buyer, Julian Reed, in a motel room. Silas freely reached into Arthur's unzipped backpack to take two grams of the substance and handed it to Julian, who voluntarily ingested it and immediately began convulsing.
6. Silas pulled out his cell phone and stated, "He's dying, I'm calling 911."
7. Arthur knocked the phone out of Silas's hand and stepped on it, shattering the screen. Arthur said, "If the ambulance comes, the police come. I lose my job and my treatment money. It's just a bad reaction, he will sleep it off." Arthur genuinely believed Julian would recover.
8. Arthur then told Silas, "We're leaving." Silas protested but ultimately followed Arthur out of the room.
9. Arthur locked the motel room door from the outside. The motel room did not have a landline telephone or any other method for Julian to summon assistance.
10. The medical examiner concluded that Julian died from acute cardiac arrhythmia caused by the substance. The ME established that if paramedics had been called when Julian collapsed, standard anti-arrhythmia medication would have provided a 95% chance of survival.
