# Scenario: The Desperate Professor

Arthur Pendelton, a 55-year-old tenured chemistry professor, received a terminal liver cancer diagnosis. Desperate for experimental treatment funding, he partnered with his former graduate student, Silas Vance, a 24-year-old with a low-level drug record. 

Arthur synthesized 500 grams of a chemical that is actually a Schedule I controlled substance. However, Arthur genuinely believed he was synthesizing a novel, unscheduled, fully legal designer supplement (a "nootropic"). Arthur knew the exact molecular structure he was synthesizing, but he had misread the regulatory database. 

Silas did not participate in the synthesis but purchased the specialized glassware, knowing what Arthur would use it for, in exchange for a 20% cut of the sales. Later, during a transaction in a motel room with a buyer named Julian Reed, Silas freely reached into Arthur's backpack to take out two grams. Julian voluntarily ingested the substance and immediately began convulsing. 

Silas pulled out his cell phone and stated, "He's dying, I'm calling 911." Arthur knocked the phone out of Silas's hand and stepped on it, shattering the screen. Arthur said, "If the ambulance comes, the police come. I lose my job and my treatment money. It's just a bad reaction, he will sleep it off." Arthur genuinely believed Julian would recover. Arthur then told Silas, "We're leaving." Silas protested but ultimately followed Arthur out of the room. Arthur locked the motel room door from the outside. The motel room did not have a landline telephone or any other method for Julian to summon assistance.

The medical examiner concluded that Julian died from acute cardiac arrhythmia caused by the substance. The ME established that if paramedics had been called when Julian collapsed, standard anti-arrhythmia medication would have provided a 95% chance of survival.
