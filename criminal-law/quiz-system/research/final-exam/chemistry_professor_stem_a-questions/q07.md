**Q7.** Assume the jurisdiction applies the merger doctrine (the independent felonious purpose test) to limit felony murder. How does this doctrine apply to Arthur's drug distribution?

(a) The felony does not merge because Arthur's purpose was to sell the drug for money, which is an objective independent of the violence causing death. <!-- correct -->
(b) The felony merges because distributing a lethal toxic drug is inherently an assaultive act that cannot be separated from the resulting fatal homicide.
(c) The felony does not merge because drug distribution is a strict liability offense that operates without requiring any independent and distinct mens rea.
(d) The felony merges because Arthur's act of providing the drug and Julian's act of consuming it were part of a single continuous transaction.
(e) The felony does not merge because the merger doctrine only applies when the defendant acts with the specific intent to physically kill the victim.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. The merger doctrine prevents assaultive felonies from serving as predicate offenses for felony murder because they lack an independent felonious purpose beyond the violence itself. Arthur's drug distribution does not merge because its primary objective was the commercial sale of drugs, which is entirely independent of injuring or killing Julian.
(b) is incorrect because while the drug distribution proved lethal in this instance, the crime of distribution is not legally defined as an assaultive act.
(c) is incorrect because strict liability is irrelevant to whether a felony has an independent purpose that avoids merger.
(d) is incorrect because a continuous transaction is required for felony murder generally, but does not dictate whether the underlying felony merges.
(e) is incorrect because merger applies to felonies lacking independent purpose, regardless of whether the defendant specifically intended to kill.
