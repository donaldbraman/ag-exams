**Q7.** Assume Arthur and Silas are guilty of conspiracy to distribute controlled substances. The prosecution seeks to hold Silas liable for Arthur's actions in preventing Julian's rescue (shattering the phone, locking the door) under the *Pinkerton* doctrine. Is Silas liable for these acts?

(a) Yes, because Arthur's actions to prevent detection by police were reasonably foreseeable acts done in furtherance of the ongoing conspiracy to sell illegal drugs. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because under the doctrine, a co-conspirator is strictly and automatically liable for any criminal act committed by a partner, regardless of foreseeability.
(c) No, because Silas explicitly verbally protested Arthur's actions, which immediately severed his liability for any of Arthur's subsequent criminal acts.
(d) No, because Arthur's extreme actions constituted an independent intervening cause that fell completely outside the agreed-upon scope of simply distributing the drug.
(e) No, because liability requires the prosecution to prove that the accomplice shared the specific intent for the exact substantive offense committed by the co-conspirator.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under the *Pinkerton* doctrine, a co-conspirator is liable for the substantive offenses committed by a partner if those offenses are done in furtherance of the conspiracy and are reasonably foreseeable consequences of the unlawful agreement. Preventing someone from calling the police to avoid arrest for drug distribution is highly foreseeable and in furtherance of the conspiracy. (b) is incorrect because *Pinkerton* requires foreseeability and furtherance; it is not absolute strict liability for completely unrelated acts. (c) is incorrect because a mere verbal protest does not constitute a legal withdrawal capable of severing liability. (d) is incorrect because actions taken to avoid detection are considered within the foreseeable scope of drug conspiracies. (e) describes traditional accomplice liability, not *Pinkerton* liability, which relaxes the mens rea requirement.
