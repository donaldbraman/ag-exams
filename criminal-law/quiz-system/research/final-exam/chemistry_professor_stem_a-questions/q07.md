**Q7.** The state considers felony-murder charges against Vance based on the felony of "unlicensed manufacturing of a controlled substance." If the jurisdiction applies the abstract "elements" approach to determine if a predicate felony is inherently dangerous, will this felony qualify?

(a) Yes, because manufacturing synthetic narcotics in a mobile RV laboratory inherently creates a massive risk of explosion and toxic exposure that threatens human life.
(b) Yes, because the specific facts of this case demonstrate that Vance's manufacturing process actually resulted in a highly toxic and deadly batch of synthetic drugs.
(c) No, because the elements approach requires the court to look only at the statutory definition, and unlicensed manufacturing could theoretically be committed safely without endangering human life. <!-- correct -->
(d) No, because the elements approach strictly requires the predicate felony to involve the use of a deadly weapon, which manufacturing a controlled substance does not mandate.
(e) No, because an unlicensed manufacturing charge automatically merges with any resulting homicide under the elements approach, preventing its use as a valid felony-murder predicate.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** (c) is correct because the abstract elements approach (e.g., California's Phillips rule) requires the court to look only at the statutory definition of the felony; if the crime could theoretically be committed safely (like manufacturing in a controlled, sterile lab), it is not inherently dangerous. (a) fails because it relies on the facts of how manufacturing generally occurs in RVs, rather than the pure statutory elements. (b) fails because evaluating the specific deadly batch applies the facts-as-committed approach, which the abstract approach prohibits. (d) fails because the elements approach does not strictly require a deadly weapon element, just inherent danger to life. (e) fails because merger applies to assaultive felonies, not automatically to manufacturing based on the elements approach.
