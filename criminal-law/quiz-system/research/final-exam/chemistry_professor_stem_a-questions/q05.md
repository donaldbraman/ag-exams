**Q5.** Assume that Vance owed a legal duty to Gable. Does Vance's decision to pocket the phone and flee satisfy the mental state required for depraved-heart murder?

(a) Yes, because leaving a convulsing victim to die in an isolated parking lot to protect his pension manifests an extreme indifference to the value of human life. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because Vance's failure to act constituted ordinary negligence, which is sufficient to establish depraved-heart murder when a fatal overdose actually results from the conduct.
(c) No, because depraved-heart murder requires a specific intent to kill, and Vance's primary motive was only to protect his pension and ensure his daughter received money.
(d) No, because fleeing the scene of a crime is an independent intervening act that inherently negates the conscious disregard required for a depraved-heart murder conviction.
(e) No, because the standard for depraved-heart murder strictly requires the affirmative use of a deadly weapon, which was entirely absent in this abandonment scenario.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because leaving a dying victim to convulse on the pavement merely to protect a pension demonstrates the conscious disregard and extreme indifference to the value of human life required for depraved-heart murder. (b) fails because depraved-heart murder requires far more than ordinary negligence; it requires extreme recklessness. (c) fails because depraved-heart murder involves unintentional killings where the extreme recklessness substitutes for a specific intent to kill. (d) fails because fleeing and abandoning the victim is the exact conduct that manifests the extreme indifference; it does not negate it. (e) fails because depraved-heart murder can be committed without a deadly weapon through extremely reckless conduct or omissions.
