**Q5.** Assume the prosecution charges Arthur with murder based on extreme indifference to human life under the Model Penal Code. How is this standard evaluated?

(a) A jury could find him guilty of murder because the MPC delegates to the finder of fact whether his recklessness manifested extreme indifference. <!-- correct -->
(b) The judge must direct a guilty verdict because interfering with emergency medical aid is deemed inherently dangerous as a matter of strict law.
(c) He cannot be found guilty of murder because extreme indifference requires proof that the defendant acted with the specific purpose to cause death.
(d) He cannot be found guilty of murder because the MPC requires the judge to first find that death was likely if not certain.
(e) He must be found guilty of murder because his act of locking the motel door creates an irrebuttable presumption of extreme indifference.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Under MPC § 210.2(1)(b), depraved heart murder requires recklessness "manifesting extreme indifference to the value of human life." The MPC delegates this normative determination entirely to the finder of fact. A jury must decide whether Arthur's conscious decision to block life-saving aid to protect himself meets this standard.
(b) is incorrect because the MPC explicitly commits the extreme indifference assessment to the jury, not the judge as a matter of law.
(c) is incorrect because extreme indifference murder is specifically designed for cases of extreme recklessness, not purposeful or knowing killings.
(d) is incorrect because "likely, if not certain" is the Maryland standard (*Beckwitt*), whereas the MPC uses the broader "extreme indifference" standard and gives the question to the jury.
(e) is incorrect because no such conclusive presumption exists; the jury must always evaluate the defendant's actual mental state.
