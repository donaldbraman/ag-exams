**Q3.** Assume that, whether or not Arthur's manufacturing caused the death, the prosecution charges Arthur with homicide based on his failure to seek medical help for Julian. Arthur argues he had no legal duty to rescue a buyer. Under traditional omission doctrine, which theory best establishes Arthur's legal duty to act?

(a) Status relationship, because Arthur and Julian were engaged in a joint commercial enterprise which inherently creates mutual legal duties to rescue.
(b) Statutory duty, because implied provisions in public health laws mandate that suppliers of ingestible substances monitor buyers for adverse reactions.
(c) Creation of peril, because Arthur supplied the initial dosage, thereby becoming exclusively responsible for any biological reactions the victim suffered.
(d) Creation of peril and seclusion, because Arthur supplied the drug that caused the emergency, then affirmatively isolated Julian and prevented Silas's rescue attempt. <!-- correct -->
(e) General duty, because criminal law universally requires any bystander with the physical capacity to assist to intervene when another person is dying.

**Answer:** (d)

**Explanation:** Arthur owed a legal duty to act under two established common law categories: creation of peril (he supplied the drug that caused the overdose) and voluntary assumption of care/seclusion (by shattering Silas's phone and locking the door, he secluded the victim and prevented others from rendering aid). (a) is incorrect because a commercial transaction does not create a special status relationship like parent-child or spouses. (b) is incorrect as no such implied statutory duty exists in criminal law. (c) identifies creation of peril but incorrectly states it makes him "exclusively responsible for any biological reactions" regardless of fault. (e) is incorrect because the common law imposes no general duty to rescue on bystanders.
