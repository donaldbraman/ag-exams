**Q3.** Assume the prosecution bases a homicide charge on Arthur's failure to summon an ambulance when Julian collapsed. Does Arthur have a legal duty to act under these circumstances?

(a) Arthur had a duty because his prior conduct of synthesizing and supplying the toxic drug created the peril that Julian subsequently faced. <!-- correct -->
(b) Arthur had no duty because the law imposes no general obligation to rescue a person with whom the defendant has no special status relationship.
(c) Arthur had a duty because the Good Samaritan doctrine legally requires all individuals to affirmatively intervene during any observable severe medical emergency.
(d) Arthur had no duty because Julian's voluntary act of snorting the drug legally extinguished any prior responsibility Arthur held for the situation.
(e) Arthur had a duty because he assumed a formal contractual obligation to protect Julian by selling him the stimulant for financial profit.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. While the law generally imposes no duty to act or rescue, one of the primary exceptions (the creation-of-peril category) arises when the defendant's own conduct puts the victim in danger. By synthesizing and supplying the toxic drug, Arthur created the peril and therefore owed a legal duty to summon aid when Julian collapsed.
(b) is incorrect because the creation of peril establishes a duty even in the absence of a formal status relationship.
(c) is incorrect because there is no universal Good Samaritan duty to rescue strangers in American criminal law.
(d) is incorrect because providing the deadly agent is exactly what generates the subsequent ongoing duty to mitigate the harm.
(e) is incorrect because a standard illegal drug sale does not inherently create a formal contractual duty of care.
