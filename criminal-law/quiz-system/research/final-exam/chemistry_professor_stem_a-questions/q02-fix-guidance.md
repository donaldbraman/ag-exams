**Q2.** Assume the court determines the statute requires the prosecution to prove Arthur "knowingly" manufactured and distributed Compound Y. Which of the following describes the legal effect of Arthur's mistaken belief regarding the chemical diagram?

(a) It provides a valid mistake of fact defense because his honest belief that he synthesized a different substance negates the required mental state of knowledge. <!-- correct -->
(b) It provides no defense because mistake of fact is only available if the underlying conduct would have been entirely lawful even if the facts were as believed.
(c) It provides a valid mistake of law defense because his misreading of the complex diagram was a reasonable interpretation of the regulatory definitions.
(d) It provides no defense because his belief concerns the legal status of the chemical, and ignorance of the law is generally not a valid excuse.
(e) It provides a valid mistake of fact defense because he lacked the specific intent to violate the jurisdiction's Controlled Substances Act.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Arthur's mistake is about the factual identity of the chemical he synthesized (believing it was an unregulated analogue rather than Compound Y). This mistake of fact negates the attendant circumstance element requiring him to "knowingly" manufacture Compound Y. (b) is wrong because the legal wrongfulness test generally applies to strict liability or ambiguous common-law crimes, not where a specific mens rea must be negated. (c) is wrong because this is a mistake about the chemical's factual identity, not an official statement of law. (d) is wrong because the mistake is factual (what chemical is this?) rather than legal (is Compound Y illegal?). (e) is wrong because the defense negates the knowledge element of the offense, not a specific intent to break the law.
