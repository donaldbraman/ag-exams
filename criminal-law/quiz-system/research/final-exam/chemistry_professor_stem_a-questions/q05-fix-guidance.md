## Issue 2 — argpass-sonnet

**Q5.** Assume Arthur is charged with second-degree depraved heart murder. Which of the following facts best supports a finding of the mental state required for depraved heart murder?

(a) Arthur's initial decision to synthesize Compound Y, because manufacturing drugs inherently risks human life.
(b) Arthur's act of distributing the drug to Marcus, because selling untested chemicals constitutes gross negligence.
(c) Arthur's failure to call 911 when Marcus initially collapsed, because ordinary negligence can support a murder conviction.
(d) Arthur's act of dragging Marcus into the brush and driving away, because it manifested an extreme indifference to human life. <!-- correct -->
(e) Arthur's recognition that Marcus was overdosing, because actual awareness of a risk is sufficient for depraved heart murder.

**Answer:** (d)

**Explanation:** Depraved heart murder requires recklessness manifesting an extreme indifference to the value of human life. Actively dragging a dying man into dense brush, ensuring he will not be discovered or treated, goes beyond mere recklessness and demonstrates extreme indifference to his survival. (a) and (b) describe conduct that might be reckless or grossly negligent, but typically do not rise to the level of depraved heart murder on their own. (c) is wrong because ordinary negligence cannot support a murder conviction; depraved heart requires extreme recklessness. (e) is wrong because mere awareness of a risk establishes standard recklessness (manslaughter), not the heightened extreme indifference required for murder.
