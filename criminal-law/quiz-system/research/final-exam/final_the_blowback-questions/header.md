# Scenario: The Syndicate

### Facts

1. At a sit-down, Carmine and his crew (Benny, Dom, and Leo) explicitly agreed to hijack a rival cartel's incoming pharmaceutical fentanyl shipment, sketching the ambush route on a napkin. Weapons were not explicitly discussed.
2. Later that week, Carmine and Paulie went out on Carmine's boat. Carmine routinely kept a loaded revolver on the boat's console to shoot aggressive sharks; investigation established he did not plan to use it on Paulie when they boarded.
3. While on the water, Paulie admitted to talking to the feds and yelled a vicious, graphically detailed insult regarding Carmine's wife.
4. Less than ten seconds after the insult, Carmine grabbed the revolver from the console and shot Paulie once in the chest, killing him instantly.
5. Later that evening, to cover up the syndicate's separate illicit fentanyl manufacturing operation, Carmine ordered Benny to discreetly dispose of fifty barrels of highly toxic chemical solvent.
6. Seeking to save time, Benny drove the barrels to a shallow drainage ditch adjacent to a popular suburban jogging trail instead of using the designated remote industrial site.
7. While dumping the barrels, Benny felt the fumes burning his lungs. A wiretap recorded him muttering to himself, "This stuff could kill a stray dog, but I'm sure the rain will wash it away before any human comes near it."
8. Victor, a local jogger, inhaled the lingering fumes later that afternoon and died from respiratory failure.
9. To obtain an untraceable getaway car for the planned fentanyl hijacking, Dom went to Frankie, a local mechanic, and paid triple the usual rental rate. Frankie knew the car was for a hijacking but told Dom, "I don't care what you do, as long as the money is green."
10. The following morning, Dom arrived at the hijack scene. He lay in wait in an alley next to the target truck and independently escalated the plan by aiming a shotgun directly at the target driver's side door as the driver approached.
11. Before the target driver could hand over the keys, a marked police cruiser turned the corner and activated its sirens for an unrelated traffic stop.
12. Seeing the cruiser, Dom dropped the shotgun, yelled, "I give up, the hit is off!" and tossed his backpack into the idling getaway car before fleeing on foot down an alley, leaving his co-conspirator Leo in the vehicle alone.
13. Leo sped away in the car alone, initiating a high-speed chase. During the chaotic flight, a pursuing police officer fired at the getaway car but missed, striking and killing Wendy, a pedestrian.
14. After Leo crashed into a barricade, police approached the vehicle. They found Dom's backpack ripped open, with baggies of fentanyl spilling out in plain view on the passenger seat next to Leo. Leo claimed he had no idea the drugs were there.

### Stem 1: The Execution and the Environment

The DA's Organized Crime Division just dropped a massive file on our desks. We've got a syndicate boss executing an informant on a boat, a subordinate dumping lethal chemicals, and evidence of a broader criminal enterprise. I need a comprehensive breakdown of the initial charges. Evaluate Carmine's liability for Paulie's death, Benny's exposure for the environmental casualty, and whether the syndicate's structure supports overarching RICO and conspiracy charges.

### Stem 2: The Hijacking and the Flight

The pharmaceutical fentanyl hijacking went completely sideways and left a pedestrian dead in a police shootout. Dom is claiming he legally abandoned the robbery before anyone got hurt, and Leo says he had no idea there would be a gun or that drugs were in the car. Analyze Frankie's liability for supplying the car, evaluate Dom's attempt actus reus and abandonment defense, break down the felony murder exposure for the whole crew, assess Carmine's Pinkerton liability, and address Leo's drug possession charge.
