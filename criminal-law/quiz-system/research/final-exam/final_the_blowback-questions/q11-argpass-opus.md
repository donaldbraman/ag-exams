**Q11.** Assume Leo is charged with felony murder for Wendy's death in California, which operates under the SB 1437 reforms. What is the most likely outcome?

(a) Guilty of felony murder, because his role as the designated getaway driver categorically satisfies the statutory definition of a major participant regardless of his subjective knowledge.
(b) Not guilty of felony murder, because under SB 1437, an accomplice who is completely unaware that a co-felon is armed cannot possess the requisite reckless indifference to human life. <!-- correct -->
(c) Guilty of felony murder, because SB 1437 strictly holds all accomplices liable if a police officer's foreseeable response during the flight from a felony causes a fatal injury.
(d) Not guilty of felony murder, because the abstract elements approach dictates that the crime of operating a getaway vehicle is not inherently dangerous to human life.
(e) Guilty of felony murder, because his act of fleeing the scene proves a corrupt motive, which structurally substitutes for the requirement of reckless indifference to human life.

**Answer:** (b)

**Explanation:** (b) is correct because California's SB 1437 requires that a non-killer accomplice be a "major participant" who acted with "reckless indifference to human life." *People v. Emanuel* clarified that an accomplice who does not know a co-felon is armed cannot demonstrate the necessary reckless indifference to support a murder conviction. (a) is wrong because status as a getaway driver is not a categorical proxy; the statute demands an individualized assessment of the accomplice's subjective mental state regarding the risk of death. (c) is wrong because SB 1437 specifically abolished the broad foreseeability theories that previously captured peripheral accomplices. (d) is wrong because the abstract elements test applies to determining whether the predicate felony itself is inherently dangerous, not whether the accomplice exhibited reckless indifference. (e) is wrong because a general corrupt motive or desire to flee does not substitute for the statutorily required extreme mental state of reckless indifference to life.
