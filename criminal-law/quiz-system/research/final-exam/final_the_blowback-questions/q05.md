**Q5.** Did Carmine and his crew commit the offense of conspiracy when they met to plan the hijacking?

(a) Yes, because sketching the ambush route on the napkin satisfies the overt act requirement for a conspiratorial agreement. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because the bilateral agreement requirement is satisfied simply by their attendance at the sit-down meeting.
(c) No, because weapons were not explicitly discussed, demonstrating the agreement lacked the necessary specificity for the target offense.
(d) No, because the hijacking never successfully occurred, rendering the conspiracy incomplete.
(e) No, because the conspiracy doctrine merges with the substantive offense of attempting to commit a crime.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Conspiracy requires an intent to agree, an intent to commit the target offense, and (in most jurisdictions) an overt act in furtherance of the conspiracy. The explicit agreement at the sit-down satisfies the mental state and agreement elements, and sketching the route on the napkin easily qualifies as an overt act. (b) is wrong because mere attendance is insufficient; an actual agreement to commit the offense is required. (c) is wrong because an agreement need not encompass every detail or the use of weapons to constitute a conspiracy to hijack. (d) is wrong because conspiracy is an inchoate offense that is complete upon the agreement and overt act, regardless of whether the target crime succeeds. (e) is wrong because conspiracy does not merge with attempt or the completed offense.
