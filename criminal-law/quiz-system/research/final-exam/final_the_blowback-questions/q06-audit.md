**Q6.** Assume the prosecution seeks to indict Carmine under the federal RICO statute. Based on the syndicate's overall structure and activities, how will a court likely rule on the "enterprise" and "pattern of racketeering" elements?

(a) Guilty under RICO, because the syndicate possesses a shared purpose, relationships among its associates, and sufficient longevity to pursue its criminal objectives. <!-- correct -->
(b) Not guilty under RICO, because the hijacking agreement and the chemical dumping were entirely separate activities that lacked the required relatedness for a pattern of racketeering.
(c) Guilty under RICO, because the Hobbs Act presumes that any agreement involving bulk cash or pharmaceutical drugs substantially affects interstate commerce.
(d) Not guilty under RICO, because the syndicate lacks the formal corporate charter or legal structure necessary to qualify as an enterprise under the federal statute.
(e) Guilty under RICO, because any two criminal offenses committed by the same defendant within a ten-year period automatically satisfy the pattern of racketeering activity element.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because it directly applies the Supreme Court's three-part test from *Boyle v. United States* for an association-in-fact enterprise: a purpose, relationships among associates, and longevity sufficient to permit pursuit of the purpose. (b) is wrong because the activities (manufacturing drugs, hijacking rival drugs) share the common purpose of enriching the syndicate, satisfying the relatedness requirement for a pattern. (c) is wrong because while the Hobbs Act regulates interstate commerce, it does not define the RICO enterprise or pattern elements being tested here. (d) is wrong because RICO explicitly covers informal "associations in fact" and does not require any formal legal or corporate structure. (e) is wrong because the Supreme Court requires that predicate acts show both "relatedness" and "continuity"; merely committing any two unrelated offenses within ten years is insufficient.
