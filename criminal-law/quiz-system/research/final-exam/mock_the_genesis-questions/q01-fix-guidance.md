**Q1.** Assume that Albert and Benny are charged with conspiracy to manufacture and distribute Blue-X. Which of the following is the most accurate legal conclusion regarding their arrangement?

(a) Guilty of conspiracy because their verbal plan to synthesize and distribute the drugs constitutes a bilateral agreement to commit the target offense. <!-- correct -->
(b) Not guilty of conspiracy because Benny merely agreed to distribute the final product and did not participate in the actual chemical synthesis.
(c) Not guilty of conspiracy because their arrangement was an informal verbal understanding rather than a formalized and documented criminal contract.
(d) Guilty of conspiracy because Albert's specific intent to manufacture the illicit narcotics is automatically imputed to Benny through their association.
(e) Not guilty of conspiracy because Albert had not yet completed the manufacturing process before the laboratory was destroyed by the explosion.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because Albert and Benny's verbal arrangement to synthesize and distribute the drugs fully satisfies the core requirement of conspiracy: a mutual agreement to commit a crime. (b) is wrong because a co-conspirator need not participate in every element of the target offense; dividing labor is the essence of conspiracy. (c) is wrong because agreements need not be written or formal to trigger conspiracy liability. (d) is wrong because conspiracy requires proof of an actual agreement, not mere association or imputed intent. (e) is wrong because the crime of conspiracy is complete upon the agreement (and an overt act, depending on the jurisdiction), regardless of whether the target offense succeeds.
