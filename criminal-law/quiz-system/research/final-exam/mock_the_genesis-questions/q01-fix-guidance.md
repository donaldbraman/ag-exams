**Q1.** Benny claims he cannot be convicted of conspiracy because he and Albert never explicitly agreed to manufacture illicit drugs; they only spoke of a "lucrative new chemistry project." Which of the following is the most accurate assessment of Benny's defense?

(a) The defense will fail because a jury may infer an agreement to manufacture illicit drugs from Benny's nod, acceptance of the keys, and delivery of cash advances. <!-- correct -->
(b) The defense will fail because conspiracy is a strict liability offense regarding the object of the agreement, requiring only that the parties agreed to act together.
(c) The defense will succeed because an agreement to commit a specific crime requires express verbal or written communication to satisfy the actus reus of conspiracy under the common law.
(d) The defense will succeed because Benny's actions constituted mere preparation and did not amount to a substantial step toward the commission of the target crime, defeating liability.
(e) The defense will succeed unless the prosecution can prove Benny knew the specific chemical composition of the substance Albert intended to manufacture, as required for the specific intent of the conspiracy.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Benny's non-verbal actions (nodding, taking keys, delivering cash) in response to Albert's request are sufficient for a jury to infer a mutual agreement to commit the crime (the "lucrative new chemistry project" understood as drug manufacturing). (b) is incorrect because conspiracy requires specific intent to commit the target crime, not strict liability. (c) is incorrect because an agreement can be inferred from a concert of action; express words are not required. (d) is incorrect because "substantial step" is the actus reus standard for attempt, not the agreement standard for conspiracy. (e) is incorrect because conspirators need not know every technical detail of the target offense, only its general illegal nature.
