**Q11.** Albert is charged with a homicide offense for Chloe's death. He argues that the hospital's severe charting and dosing error was an intervening cause that relieves him of liability. Which of the following is the most accurate causation analysis?

(a) The medical error is an independent intervening cause that severs the causal chain because it was the immediate factual but-for cause of her sudden fatal hemorrhage.
(b) The medical error is a dependent intervening cause that generally does not sever the causal chain unless the medical treatment was intentionally harmful or highly extraordinary. <!-- correct -->
(c) Albert is not the proximate cause because the specific manner of Chloe's death—a blood thinner overdose—was not reasonably foreseeable when the chemical explosion initially occurred.
(d) Albert is not the factual cause because the explosion only caused a neck laceration, which is rarely fatal on its own with prompt and proper medical intervention.
(e) The hospital staff's actions constitute a superseding cause that entirely relieves Albert of liability because routine charting errors are strictly prohibited by internal hospital regulations.

**Answer:** (b)

**Explanation:** Medical negligence during the treatment of a victim's injuries is considered a dependent intervening cause that is reasonably foreseeable. It does not sever the causal chain unless the treatment is highly extraordinary or intentionally harmful, meaning Albert remains the legal cause of Chloe's death. (a) is incorrect because ordinary medical negligence is a dependent, not independent, intervening cause. (c) is incorrect because the specific manner of death (blood thinner overdose) need not be foreseeable, so long as the general type of harm (medical complication from the initial injury) is foreseeable. (d) is incorrect because Albert's explosion was a necessary "but-for" factual cause of her hospitalization and subsequent death. (e) is incorrect because routine charting errors, even if prohibited by hospital policy, are foreseeable dependent causes, not superseding causes.
