**Q9.** Assume the court finds Albert performed a voluntary act. The prosecution charges Albert with attempted drug manufacturing under a traditional common law proximity test (such as "dangerous proximity"). Which of the following is Albert's best argument that his conduct falls short of an attempt?

(a) He had not yet begun the actual chemical synthesis and had merely placed sealed containers at his workstation, constituting mere preparation rather than a proximate act. <!-- correct -->
(b) He experienced a sudden spasm that physically prevented him from completing the manufacturing process, establishing the complete defense of factual impossibility under the traditional common law approach.
(c) He lacked the specific intent to manufacture the illicit drugs because his primary and overriding motivation was paying off his mounting medical debts, which negates the required mens rea.
(d) He voluntarily abandoned the crime when he sat down to review his notes instead of immediately opening the containers and mixing the chemical precursors, satisfying the abandonment defense.
(e) He did not have Benny present to assist with the synthesis, making it legally impossible to complete the conspiracy or the underlying target offense under the bilateral agreement requirement.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under traditional common law proximity tests, the defendant's actions must come dangerously close to completing the crime. Merely placing sealed containers on a desk and reviewing notes typically constitutes mere preparation, not a proximate act. (b) is incorrect because factual impossibility (such as a spasm preventing completion) is not a defense to attempt. (c) is incorrect because Albert had the specific intent to manufacture the drugs; his financial motive explains *why* he had that intent but does not negate it. (d) is incorrect because pausing to read notes is not a complete and voluntary renunciation of the criminal purpose required for abandonment. (e) is incorrect because a defendant can commit an attempt acting alone; the physical presence of a co-conspirator is not legally required.
