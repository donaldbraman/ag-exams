## Issue 1 — audit

**Q12.** Assume Benny is prosecuted for possessing the Blue-X located inside the stash house safe. Can the prosecution successfully establish that Benny constructively possessed the drugs?

(a) Benny is in constructive possession because he knew the drugs were located in the safe and his possession of the combination gave him the ability to exercise control over them. <!-- correct -->
(b) Benny is in constructive possession because the illicit drugs were originally manufactured pursuant to his earlier criminal conspiracy with Albert.
(c) Benny is not in constructive possession because Kev was the specific individual who physically handled and placed the narcotics into the floor safe.
(d) Benny is not in constructive possession because he did not possess a formal ownership or leasehold interest in the stash house property where the safe was located.
(e) Benny is not in constructive possession because the heavy safe was securely locked, preventing immediate physical access without Kev's explicit permission.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Constructive possession requires knowledge of the contraband's presence and the ability to exercise dominion and control over it. By holding the safe's combination, Benny had independent access and control over the drugs. (b) is wrong because prior manufacture does not inherently prove a current ability to control the specific items. (c) is wrong because a defendant does not need to physically place the items to constructively possess them. (d) is wrong because property ownership is not required if the defendant has targeted control over the specific restricted space (the safe). (e) is wrong because Benny had the combination, overcoming the locked barrier and establishing his immediate control.
