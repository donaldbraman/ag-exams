**Q13.** Assume Alba and Bex are charged with felony murder based on Cody's death, using the initial drug distribution as the predicate felony. Which of the following is the most significant doctrinal hurdle to this charge?

(a) Whether the jurisdiction considers the distribution of fentanyl to be an inherently dangerous felony capable of supporting a felony murder charge. <!-- correct -->
(b) Whether the drug distribution merged with the homicide because it was committed with an independent felonious purpose.
(c) Whether Cody's voluntary consumption of the drug acts as a superseding intervening cause.
(d) Whether Bex's duress claim regarding standing guard retroactively excuses her from the earlier drug distribution.
(e) Whether the 50 grams of fentanyl meets the federal statutory weight requirement for a capital offense.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because felony murder requires the predicate offense to be an enumerated or inherently dangerous felony. Jurisdictions differ on whether drug distribution meets the "inherently dangerous in the abstract" test required to trigger the felony murder rule. (b) is wrong because the merger doctrine limits felony murder when the predicate offense is an integral part of the homicide (like assault); drug distribution typically has an independent felonious purpose and does not merge. (c) is wrong because courts consistently hold that a victim's voluntary ingestion does not sever proximate causation in drug-induced felony murder cases. (d) is wrong because duress cannot retroactively excuse a crime committed before the threat occurred. (e) is wrong because felony murder relies on the dangerousness of the predicate, not federal weight thresholds.
