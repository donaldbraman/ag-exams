**Q6.** Assume Alba is charged with the attempted murder of Dane. Is Alba guilty of this charge?

(a) No, because Alba intended only to knock Dane out, and attempted murder requires the specific intent to kill. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because striking someone in the head with a handgun is inherently dangerous and establishes the required mental state.
(c) Yes, because Alba's underlying motive to facilitate Cody's death transfers to the assault on Dane.
(d) No, because Dane's entry into the apartment was an uninvited trespass, legally justifying the use of lethal force.
(e) Yes, because attempted murder only requires a conscious disregard of a substantial risk of death, which Alba exhibited.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because criminal attempt requires the specific intent (purpose) to commit the target offense. Because Alba specifically intended only to incapacitate Dane, the mens rea for attempted murder is lacking. (b) is wrong because while the act is dangerous and could support depraved-heart murder if Dane died, attempt requires purpose, not just recklessness or implied malice. (c) is wrong because transferred intent applies when the actor intends to kill one person and kills another; Alba did not intend to kill Dane. (d) is wrong because lethal force is not justified against a medical rescue, and the question specifically tests Alba's mens rea. (e) is wrong because recklessness is insufficient for attempt liability, even for result crimes like murder.
