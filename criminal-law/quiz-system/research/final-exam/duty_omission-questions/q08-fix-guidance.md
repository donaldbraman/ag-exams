**Q8.** Assume that prosecutors charge Alba with homicide for Cody's death. Which of the following best establishes Alba's factual causation regarding the thwarted rescue?

(a) Factual causation is established because, but for Alba incapacitating Dane, the Narcan would have reversed the overdose and Cody would have survived. <!-- correct -->
(b) Factual causation is established because Alba originally supplied the fentanyl and directed the sales operation from within Bex's apartment.
(c) Factual causation is established because Alba explicitly stated his intention that Cody should die before dragging him into the windowless bathroom.
(d) Factual causation fails because Cody's voluntary injection of the fentanyl was an independent intervening act that severed the required causal chain.
(e) Factual causation fails because the medical examiner definitively ruled that Cody died from respiratory failure induced by the drug, not from physical trauma.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Factual causation applies the "but-for" test. But for Alba forcefully incapacitating Dane, the paramedic would have administered the Narcan, reversed the overdose, and Cody would not have died. (b) is wrong because supplying drugs is too remote; the thwarted rescue is the direct factual cause of the ultimate death. (c) is wrong because Alba's malicious statements establish mens rea, not the physical but-for factual causation. (d) is wrong because Cody's injection set the stage, but Alba's subsequent incapacitation of the rescuer was a necessary but-for cause of the failure to survive. (e) is wrong because the medical cause of death (fentanyl) does not negate Alba's factual role in preventing the life-saving medical intervention.
