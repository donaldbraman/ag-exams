**Q9.** Assume that, whether or not factual causation is established, Alba argues he is not the proximate cause of Cody's death because the fentanyl killed him. Which of the following best analyzes proximate cause?

(a) Proximate cause is established because it is reasonably foreseeable that actively incapacitating a responding paramedic will result in the overdosing victim's death. <!-- correct -->
(b) Proximate cause is established because strict liability automatically applies to any fatality that occurs during the commission of a felony drug distribution.
(c) Proximate cause fails because Dane's forceful, destructive entry into the apartment constituted an independent intervening act that broke the causal chain.
(d) Proximate cause fails because the medical examiner concluded the direct mechanism of death was the fentanyl itself rather than blunt force trauma.
(e) Proximate cause fails because Alba's verbal instruction to "Let him die" transformed an accidental overdose into an unforeseeable intentional homicide.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Proximate cause asks whether the resulting harm was a reasonably foreseeable consequence of the defendant's conduct. Thwarting a medical rescue predictably leads to the overdosing victim's death, establishing Alba as the proximate cause. (b) is wrong because strict liability does not apply to proximate cause analysis. (c) is wrong because Dane's entry was a dependent intervening act aimed at rescue, not a superseding cause that breaks the chain. (d) is wrong because proximate cause assigns legal responsibility for the sequence of events, regardless of the physiological mechanism of death. (e) is wrong because verbal instructions go to mens rea, while proximate cause relies on the objective foreseeability of the acts.
