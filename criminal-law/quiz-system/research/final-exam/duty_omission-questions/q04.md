**Q4.** Assume Alba is charged with omission-based homicide. Did Alba's act of dragging Cody into the windowless bathroom and shutting the door create a legal duty to aid?

(a) Yes, because this conduct constituted a voluntary assumption of care combined with secluding the victim from other sources of aid. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because Alba explicitly stated "Let him die," demonstrating an intent to harm rather than a voluntary assumption of care.
(c) Yes, because moving a body legally converts an omission offense into a strict liability conduct offense.
(d) No, because Cody was already unconscious and therefore unaware of Alba's actions.
(e) Yes, because shutting a door satisfies the common law requirement for a contractual duty to rescue.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because under the Jones categories, a duty to act arises when a defendant undertakes to act and thereby secludes the victim, preventing others from rendering aid. Dragging Cody into a closed bathroom isolated him from potential rescue. (b) is wrong because the legal category focuses on the objective act of taking charge and secluding, regardless of the defendant's malicious subjective motivation. (c) is wrong because moving someone does not create strict liability; it merely establishes the duty element of the omission framework. (d) is wrong because the victim's awareness is not an element of the voluntary assumption and seclusion duty. (e) is wrong because these facts demonstrate assumption and seclusion, not a contractual agreement.
