**Q12.** Assume the jurisdiction recognizes common law duress. If Bex is charged with the intentional murder of Cody based on standing guard, can she successfully assert a duress defense?

(a) No, because under the common law, duress is categorically unavailable as a defense to intentional murder. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because Alba's threat to "blow your brains out" constituted a threat of imminent death or serious bodily injury.
(c) No, because Bex's initial participation in the drug conspiracy means she was at fault for creating the coercive situation.
(d) Yes, because Bex acted solely to protect her own life, which legally justifies sacrificing Cody's life.
(e) No, because Bex had a reasonable legal alternative to overpower Alba and administer aid to Cody.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because the traditional common law rule categorically bars duress as a defense to intentional murder. A defendant may not choose to sacrifice an innocent life to save their own. (b) is wrong because although the threat meets the imminence and severity thresholds for duress, the categorical murder bar precludes the defense. (c) is wrong because while the "self-created situation" rule limits duress, the categorical murder bar is the primary and absolute obstacle here. (d) is wrong because this describes necessity, and the law does not permit necessity or duress to justify taking an innocent life. (e) is wrong because overpowering an armed assailant pointing a gun at one's head is not a "reasonable" legal alternative.
