**Q12.** Assume Dana is charged with conspiracy to manufacture opioids. Which fact provides the strongest basis for inferring that she actually agreed to participate in the conspiracy, rather than merely acting as a neutral vendor?

(a) She charged a 20% premium based on her knowledge of the illicit purpose, demonstrating a financial stake in the venture's success. <!-- correct -->
(b) She legally sold dual-use chemicals, which automatically establishes an agreement to participate in any subsequent illegal use by the buyer.
(c) She broke into the veterinary clinic to save Chris, proving she had a pre-existing explicit contract to manufacture the synthetic opioids.
(d) She frequently visited the rural cabin, showing physical proximity to the manufacturing process and an implicit agreement to distribute narcotics.
(e) She possessed advanced knowledge of chemistry, creating a strict legal presumption that she actively directed the entire illicit manufacturing operation.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Under *Lauria*, a supplier of lawful goods can be inferred to have agreed to a conspiracy if they demonstrate a "stake in the venture," such as charging an inflated premium based on the illicit use. (b) is wrong because neutral sales of dual-use goods without a stake in the venture do not establish an agreement. (c) is wrong because the burglary occurred after the agreement and does not prove the nature of the initial contract. (d) is wrong because mere physical proximity to a crime scene is insufficient to prove a conspiratorial agreement. (e) is wrong because advanced knowledge of a subject does not create legal presumptions of directing a criminal enterprise.
