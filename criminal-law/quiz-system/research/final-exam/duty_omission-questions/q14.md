**Q14.** Assume Alba claims self-defense and defense of habitation for striking Dane as Dane breached the door. Will these defenses succeed?

(a) No, because Alba knew Dane was attempting a medical rescue, meaning Alba could not reasonably believe Dane was an unlawful aggressor. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because Dane's forceful destruction of the locked door established a presumption of a deadly threat.
(c) No, because Alba was engaged in illegal drug distribution, which completely forfeits all self-defense claims in every jurisdiction.
(d) Yes, because defense of habitation permits the use of force to repel any uninvited entry, regardless of the entrant's intent.
(e) No, because Alba used a firearm, and defense of habitation only permits empty-handed force.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because both self-defense and defense of habitation require the defendant to reasonably believe that the intruder poses an unlawful threat. Because Alba knew Dane was intervening to rescue Cody, Alba could not reasonably perceive Dane as an unlawful aggressor. (b) is wrong because while forced entry can create a presumption of a threat in some castle doctrine states, Alba's actual knowledge of Dane's rescue intent defeats the subjective and objective reasonableness of any perceived threat. (c) is wrong because while some states limit "stand your ground" rights for those engaged in illegal activity, the foundational lack of a reasonable belief in unlawful aggression is the primary reason the defense fails. (d) is wrong because defense of habitation does not justify force against someone legally privileged to enter, such as a neighbor acting under the necessity of a medical emergency. (e) is wrong because defense of habitation can permit deadly force under certain circumstances, but those circumstances are not met here.
