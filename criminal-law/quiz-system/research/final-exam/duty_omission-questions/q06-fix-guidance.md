**Q6.** Did Alba and Bex owe a legal duty to act to save Cody's life?

(a) Yes, because they created the peril by providing the fentanyl, and Alba further assumed a duty by secluding Cody from other aid. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because criminal law imposes a universal Good Samaritan duty on all bystanders who witness a life-threatening medical emergency.
(c) No, because Cody's decision to voluntarily inject the drugs legally absolved them of any prior duty related to the distribution.
(d) No, because criminal liability for an omission requires a formal status relationship or a written contractual duty to provide care.
(e) No, because Alba's subsequent assault on Dane definitively severed any legal duty that Alba or Bex might have owed to Cody.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because providing the fentanyl satisfied the creation-of-peril duty for both defendants, and Alba additionally satisfied the voluntary assumption of care category by taking charge of Cody and secluding him in the windowless bathroom. (b) is wrong because Anglo-American law generally imposes no universal duty to rescue a stranger. (c) is wrong because the voluntary ingestion of the drug does not negate the duty to mitigate harm arising from having created the peril. (d) is wrong because duties can arise from creating peril or assuming care, not just formal status or contract. (e) is wrong because committing a subsequent crime to prevent rescue exacerbates rather than severs an existing legal duty.
