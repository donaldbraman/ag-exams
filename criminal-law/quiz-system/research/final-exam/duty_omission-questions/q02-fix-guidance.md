**Q2.** Assume that Cody survived the overdose and prosecutors charge him with the Alba-Bex drug distribution conspiracy. Is Cody guilty of conspiracy?

(a) Guilty of conspiracy because he purchased drugs from a known distribution operation, showing intent to participate.
(b) Guilty of conspiracy because his presence in Bex's apartment established a tacit agreement to the overall criminal enterprise.
(c) Not guilty of conspiracy because his voluntary ingestion of the drugs served as a complete withdrawal from any prior agreement.
(d) Not guilty of conspiracy because a mere transaction between a buyer and seller does not establish an agreement to distribute. <!-- correct -->
(e) Not guilty of conspiracy because the distribution operation was physically controlled solely by Alba and Bex.

**Answer:** (d)

**Explanation:** (d) is correct because under the buyer-seller exception, a simple exchange of drugs for money, without a broader agreement to advance a distribution enterprise, does not make the buyer a co-conspirator. (a) is wrong because buying drugs does not inherently show the required intent to join the sellers' distribution conspiracy. (b) is wrong because mere presence and a singular purchase do not prove tacit agreement to the distribution enterprise. (c) is wrong because withdrawal requires a voluntary, affirmative act to defeat the conspiracy, not merely consuming the purchased product. (e) is wrong because physical control of the operation is not required to be a conspirator, though Cody fails on the agreement element regardless.
