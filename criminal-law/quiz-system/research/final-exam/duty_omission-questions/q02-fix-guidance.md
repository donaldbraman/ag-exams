**Q2.** Assume that, whether or not the possession charge succeeds, Alba and Bex are charged with conspiracy to distribute fentanyl based on their initial arrangement. Which of the following best analyzes this charge?

(a) Guilty of conspiracy because their verbal agreement to use the apartment as a stash house satisfies the requirement of an agreement to commit an unlawful act. <!-- correct -->
(b) Not guilty of conspiracy because Alba and Bex did not formalize their arrangement in writing or explicitly define their specific profit shares.
(c) Not guilty of conspiracy because merely allowing an apartment to be used for a crime constitutes facilitation rather than an affirmative criminal agreement.
(d) Guilty of conspiracy because Bex's presence during Cody's subsequent drug purchase constitutes an overt act that retroactively creates the agreement.
(e) Guilty of conspiracy because strict liability applies to any joint venture involving the distribution of Schedule I narcotics.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Alba and Bex are guilty of conspiracy because their verbal agreement to use the apartment for selling drugs establishes an agreement to commit an unlawful act. (b) is wrong because conspiracy does not require a formal or written contract. (c) is wrong because actively agreeing to partner in a stash house is an affirmative agreement, not mere facilitation. (d) is wrong because the agreement itself completes the core of the conspiracy; overt acts merely further it and do not retroactively create the agreement. (e) is wrong because conspiracy requires specific intent to agree and to commit the offense, not strict liability.
