## Issue 1 — audit

**Q10.** Assume that prosecutors charge Bex as an accomplice to Alba's homicide of Cody, based on Bex standing guard at the door. Which of the following best analyzes Bex's liability?

(a) Not guilty as an accomplice because Bex acted solely out of fear for her life and therefore lacked the required purpose to promote or facilitate Cody's death. <!-- correct -->
(b) Not guilty as an accomplice because merely looking through a peephole does not constitute a sufficient affirmative act to satisfy the actus reus requirement.
(c) Guilty as an accomplice because her presence at the front door objectively assisted Alba in completing the seclusion of the overdosing victim.
(d) Guilty as an accomplice because she knew that standing guard would help Alba ensure Cody's death, which satisfies the mens rea for accomplice liability.
(e) Guilty as an accomplice because she originally handed the loaded syringe to Cody, making her strictly liable for all subsequent events that caused his death.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Accomplice liability requires the purpose to promote or facilitate the commission of the underlying offense. Because Bex stood guard solely out of fear for her life and without the desire for Cody to die, she lacked the required purposeful mens rea. (b) is wrong because standing guard and watching a door is a classic affirmative act (actus reus) of aiding. (c) is wrong because objective assistance satisfies the actus reus, but the charge still fails on the mens rea requirement. (d) is wrong because mere knowledge that her actions would help Alba is insufficient; accomplice liability for homicide requires purpose. (e) is wrong because handing over the syringe relates to the drug distribution, not strict liability for the subsequent intentional homicide.
