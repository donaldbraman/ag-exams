**Q10.** Assume Bex is charged as an accomplice to Alba's omission-based homicide. Based on her conduct at the door, did Bex possess the necessary mens rea for accomplice liability under the common law?

(a) No, because Bex acted solely out of fear for her life and lacked the purpose to facilitate Cody's death. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because Bex knew that standing guard would assist Alba in preventing Cody's rescue.
(c) No, because mere presence at the scene of a crime is insufficient to establish accomplice liability.
(d) Yes, because Bex's prior agreement to sell drugs automatically supplies the mens rea for any subsequent acts.
(e) Yes, because standing guard is an affirmative act that inherently proves a desire for the victim to die.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because common law accomplice liability requires that the defendant act with the specific purpose of promoting or facilitating the underlying offense. Fact 6 stipulates Bex acted without any desire for Cody to die, negating the required purpose. (b) is wrong because knowledge that one's actions will aid the principal is generally insufficient for accomplice liability without the purpose to facilitate the crime. (c) is wrong because Bex was not "merely present"; she actively stood guard, satisfying the actus reus, but lacked the mens rea. (d) is wrong because the mens rea for conspiracy does not automatically transfer to become the specific purpose required for accomplice liability to a separate homicide. (e) is wrong because acts performed under a death threat (duress) can lack the purpose to facilitate the crime, even if they physically assist it.
