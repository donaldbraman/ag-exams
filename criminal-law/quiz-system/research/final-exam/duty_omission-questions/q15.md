**Q15.** Assume, as an alternative fact, that when Alex pointed the gun and threatened Blake, Blake immediately drew a concealed weapon and shot Alex dead. Blake claims self-defense. Will this defense likely succeed?

(a) Yes, because Alex pointing a gun and issuing a conditional threat constituted an imminent deadly threat, making Blake's response objectively reasonable. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because any threat made during the commission of a drug felony automatically justifies the use of lethal force by the victim.
(c) No, because the threat was conditional on the police arriving, meaning it lacked the strict imminence required for deadly self-defense.
(d) No, because Blake was a participant in an illegal drug conspiracy, which permanently strips him of the right to claim self-defense.
(e) No, because Blake had a legal duty to retreat from the cabin before resorting to the use of a concealed deadly weapon.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. A drawn weapon combined with a threat to shoot "right now" constitutes an imminent threat of deadly force. Conditional threats ("If the cops come...") do not negate imminence when the gun is already drawn and pointed. (b) is wrong because there is no categorical self-defense immunity merely because a threat occurs during a felony. (c) is wrong because courts routinely hold that drawn weapons with immediate conditional demands constitute imminent threats. (d) is wrong because participating in a conspiracy does not permanently strip a person of the right to self-defense against sudden deadly escalation. (e) is wrong because the duty to retreat only applies if retreat can be made in complete safety, which is impossible with a gun pointed at one's chest.
