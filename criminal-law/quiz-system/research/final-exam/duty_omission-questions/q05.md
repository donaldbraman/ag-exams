**Q5.** Assume, whether or not Evan is liable, Chris survives and Blake is charged with attempted murder by omission for failing to provide medical aid. Will this attempt charge succeed?

(a) No, because attempted murder requires the specific intent to kill, and Blake only acted to avoid being shot by Alex. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because criminal omissions can never serve as the actus reus for any attempt charge, regardless of the actor's mental state.
(c) Yes, because Blake's failure to act with extreme indifference to human life satisfies the mental state for attempted murder.
(d) Yes, because Blake knowingly engaged in conduct that was practically certain to cause Chris's death by blocking the only exit.
(e) Yes, because the law transfers Alex's specific intent to kill Chris directly to Blake under the doctrine of accomplice liability.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Attempt requires specific intent (purpose) to bring about the prohibited result. Blake acted out of fear, lacking the specific intent to kill Chris. (b) is wrong because an omission can serve as the actus reus for attempt if a duty exists and the requisite intent is present. (c) is wrong because extreme indifference (recklessness) cannot support an attempt charge; attempt requires purpose. (d) is wrong because knowledge is generally insufficient for attempt, which strictly requires purpose regarding the result. (e) is wrong because accomplice liability transfers liability for completed crimes, not the specific intent required for the secondary actor's attempt.
