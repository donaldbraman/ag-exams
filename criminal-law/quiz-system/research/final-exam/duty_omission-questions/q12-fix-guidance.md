**Q12.** Dana is charged with conspiracy to manufacture opioids based on her sale of precursor chemicals. The prosecution points to the 20% "special handling" premium she charged. Under Lauria, what is the significance of this premium?

(a) The inflated price allows the jury to infer that Dana had a stake in the venture, supporting a finding of specific intent to further the conspiracy. <!-- correct -->
(b) The premium proves that Dana performed an overt act, which is sufficient to establish liability even if she lacked the intent to agree.
(c) The premium demonstrates that Dana was a member of a "wheel" conspiracy and is liable for all crimes committed by the other spokes.
(d) The premium establishes that Dana violated the Controlled Substances Act, but cannot be used to prove a conspiracy agreement.
(e) The premium invokes the buyer-seller exception, protecting Dana from conspiracy liability because she was acting in a purely commercial capacity.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under Lauria, providing goods with knowledge of their illegal use is generally insufficient for conspiracy, but charging an inflated price shows a "stake in the venture," which permits an inference of the specific intent or purpose to further the conspiracy. (b) is wrong because an overt act cannot substitute for the requisite mens rea. (c) is wrong because the facts describe a single operation, not a hub-and-spoke wheel conspiracy. (d) is wrong because the premium is exactly the type of evidence used to prove the conspiracy agreement. (e) is wrong because charging a premium for known illegal use defeats the protection of the buyer-seller exception.
