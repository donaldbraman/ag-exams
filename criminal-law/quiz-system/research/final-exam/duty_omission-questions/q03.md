**Q3.** Assume Cody's overdose establishes the need for medical aid. Did Bex owe Cody a legal duty to act prior to moving him to the bathroom?

(a) Yes, under the creation of peril doctrine, because Bex's course of conduct in providing the loaded syringe contributed to creating the life-threatening situation. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because under the common law, individuals have no general duty to rescue a stranger from an overdose.
(c) Yes, under a status relationship duty, because a drug seller automatically assumes a fiduciary obligation toward their buyer.
(d) No, because Cody's voluntary choice to inject the fentanyl severed any legal duty Bex might have owed.
(e) Yes, under a statutory duty, because all jurisdictions legally require bystanders to report a drug overdose.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because the fifth Jones category (creation of peril) imposes a legal duty to mitigate harm on a person who actively creates or increases the risk of that harm, such as by supplying a loaded syringe. (b) is wrong because while there is no general duty to rescue, Bex was not a mere bystander; her actions placed her within a specific duty category. (c) is wrong because buyer and seller is not a legally recognized status relationship (like parent-child) that independently creates a duty. (d) is wrong because the victim's voluntary ingestion does not negate the peril created by the supplier's course of conduct. (e) is wrong because Good Samaritan laws requiring reporting are a minority position, not universal.
