**Q8.** Assume, regardless of the homicide charges, Blake is charged with possession of the 5 kilograms of opioids found inside the safe. Does Blake constructively possess the drugs?

(a) Yes, because he knew the safe's combination and had the ability and intent to exercise control over the concealed drugs. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because his physical presence inside the cabin creates an irrebuttable legal presumption of possession over all illicit contents.
(c) No, because Alex was the recognized ringleader who brought the drugs and exclusively maintained ultimate ownership of the supply.
(d) No, because constructive possession requires the defendant to have sole and exclusive access to the location where the contraband is stored.
(e) No, because his contract was strictly for security and medical care, which negates the mens rea required for drug possession.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Constructive possession is established when a defendant has knowledge of the contraband's presence and the ability and intent to exercise dominion and control over it (knowing the safe combination). (b) is wrong because mere presence near contraband is insufficient to establish constructive possession. (c) is wrong because constructive possession can be joint; exclusive ownership is not required. (d) is wrong because exclusive access is not required; multiple people can jointly possess items. (e) is wrong because an employment contract does not negate the elements of knowing control and dominion over the safe.
