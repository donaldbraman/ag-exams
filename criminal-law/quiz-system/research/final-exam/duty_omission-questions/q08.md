**Q8.** Assume Alba is charged with Cody's death. Which of the following best analyzes whether Alba was a factual cause of the death?

(a) Alba is a factual cause because but for Alba incapacitating Dane, the overdose would have been fully reversed by the Narcan. <!-- correct -->
(b) Alba is not a factual cause because Cody voluntarily injected the fentanyl.
(c) Alba is a factual cause because Alba's verbal statement "Let him die" established the requisite malice aforethought.
(d) Alba is not a factual cause because the medical examiner determined respiratory failure from the drug was the physiological mechanism of death.
(e) Alba is a factual cause because conspiracy members are strictly liable for any death that occurs during the conspiracy.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because factual causation asks whether the result would have occurred "but for" the defendant's conduct. Since the medical examiner testified the Narcan would have fully reversed the overdose, Alba's act of knocking out the rescuer was a necessary but-for cause of the death. (b) is wrong because a victim's voluntary act does not sever factual causation if the defendant's subsequent act is still necessary to the final result. (c) is wrong because malice aforethought addresses mens rea, whereas factual causation is a purely empirical inquiry about the chain of events. (d) is wrong because there can be multiple but-for causes; the drug being the physiological cause does not prevent Alba's thwarting of the rescue from also being a necessary condition. (e) is wrong because strict liability does not bypass the need to prove factual causation for a result crime.
