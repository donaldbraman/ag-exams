**Q7.** Assume Alba is convicted of assaulting Dane. Can Bex be held liable for this assault under the Pinkerton doctrine?

(a) Yes, if the assault on a rescuer was a reasonably foreseeable consequence of operating the stash house conspiracy and done in furtherance of it. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because Bex was acting under duress when she stood guard during the assault.
(c) Yes, because Pinkerton imposes strict liability on all co-conspirators for any crime committed at the conspiracy's location.
(d) No, because the agreement between Alba and Bex was limited to selling drugs, not committing acts of violence.
(e) No, because Bex did not personally assist Alba in striking Dane.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because the Pinkerton doctrine holds conspirators vicariously liable for the substantive offenses of their co-conspirators if those offenses are committed in furtherance of the conspiracy and are a reasonably foreseeable consequence of the agreement. (b) is wrong because Bex's duress defense to standing guard does not automatically negate her Pinkerton liability stemming from her earlier, voluntary entry into the drug conspiracy. (c) is wrong because Pinkerton is not strict liability; it requires foreseeability and furtherance. (d) is wrong because Pinkerton liability extends beyond the expressly agreed-upon crimes to foreseeable collateral crimes. (e) is wrong because Pinkerton is a theory of vicarious liability that explicitly does not require personal participation in the substantive offense.
