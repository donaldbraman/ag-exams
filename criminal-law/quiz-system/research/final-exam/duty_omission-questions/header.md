# Scenario: Fentanyl Operation

### Facts

1. The investigation established that Alba and Bex verbally agreed to distribute fentanyl from Bex's apartment. Alba brought a handgun to the apartment to protect the operation, and Bex knew Alba was armed.
2. During a transaction, Alba negotiated the sale, pointing to a loaded syringe sitting on Bex's coffee table. Cody picked it up and handed the cash to Alba while Bex watched.
3. Cody voluntarily injected the fentanyl in the living room, immediately collapsed, and stopped breathing. Both Alba and Bex recognized the symptoms of a lethal overdose.
4. Bex pulled out her phone to dial 911. Alba immediately drew her handgun, stood between Bex and the only exit to ensure Bex was physically trapped, and ordered Bex to drop the phone.
5. Bex dropped the phone. Alba stated, "He's a rat. Let him die. Grab his legs." Alba forced the terrified Bex to help drag Cody into a windowless bathroom, shutting the door to seclude him from the main living area.
6. Alba ordered, "Go watch the hallway." Bex stood by the front door, looking through the peephole to ensure no one interrupted, acting solely out of fear that Alba would kill her if she refused.
7. Dane, an off-duty paramedic who lived next door, heard the collapse and Cody's agonal breathing through the thin walls. Recognizing a medical emergency, Dane kicked open Bex's locked front door, causing $800 in damage, intending to administer Narcan.
8. As Dane entered, Alba forcefully struck Dane in the head with the butt of the handgun. The investigation confirmed Alba intended only to knock Dane unconscious to prevent the rescue, not to kill him. Dane fell to the floor, incapacitated.
9. Cody died in the bathroom twenty minutes later. The medical examiner testified that Cody died from respiratory failure induced by the fentanyl, and confirmed that Dane's Narcan would have fully reversed the overdose had Dane not been incapacitated.

### Stem 1: Preliminary Offenses

The ADA has handed us the investigation file on the drug operation and the assault on the neighbor. Before we dive into the homicide charges, I need a memo analyzing the preliminary offenses. Look at Bex's constructive possession of the drugs and Cody's role in the conspiracy. Then, evaluate the liability surrounding Dane's attempted rescue: Dane's necessity defense, Alba's attempt mens rea for striking him, and Bex's Pinkerton liability for Alba's assault.

### Stem 2: Homicide and Affirmative Defenses

Cody's death elevates this case to homicide. I want to look at omission liability, causation, and grading. First, establish the duty-to-act categories for Alba and Bex, and determine if Cody's voluntary ingestion severs proximate causation. Next, evaluate the homicide theories: Alba's depraved-heart grading, and Bex's accomplice liability for acting as a lookout. Finally, analyze Bex's duress defenses against the homicide charges—specifically, the felony-murder duress exception and whether the common law duress-murder bar applies to her affirmative actions.
