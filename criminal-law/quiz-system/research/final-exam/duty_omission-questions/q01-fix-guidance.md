**Q1.** Does Bex have constructive possession of the fentanyl syringe located on her coffee table during the transaction?

(a) Yes, because she had both awareness of the drug's presence and the ability to exercise control over it in her own apartment. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because her presence during the transaction establishes absolute liability for any contraband found in her residence.
(c) No, because she did not physically touch or personally handle the syringe during the transaction with Cody.
(d) No, because Alba was the one actively negotiating the sale, demonstrating that Alba had exclusive control over the drugs.
(e) No, because the drugs were in plain view of a third party, breaking the chain of constructive possession for the apartment's owner.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because constructive possession requires awareness of the contraband and the ability to exercise dominion and control over it, which Bex clearly possessed as a co-conspirator operating in her own apartment. (b) is wrong because mere presence does not establish absolute liability without the ability and intent to control the item. (c) is wrong because constructive possession doctrine explicitly covers situations where the defendant lacks physical touch. (d) is wrong because possession can be joint; exclusive control by Alba is not legally required. (e) is wrong because plain view does not break constructive possession, and in fact often serves to support the awareness element.
