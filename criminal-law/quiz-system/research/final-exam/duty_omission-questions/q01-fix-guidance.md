**Q1.** Assume that Alba and Bex are charged with possession of the 50 grams of fentanyl found in the kitchen lockbox. Which of the following is the most accurate assessment of Bex's liability?

(a) Guilty of constructive possession because Bex's exclusive knowledge of the combination gave her the power and intent to exercise control over the drugs. <!-- correct -->
(b) Not guilty of constructive possession because Alba supplied the drugs and directed all sales, granting Alba exclusive physical control.
(c) Not guilty of constructive possession because placing the drugs in a lockbox inside a shared apartment defeats the exclusivity requirement.
(d) Guilty of actual possession because Bex physically handled the drugs when placing them into the lockbox and during the sale to Cody.
(e) Guilty of constructive possession because Bex's name was on the lease, which automatically establishes possession of all illegal contents.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Bex is in constructive possession because having the sole combination to the lockbox gave her the power and intent to exercise physical control over the fentanyl, satisfying the elements of constructive possession. (b) is wrong because Alba's ultimate authority over sales does not negate Bex's physical control over the stash. (c) is wrong because the lockbox itself was under her exclusive control, regardless of the shared apartment. (d) is wrong because the charge focuses on the stored 50 grams, making it constructive rather than actual possession. (e) is wrong because a lease alone does not prove knowledge or control over specific illegal contents.
