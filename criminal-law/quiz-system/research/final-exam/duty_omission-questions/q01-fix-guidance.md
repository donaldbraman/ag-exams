**Q1.** Did Blake owe a legal duty to seek medical aid for Chris when Chris collapsed?

(a) Yes, because he entered into an oral contract to act as Chris's medical caretaker. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because his presence during the overdose created a status relationship.
(c) No, because an oral contract without a formal written agreement is legally insufficient.
(d) No, because he was acting under duress when he locked the door.
(e) Yes, because Good Samaritan statutes impose a universal common law duty to rescue.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under the Jones categories, a contractual obligation to care for another creates a legal duty to act. An oral agreement in exchange for payment is sufficient to create this duty (Pestinikas). (b) is wrong because mere presence does not create a legally recognized status relationship. (c) is wrong because oral contracts are legally sufficient to form a duty of care. (d) is wrong because duress is an affirmative defense that excuses criminal liability, but it does not negate the underlying existence of the legal duty to act. (e) is wrong because Anglo-American law has no universal Good Samaritan duty to rescue.
