**Q13.** Assume that Alba and Bex are charged with felony murder based on Cody's death. Which of the following is the most critical requirement for the felony murder rule to apply?

(a) The jurisdiction must recognize drug distribution as an inherently dangerous predicate felony, which substitutes for the malice otherwise required for murder. <!-- correct -->
(b) The jurisdiction must apply strict liability automatically to any death that occurs within a shared residence during the commission of an ongoing crime.
(c) The felony murder rule cannot apply because Cody voluntarily injected the drugs, making him a co-felon who is excluded from the doctrine's protection.
(d) The felony murder rule cannot apply because the drug transaction had fully concluded before the overdose occurred, breaking the required res gestae continuous chain.
(e) The felony murder rule applies universally because Alba and Bex possessed a handgun during the commission of the drug distribution, elevating it to an armed felony.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** For the felony murder rule to apply, the underlying felony (drug distribution) must be recognized by the jurisdiction as inherently dangerous to human life, thereby substituting for the malice aforethought required for a murder conviction. (b) is wrong because felony murder requires a qualifying predicate felony, not merely the commission of any crime in a shared residence. (c) is wrong because the victim's voluntary participation in the drug use does not inherently exempt the distributors from felony murder liability. (d) is wrong because deaths resulting immediately from the distributed drugs are typically considered within the continuous res gestae of the felony. (e) is wrong because possessing a handgun does not automatically trigger felony murder unless the underlying crime is a qualifying predicate felony.
