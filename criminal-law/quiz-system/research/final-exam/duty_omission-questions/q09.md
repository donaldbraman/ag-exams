**Q9.** Assume Alex is charged with extreme indifference murder for pointing the gun at Blake and forcing him to block the door, leaving Chris to die. (Assume proximate cause is satisfied). Which mental state theory best supports a murder conviction for Alex?

(a) Extreme indifference, because pointing a gun to block medical rescue for a dying person evinces a depraved heart regarding human life. <!-- correct -->
(b) Ordinary negligence, because Alex merely failed to appreciate the full medical risk of handing Chris the untested dose of opioids.
(c) Specific intent, because Alex's statement about going to prison proves he explicitly wanted Chris to die to silence a witness.
(d) Felony murder, because the simple possession of narcotics is an inherently dangerous felony that automatically elevates any resulting death to murder.
(e) Misdemeanor manslaughter, because threatening a subordinate is a minor offense that unintentionally resulted in a fatal consequence for a third party.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Extreme indifference (depraved heart) murder requires a conscious disregard of a grave risk to human life. Pointing a gun to block rescue for a dying overdose victim clearly demonstrates this standard. (b) is wrong because Alex's conduct involved a conscious awareness and disregard of the risk, elevating it beyond ordinary negligence. (c) is wrong because Alex's statement shows a desire to avoid prison, which does not necessarily prove a specific purpose to kill. (d) is wrong because simple drug possession is generally not an inherently dangerous felony that supports felony murder. (e) is wrong because blocking a rescue at gunpoint is a severe felony, not a minor misdemeanor.
