**Q1.** Assume Blake is charged with a homicide offense based on his failure to provide medical aid to Chris. Which of the following establishes Blake's legal duty to act?

(a) The oral agreement, because a contractual obligation to provide medical care creates a legally recognized duty to act. <!-- correct -->
(b) His physical proximity, because being the closest able-bodied person to an emergency creates a legal duty of rescue.
(c) His knowledge of the danger, because being aware of a life-threatening overdose triggers a universal duty to intervene.
(d) His voluntary assumption of care, because merely standing near the door secluded Chris from receiving other assistance.
(e) His status relationship, because co-conspirators inherently owe each other a legally recognized duty of mutual protection.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Under *Jones* and *Pestinikas*, an oral agreement to provide care for compensation creates a legally recognized contractual duty to act. (b) is wrong because the law imposes no general duty to rescue strangers, regardless of physical proximity. (c) is wrong because mere knowledge of danger does not trigger a duty to act. (d) is wrong because voluntary assumption requires actual assistance and seclusion, not just creating a barrier. (e) is wrong because co-conspirator status is not one of the five *Jones* duty categories.
