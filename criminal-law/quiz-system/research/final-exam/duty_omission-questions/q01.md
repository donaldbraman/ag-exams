**Q1.** Assume Bex is charged with possession of the 50 grams of fentanyl. Which of the following is the most accurate assessment of Bex's liability?

(a) Guilty, because Bex knowingly exercised exclusive physical control over the lockbox containing the fentanyl. <!-- correct -->
(b) Not guilty, because Alba supplied the fentanyl and directed all sales.
(c) Not guilty, because constructive possession requires the defendant to be the primary beneficiary of the contraband.
(d) Guilty, but only under an accomplice theory, because Alba was the actual owner of the drugs.
(e) Not guilty, because the drugs were kept in a common area of the apartment where Cody could access them.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because Bex placed the drugs in a lockbox to which only she had the combination, satisfying the requirement for knowing and exclusive control over the contraband (constructive possession). (b) is wrong because one can possess contraband even if someone else supplied it or directs its sale. (c) is wrong because constructive possession is based on control and awareness, not financial benefit. (d) is wrong because Bex's exclusive combination gave her direct possessory control, making her liable as a principal. (e) is wrong because the lockbox was exclusively controlled by Bex, not accessible to Cody or the public.
