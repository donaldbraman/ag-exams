**Q11.** Assume Alba is charged with homicide for Cody's death. Which of the following theories best supports grading the offense as murder rather than manslaughter?

(a) Alba's actions in dragging Cody to isolation and violently thwarting a rescuer demonstrated an extreme indifference to the value of human life. <!-- correct -->
(b) Alba's failure to call 911 constituted ordinary negligence, which is sufficient for murder when the victim is a known informant.
(c) Alba's initial provision of the drug was a misdemeanor, elevating any resulting death to murder.
(d) Alba's intent to only incapacitate Dane transfers into a specific intent to kill Cody.
(e) Alba's voluntary intoxication from the stash house environment legally implies malice aforethought.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because depraved-heart (extreme indifference) murder applies when a defendant acts with gross recklessness manifesting an extreme indifference to human life. Isolating a dying person and assaulting their rescuer perfectly illustrates this standard. (b) is wrong because ordinary negligence only supports negligent homicide or involuntary manslaughter, not murder. (c) is wrong because the misdemeanor manslaughter rule elevates a death to manslaughter, not murder. (d) is wrong because transferred intent requires an actual intent to kill, which Alba lacked toward Dane. (e) is wrong because there are no facts indicating Alba was intoxicated, and intoxication does not inherently imply malice.
