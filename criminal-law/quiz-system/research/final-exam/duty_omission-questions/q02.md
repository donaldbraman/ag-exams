**Q2.** Assume Alba and Bex are charged with conspiracy to distribute fentanyl. Are they guilty of this offense?

(a) Yes, because their verbal agreement to use the apartment as a stash house, combined with the storage of the drugs, satisfies the agreement and overt act requirements. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because Alba retained ultimate authority over the sales, precluding a true bilateral agreement.
(c) No, because the conspiracy merged with the completed offense of drug distribution once Cody received the syringe.
(d) Yes, because merely discussing the possibility of selling drugs satisfies the actus reus for conspiracy in most jurisdictions.
(e) No, because Cody's fatal overdose was a foreseeable but unintended consequence of the agreement.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because the agreement was explicitly formed, and storing the fentanyl in the lockbox constituted an overt act in furtherance of the conspiracy. (b) is wrong because unequal roles in a criminal enterprise do not defeat the existence of a bilateral agreement. (c) is wrong because conspiracy, unlike attempt, does not merge with the completed target offense. (d) is wrong because most jurisdictions require an overt act in furtherance of the agreement, not just discussion. (e) is wrong because an unintended outcome of the target offense does not negate the elements of the conspiracy itself.
