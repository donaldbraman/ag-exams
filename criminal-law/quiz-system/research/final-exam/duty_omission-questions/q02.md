**Q2.** Assume, whether or not Blake had a duty, Alex is charged with a homicide offense based on his failure to call 911 for Chris. What establishes Alex's legal duty to act?

(a) Yes, because he supplied the drugs and actively blocked rescue, creating a peril that triggered a duty to mitigate harm. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because his status as the ringleader of the operation automatically imposes a parental-like duty of care over subordinates.
(c) Yes, because all drug distributors owe a strict statutory duty to provide medical assistance to their immediate end-users.
(d) No, because Alex never explicitly contracted to provide medical care to Chris, leaving the legal duty entirely with Blake.
(e) No, because the harm was caused by Chris's voluntary decision to inject the drugs, severing any duty Alex might owe.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Under *Voss*, creating a dangerous situation—such as providing narcotics and actively blocking rescue—triggers a legal duty to mitigate the resulting harm. (b) is wrong because "ringleader" is not a legally recognized status relationship that creates a duty. (c) is wrong because no universal statutory duty exists requiring drug dealers to provide medical care. (d) is wrong because the creation of peril category operates independently of any contractual agreements. (e) is wrong because the victim's voluntary ingestion does not negate the duty arising from creating the peril.
