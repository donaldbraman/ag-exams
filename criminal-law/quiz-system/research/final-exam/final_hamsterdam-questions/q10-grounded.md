**Q10.** Assume instead that Victor is charged as an accomplice to the shooting. Under the traditional purpose standard for accomplice liability, is Victor guilty?

(a) Yes, because Victor knew that Xavier was armed and that violence was substantially certain to occur during a drug robbery.
(b) Yes, because Victor's role as a lookout provided actual physical assistance that facilitated Xavier's ability to draw his weapon and fire.
(c) No, because Victor did not provide the weapon, aim the gun, or physically pull the trigger during the confrontation with Yasmin.
(d) No, because Victor merely acted as a lookout for drug sales and did not have the conscious objective to promote or facilitate the shooting. <!-- correct -->
(e) No, because accomplice liability is categorically precluded when the principal actor asserts any form of imperfect or perfect self-defense.

**Answer:** (d)

**Explanation:** Traditional accomplice liability requires the defendant to assist the principal with the true purpose (conscious objective) of promoting or facilitating the specific crime charged. While Victor intentionally acted as a lookout for the drug distribution, there are no facts to suggest his purpose was to promote Xavier's unjustified shooting of a surrendering person. Option (a) incorrectly applies a knowledge/foreseeability standard instead of purpose. Option (b) fails because even if his presence facilitated the act, he lacked the requisite purposeful mens rea for the shooting. Option (c) incorrectly implies that physical participation in the actus reus is required for accomplice liability. Option (e) is legally false; a principal's failed defense does not shield an accomplice.
