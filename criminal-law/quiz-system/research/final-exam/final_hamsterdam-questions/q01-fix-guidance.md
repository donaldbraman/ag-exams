## Issue 1 — grounding

**Q1.** Evaluate William's racketeering liability under the corporate enterprise theory. Can the police precinct serve as the "enterprise" for a federal RICO charge against William?

(a) Yes, because a legitimate organization's infrastructure can constitute a RICO enterprise when repurposed to conduct racketeering activity. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because a government entity possesses lawful authority and therefore cannot legally constitute an association-in-fact enterprise.
(c) No, because the enterprise element requires an inherently criminal organization distinct from legitimate societal institutions.
(d) Yes, but only if the precinct's primary overall purpose was transformed from law enforcement to criminal extortion.
(e) No, because William and Davis were government employees acting under the color of law, shielding the precinct itself.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** A legitimate corporation or government entity can serve as a RICO enterprise when its ordinary infrastructure is repurposed to conduct or facilitate racketeering activity. William and Davis repurposed official police dispatch, marked cars, and holding cells to facilitate their extortion and drug ring (Fact 3), satisfying the enterprise element under the corporate enterprise theory. (b) fails because lawful authority does not immunize an entity from being an enterprise if its structure is hijacked for racketeering. (c) is incorrect because RICO does not require an inherently criminal organization (like the Mafia). (d) misstates the law; the entity's primary purpose can remain legitimate as long as its affairs are being conducted through a pattern of racketeering. (e) fails because acting under color of law does not shield individuals or the entity from RICO liability when committing predicate acts.
