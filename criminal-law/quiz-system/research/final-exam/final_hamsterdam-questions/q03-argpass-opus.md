**Q3.** Assume Victor and Xavier are charged with conspiracy to distribute heroin. Does their arrangement satisfy the agreement element of conspiracy?

(a) Yes, because Victor's physical act of guarding the safe constituted a substantial step toward the completion of the joint distribution offense.
(b) Yes, because they verbally agreed to split profits from the drug sales, establishing a mutual understanding to pursue a common criminal objective. <!-- correct -->
(c) No, because the informal Free Zone created by the police effectively legalized their daily conduct, legally eliminating the unlawful objective requirement.
(d) No, because Xavier retained the only key to the safe, meaning there was no shared access necessary to form a bilateral agreement.
(e) No, because Victor's role was merely secondary as a lookout, which is insufficient to establish intent to commit the underlying distribution offense.

**Answer:** (b)

**Explanation:** Conspiracy requires an agreement between two or more persons to commit a criminal offense. Their explicit verbal agreement to split profits 70/30 demonstrates a mutual understanding to pursue the shared criminal objective of distributing heroin. Option (a) incorrectly cites the "substantial step" test, which belongs to attempt law, not the agreement element of conspiracy. Option (c) is false; informal police toleration does not repeal the statutory prohibition against drug distribution. Option (d) is wrong because shared physical access is not required to form a cognitive agreement. Option (e) fails because lookouts purposefully promote the target crime and are quintessential conspirators.
