**Q5.** Assume Yasmin is arrested in the alley. Under the Model Penal Code, is she guilty of attempted robbery?

(a) Yes, because donning the mask and loading the gun 50 feet away constitute a substantial step strongly corroborative of her criminal purpose. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because she had not yet entered the physical boundaries of the Zone, meaning she had not crossed the threshold of dangerous proximity.
(c) Yes, because mere preparation to commit a crime of violence is sufficient for attempt liability under the modern penal code framework.
(d) No, because she had not yet demanded the stash from Xavier, leaving her the opportunity to freely and voluntarily abandon her plan.
(e) Yes, because uttering "Time to get paid" while holding a weapon constitutes the completed target offense of criminal extortion.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under the MPC, attempt requires a "substantial step" that is strongly corroborative of the actor's criminal purpose. Arriving at the location, masking one's face, and loading a weapon just 50 feet away clearly satisfy this standard. (b) fails because "dangerous proximity" is the restrictive common-law test, which the MPC explicitly rejected in favor of the broader substantial step test. (c) fails because "mere preparation" remains insufficient even under the MPC; the act must amount to a substantial step. (d) fails because while abandonment is an affirmative defense under the MPC, arriving at the scene and loading a weapon already completes the actus reus for attempt; she does not need to issue the demand first. (e) fails because her intent was robbery, and preparing to rob someone is an attempt, not a completed extortion offense.
