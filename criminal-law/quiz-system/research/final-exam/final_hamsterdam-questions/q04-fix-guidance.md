## Issue 1 — audit

**Q4.** William asserts a necessity defense against federal charges, claiming his Free Zone stopped a greater evil of gang warfare. Will his necessity defense succeed?

(a) No, because Congress's classification of controlled substances categorically preempts the defense by legislatively weighing the competing harms. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because containing gang violence and saving lives clearly outweighs the harm of allowing a localized, controlled illicit drug market.
(c) No, because police officers are categorically barred from asserting justification defenses for conduct committed while officially on duty.
(d) Yes, because William lacked a reasonable legal alternative to stop the 300% surge in gang violence using traditional law enforcement.
(e) No, because William created the emergency by establishing the Free Zone, permanently barring the defense under the MPC fault rule.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** The necessity defense is absolutely barred when the legislature has already weighed the competing harms and preempted the specific conduct. In the federal system, Congress's scheduling of controlled substances forecloses the necessity defense for drug distribution (*Oakland Cannabis*). Thus, William cannot ask a jury to re-weigh the harms of distributing drugs. (b) fails because a court or jury cannot substitute its own harm-balancing for the legislature's classification. (c) is incorrect; police officers can and frequently do use justification defenses (like self-defense), though necessity is preempted here. (d) fails because even if he lacked a legal alternative, legislative preemption serves as an absolute bar. (e) misapplies the facts and law; William did not create the surge in gang violence (the emergency), and under the MPC, creating an emergency calibrates rather than permanently bars the defense.

**Tags:** chapters: [21], topics: [necessity, legislative preemption], difficulty: medium, cognitive: application

**Grounding:** Chapter 21, United States v. Oakland Cannabis Buyers' Cooperative (legislative-preemption-absolute)
