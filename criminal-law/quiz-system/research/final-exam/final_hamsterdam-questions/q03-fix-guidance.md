## Issue 1 — grounding

**Q3.** Evaluate the conspiracy charge against Xavier and Victor for the drug sales. Do they satisfy the agreement element for criminal conspiracy?

(a) Yes, because their explicit verbal commitment to divide the proceeds of the illicit operation constitutes a mutual criminal agreement. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because Xavier retained exclusive control over the heroin supply, rendering Victor a mere accomplice rather than a co-conspirator.
(c) Yes, because Victor's physical presence at the scene while Xavier sold drugs constitutes an implicit agreement under unilateral theory.
(d) No, because the unequal 70/30 profit split demonstrates that Victor lacked the shared criminal purpose required for conspiracy liability.
(e) Yes, but only if they explicitly discussed the specific quantities of heroin they intended to sell to their prospective buyers.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Conspiracy requires an agreement between two or more persons to commit an unlawful act. Xavier and Victor verbally agreed to split the profits of their drug sales (Fact 4), explicitly forming a bilateral agreement to distribute heroin. (b) is incorrect because unequal control over the means of the conspiracy does not negate the existence of the agreement itself. (c) fails because mere presence is generally insufficient to prove agreement, and here there is actual evidence of an explicit verbal agreement. (d) is wrong because conspirators do not need an equal share of the profits; they only need to agree to further the criminal objective. (e) imposes a false standard; the agreement does not need to specify the exact granular details of the future crimes, only the general criminal objective.

**Tags:** chapters: [19], topics: [conspiracy, agreement], difficulty: easy, cognitive: application

**Grounding:** Foundational Conspiracy / Bilateral Agreement Requirement
