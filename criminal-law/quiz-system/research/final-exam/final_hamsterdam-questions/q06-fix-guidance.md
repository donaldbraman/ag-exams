## Issue 1 — audit

**Q6.** Assume the jurisdiction imposes a traditional duty to retreat. Was Xavier legally required to flee through the exit door instead of drawing his weapon?

(a) Yes, because a safe retreat was available and he was outside his home, obligating him to avoid using deadly force if possible. <!-- correct -->
(b) No, because Yasmin pointed a loaded revolver at his chest, which completely suspends the duty to retreat in all jurisdictions.
(c) Yes, because Xavier was engaged in an illegal drug enterprise, which automatically strips him of the right to claim self-defense.
(d) No, because the warehouse was his place of business, triggering the castle doctrine exception to the standard retreat requirement.
(e) Yes, because the threat was not yet imminent, given that Yasmin had merely demanded the stash and had not yet fired her weapon.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** In a duty-to-retreat jurisdiction, a defendant must retreat before using deadly force if a safe retreat is possible. Xavier saw a clearly marked, unobstructed exit door ten feet behind him (Fact 8) and was not in his home, triggering the duty. (b) is wrong because pointing a gun creates the threat that triggers self-defense analysis, but it does not suspend the duty to retreat if a safe escape is actually available. (c) fails because committing a crime does not automatically strip a person of the right to self-defense against a lethal attack. (d) is incorrect because the castle doctrine universally applies to the home; while some jurisdictions extend it to a lawful workplace, an illegal abandoned warehouse drug market does not qualify. (e) is wrong because a pointed, loaded gun constitutes an imminent threat regardless of whether it has been fired.
