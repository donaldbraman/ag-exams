**Q2.** Assume Vance is charged with a RICO violation. Will this charge succeed?

(a) Not guilty, because he is a police officer and the police department is the only legal enterprise to which he legitimately belongs.
(b) Not guilty, because a defendant must be a formal member of the organization's leadership structure to participate in the conduct of its affairs.
(c) Guilty, because his sustained provision of protection for bribes constitutes participation in the conduct of the Syndicate's affairs, which qualifies as an enterprise. <!-- correct -->
(d) Guilty, because any individual who accepts a bribe from a criminal syndicate automatically becomes a full member of that syndicate for RICO purposes.
(e) Not guilty, because the Bayside Syndicate is an illegal association in fact, and RICO enterprises must be legally registered and formally organized corporations.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** (c) is correct. Under RICO, an enterprise includes a group of individuals associated in fact. By systematically providing protection and files in exchange for bribes, Vance participated in the operation or management of the Syndicate's affairs. (a) is wrong because a police officer can be charged under RICO for participating in a criminal enterprise outside their legal department. (b) is wrong because outsiders who exert control or participate in the enterprise's affairs (such as through bribery) can be liable; formal membership is not required. (d) is wrong because mere acceptance of a bribe doesn't automatically make one a formal member, though it may satisfy the participation element. (e) is wrong because RICO explicitly covers "associated in fact" illegal enterprises like street gangs and syndicates.
