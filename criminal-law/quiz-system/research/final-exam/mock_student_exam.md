# Scenario 1: The Genesis

### Facts

*Assume the following facts can be proven beyond a reasonable doubt.*

1. Albert Vance, a tenured chemistry professor, was recently diagnosed with terminal cancer and faced mounting medical debts. Desperate for money, Albert decided to secretly manufacture 'Blue-X,' a Schedule I narcotic.

2. Albert approached Benny Cole, a former student involved in the local drug trade. They verbally agreed that Albert would synthesize the Blue-X and Benny would manage street distribution, splitting all profits equally.

3. To procure precursors, Albert contacted Dan, a commercial chemical supplier. Dan knew Albert intended to use the chemicals to manufacture illicit drugs. Citing the legal risk, Dan charged Albert a 500% markup on 10 gallons of highly volatile ether, explicitly stating, 'I don't want any share of whatever you're selling; just pay my invoice.'

4. Albert needed a high-speed centrifuge. He walked into an adjacent university lab and took a commercial centrifuge to keep in his own workspace, genuinely believing it was the exact machine assigned to his own university grant, when in fact it belonged to Dr. Smith.

5. The State Public Welfare Chemical Act states: 'It shall be unlawful to store more than 5 gallons of ether in a single facility without a fire safety permit.' The statute is silent on mental state and carries a maximum penalty of 30 days in jail.

6. Albert read the Act online but did not apply for a permit. He personally concluded, based on his own reading of the statute's preamble, that university laboratories were implicitly exempt from the permit requirement.

7. Albert had recently started experimental cancer medication. The warning label explicitly cautioned that it causes 'sudden, severe, and uncontrollable muscle spasms.' Over the prior week, Albert experienced three such spasms, causing him to drop heavy objects, yet he decided to proceed with the volatile chemical synthesis anyway.

8. Benny delivered the final pseudoephedrine precursors to the lab. Immediately after, Albert began synthesizing the Blue-X—a process the jurisdiction explicitly classifies as an enumerated felony for purposes of felony murder. He lifted a flask of highly reactive catalyst to pour into the ether vat.

9. As Albert held the flask over the vat, a severe muscle spasm caused his hand to jerk violently, dumping the entire flask into the ether. The sudden over-concentration triggered an immediate, massive explosion that blew out the laboratory's glass walls.

10. Chloe Davis, an adult lab assistant who had no knowledge of the illicit manufacturing, was walking past the lab door at that exact moment. The explosion shattered the glass, inflicting deep, life-threatening lacerations to her neck and chest.

11. Paramedics stabilized Chloe and transported her to the hospital. In the emergency room, a doctor committed an act of ordinary medical negligence by misreading Chloe's chart and administering a massive dose of a blood thinner instead of a coagulant. The blood thinner caused her stabilized wounds to hemorrhage violently, resulting in her death.

12. Following the explosion, Benny fled the scene with the one intact case of Blue-X product. He took the case to a stash house rented under a fake name by his associate, Kev.

13. Kev placed the Blue-X in a floor safe. Benny did not live at the stash house, but Kev gave Benny the safe's combination so Benny could independently retrieve the drugs for street sales whenever he wanted.

14. One evening, while Benny was at the stash house auditing the product, a rival dealer named Slash kicked down the front door wielding a machete, screaming, 'Give me the Blue-X or I'm chopping your head off right now!'

15. Slash cornered Benny in a back room. The room had an open, ground-floor window immediately behind Benny. In this jurisdiction, there is a statutory duty to retreat before using deadly force if a safe retreat is available. Benny did not attempt to exit the window; instead, he drew a concealed handgun and shot Slash dead.

16. Hearing the gunshot, Kev emerged from the basement, furious that Benny had brought violence to his stash house. Kev racked a shotgun, pointed it at Benny's head, and screamed, 'Take this duffel bag of Blue-X to the drop point right now or I'll blow a hole in your chest!'

17. Terrified of Kev's immediate threat, Benny grabbed the bag, fled the stash house, and delivered the drugs to the specified location, where he was immediately arrested by undercover police.

# Scenario 2: The Blowback

### Facts

*Assume the following facts can be proven beyond a reasonable doubt.*

1. For over two years, Deacon continuously operated a fentanyl distribution network with a defined hierarchy, weekly operations meetings, and distinct roles for his chemist, enforcers, and street-level sellers.

2. Vance approached Deacon at a diner, stated 'Your time is over, old man,' and spat directly in Deacon's face.

3. Vance, a rival distributor, walked up to Deacon's table at a diner. Vance leaned over, stated 'Your time is over, old man,' and spat directly in Deacon's face. As Vance smugly turned away to walk to the counter, Deacon immediately retrieved a vial...

4. The next day, Deacon instructed his chemist, Sloane, to formulate a new drug batch. Deacon ordered: 'Double the fentanyl concentration. I want the street to know our product is the strongest.'

5. Sloane warned Deacon: 'This concentration is extremely dangerous. Given normal injection habits, it is highly likely to cause fatal overdoses.' Deacon replied: 'That's exactly the reputation we need. Bag it up.' Sloane packaged the product and gave it to Deacon's sellers.

6. Chloe, a 30-year-old customer, purchased the new batch. She injected her standard volume and died of a fentanyl overdose within an hour.

7. With Vance dead, Deacon directed his enforcer, Trey, to break into Vance's commercial storage unit to steal Vance's remaining drug proceeds. Deacon provided Trey with a crowbar but no weapons, and remained at his own home.

8. Trey drove to a location five blocks from the storage facility and parked. He put on a ski mask and was about to grab his crowbar when spotted a security officer who appeared to be looking at him and calling the police, then heard sirens. Trey drove away.

9. To escape, Trey committed the state-law predicate offense of 'Felony Evasion' (statutorily defined strictly as 'willfully fleeing a pursuing police vehicle in a motor vehicle'). During the chase, Trey drove 80 mph through a residential area while his driver's license was suspended. He struck a pedestrian, Omar, killing him instantly.

10. Police arrested Trey immediately after the crash. A search of the van's center console revealed 50 individual baggies of fentanyl. Trey told police, 'The van is Deacon's, I just drive it. I don't know what's in the console.'

11. A review of the network's financial ledgers recovered from Sloane's apartment showed that she did not receive a flat salary; instead, she received a 20% cut of all profits from the network's drug sales.

# Scenario 3: The Distribution Ring

### Facts

*Assume the following facts can be proven beyond a reasonable doubt.*

1. Artie, seeking to expand his distribution ring, summons Ben, Cole, and Damon to his apartment and asks them if they are in to 'clear out the East Side stash house and take over the corners.'

2. Ben, Cole, and Damon each nod silently and accept a $500 cash advance from Artie, though they do not discuss specific details among themselves.

3. Artie assigns Ben to sell one kilogram of stimulants to 'Vulture,' who is actually an undercover detective in a sting operation. Vulture does not actually have the funds to purchase the drugs, and no drugs would ever have reached the street.

4. Ben drives toward the designated meeting point with the drugs. While still half a mile away, he receives a text from his mother asking him to come home. He pulls over, turns his car around while no police are visible, and flushes the stimulants down the toilet at a gas station.

5. Meanwhile, Damon and Cole drive to the East Side stash house. Outside, Damon racks a loaded 9mm handgun in the car. Cole expresses surprise, but Damon says it is 'just for show.' Cole proceeds to his assigned post to act as a lookout at the back door.

6. Damon kicks in the front door. Inside, the occupant, Vic, mocks Damon as an 'errand boy.' Damon's face flushes red with sudden rage and he immediately shoots Vic dead without ever demanding drugs or money.

7. Cole hears the gunshot, sprints away from the house, and hides in a motel. He cuts off all communication, ignoring subsequent calls from Artie, but he does not notify the police or explicitly tell Artie he is quitting the operation.

8. Damon texts Artie confirming the kill and noting that no product was retrieved. Artie replies, 'Stay low, good work,' and wires Damon an additional $1,000.

9. The drugs are stored in a locked safe under the couch; Artie knows the drugs are inside and controls the space, but lacks the key, and he occasionally pushes the safe aside to sweep under the couch to maintain physical dominion over the container.
