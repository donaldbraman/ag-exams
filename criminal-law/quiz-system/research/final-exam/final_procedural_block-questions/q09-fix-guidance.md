(a) Luis is guilty of felony murder because the transportation of illegal narcotics inevitably attracts violent rival gangs, making the underlying crime inherently dangerous in its factual commission.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** Under the abstract approach to the felony-murder rule, a court looks only at the statutory elements of the underlying felony to determine if it is inherently dangerous. Transporting narcotics does not require a lethal risk to human life in the abstract, making (c) correct. (a) is incorrect because the abstract test strictly ignores the factual circumstances, such as attracting rival gangs, and looks only at the statutory elements. (b) is incorrect because felony murder strictly requires the underlying felony to be inherently dangerous, not just factually fatal in a specific instance. (d) is incorrect because self-defense does not serve as a blanket affirmative defense to the underlying narcotics felony itself. (e) is incorrect because transferred intent applies to felony murder; deliberate execution is not required if the killing furthers an inherently dangerous felony.
