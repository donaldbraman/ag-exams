## Issue 1 — grounding

**Q11.** Assume for this question that Luis is found guilty of a homicide offense for the bystander's death. Under the *Pinkerton* doctrine, is Kevin vicariously guilty of the homicide?

(a) Kevin is guilty of the homicide because armed conflicts and resulting casualties are a reasonably foreseeable consequence of operating a regional illicit drug distribution conspiracy. <!-- correct -->
(b) Kevin is guilty of the homicide because corporate executives are held strictly liable for any tortious or criminal acts committed by their direct employees during working hours.
(c) Kevin is not guilty of the homicide because the conspiracy's explicit agreement was strictly limited to transporting narcotics, thereby legally excluding any unforeseen acts of lethal violence.
(d) Kevin is not guilty of the homicide because Luis was acting entirely independently to protect his own life, which legally severs Kevin's vicarious liability for the fatal gunshot.
(e) Kevin is not guilty of the homicide because Pinkerton liability strictly requires the targeted co-conspirator to be physically present at the active scene of the substantive offense.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** *Pinkerton* liability allows a defendant to be convicted for the foreseeable substantive crimes committed by co-conspirators in furtherance of the conspiracy. Lethal armed conflicts and resulting homicides are widely recognized as reasonably foreseeable consequences of large-scale drug distribution, making (a) correct. (b) is incorrect because corporate strict liability concepts do not apply to vicarious criminal liability for homicides. (c) is incorrect because *Pinkerton* specifically captures unforeseen collateral acts like violence if they are a reasonably foreseeable consequence of the underlying agreement. (d) is incorrect because Luis acting to protect the shared cargo and survive an ambush during transit is sufficiently connected to the conspiracy's execution. (e) is incorrect because *Pinkerton* was specifically designed to capture leaders who are not physically present at the active crime scene.

**Tags:** chapters: [6, 14], topics: [Pinkerton liability, foreseeability, homicide], difficulty: medium, cognitive: application

**Grounding:** Under Pinkerton, violence and resulting homicides are frequently held to be a reasonably foreseeable consequence of participating in a large-scale illegal narcotics distribution conspiracy.
