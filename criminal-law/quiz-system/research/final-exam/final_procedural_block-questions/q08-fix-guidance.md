## Issue 1 — audit

**Q8.** When the rival gang members ambushed the truck, Luis returned fire to save his own life and protect the cargo. Does Luis have a valid claim of self-defense against the gang members?

(a) Luis's use of deadly force was fully justified because he faced an imminent threat of death or serious bodily harm from the unprovoked rival gang ambush. <!-- correct -->
(b) Luis's use of deadly force was fully justified because he possessed a legal privilege to defend his employer's valuable commercial cargo from an armed robbery attempt.
(c) Luis's use of deadly force was entirely unjustified because an individual actively engaged in any criminal activity automatically forfeits the inherent right to assert a self-defense claim.
(d) Luis's use of deadly force was entirely unjustified because he was strictly required to retreat from the gang ambush before resorting to lethal measures to protect the narcotics.
(e) Luis's use of deadly force was partially justified, reducing his liability to manslaughter, because his intent was equally divided between saving his life and protecting the illicit goods.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** The right of self-defense allows a non-aggressor to use deadly force when reasonably believing it is necessary to prevent imminent death or serious bodily harm. Luis was facing a lethal unprovoked ambush, justifying his defensive return fire to save his own life, making (a) correct. (b) is incorrect because the law strictly prohibits the use of deadly force merely to protect property or commercial cargo. (c) is incorrect because engaging in a concurrent non-violent crime like transporting narcotics does not automatically strip a defendant of self-defense rights against lethal force. (d) is incorrect because most jurisdictions do not require retreat when safely fleeing a sudden high-speed armed ambush is impossible. (e) is incorrect because an accompanying desire to protect property does not legally vitiate an otherwise valid self-defense claim against a lethal threat.

**Tags:** chapters: [16], topics: [self-defense, deadly force, protection of property], difficulty: easy, cognitive: application

**Grounding:** A non-aggressor is justified in using deadly force if they reasonably believe it is necessary to protect against an imminent threat of death or serious bodily injury, regardless of whether they are simultaneously engaged in transporting contraband.
