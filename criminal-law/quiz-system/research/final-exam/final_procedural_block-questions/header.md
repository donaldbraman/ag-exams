# Scenario: Waterfront Syndicate

### Facts

1. The investigation established that Dominic Vane oversees the 'Waterfront Syndicate,' a structured association engaged in ongoing loansharking, extortion, and narcotics distribution.
2. Wiretap surveillance recorded Dominic telling his lieutenant, Leo: 'We need to lock down the East End pill market.' Leo replied, 'I'll recruit the street crew today.'
3. Leo subsequently hired Marcus to distribute the syndicate's narcotics.
4. Marcus stored the narcotics in a gym locker. He gave his girlfriend, Chloe, the combination code so she could retrieve packages, but Marcus intentionally kept his own daily gym equipment in the locker to ensure he maintained joint access and control over the contents.
5. Marcus instructed Chloe to retrieve a specific bag from the locker. When Chloe retrieved the bag, she honestly believed it contained stolen cash, not narcotics.
6. Chloe drove to the gym. As she walked through the front doors, police intercepted and arrested her. Unbeknownst to her, police had already replaced the narcotics with a lookalike fake powder two days prior.
7. To execute the syndicate's agreed-upon plan to 'lock down the East End pill market' (Fact 2), Leo decided to rob and eliminate a rival pill dealer operating inside a syndicate-owned nightclub.
8. Before initiating the robbery, Leo signaled the bartender. The bartender, knowing Leo was about to physically assault the rival to protect syndicate turf but completely unaware Leo had a gun, turned off the security cameras to help Leo avoid detection.
9. Bruno was the head of security for the nightclub, working under an explicit written employment contract that imposed a legal duty to 'physically intervene to protect club patrons and employees from violent altercations.' Despite this specific contractual duty, Bruno intentionally watched the armed robbery unfold from ten feet away without intervening.
10. During the robbery, Leo recklessly waved his loaded gun directly in the rival's face, demanding his stash.
11. In response to the gun in his face, the rival dealer pulled his own knife and lunged at Leo. This caused Leo to stumble backward, leading to the accidental discharge of Leo's gun, which killed the rival.
12. Marcus (who is Black) was apprehended driving away from the club during a pretextual traffic stop under 'Operation Sweep.' Police data established that Operation Sweep stopped Black drivers at ten times the rate of white drivers, yielding contraband in fewer than 1% of all stops.
13. Marcus's defense attorney filed a pre-trial motion for discovery regarding selective prosecution, relying solely on this aggregate statistical data about Operation Sweep to justify the request, without identifying a specific similarly situated white driver who was not stopped.
14. During plea negotiations, AUSA Victor Vance offered Marcus a 3-year plea deal, threatening that if Marcus went to trial, Vance would use his prosecutorial discretion to charge Marcus under an overlapping federal statute carrying a 10-year mandatory minimum for the exact same conduct.
15. The investigation revealed that State Senator Thomas Sterling pushed through a local zoning change that benefited a waterfront property owned by Dominic's syndicate. Two weeks after the vote, Senator Sterling accepted a $25,000 'token of appreciation' from Dominic, though no prior quid pro quo agreement was ever proven.
16. During voir dire at Dominic's federal trial, defense attorney Silvia Rossi used peremptory challenges to strike six of the seven Black individuals in the jury pool, stating on the record that they 'appeared hostile to corporate business.'
17. In his final instructions to the jury, the trial judge stated: 'You possess no legal right to nullify the law. If you acquit contrary to the evidence, you will violate your sworn oath and could be subject to contempt proceedings.'
