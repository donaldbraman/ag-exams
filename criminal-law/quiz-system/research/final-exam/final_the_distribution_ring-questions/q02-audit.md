**Q2.** Darius loaded a handgun, drove around searching for Vance for two hours, but was arrested before locating him. Assume the jurisdiction applies either the common law proximity test or the MPC substantial step test to his attempt charge. Which of the following best describes Darius's liability for attempted murder regarding Vance?

(a) Guilty under both tests, because his confession to police provides the required mens rea, retroactively converting his prior preparatory acts into a fully completed criminal attempt under the law.
(b) Not guilty under the proximity test because he had not yet approached Vance, but guilty under the substantial step test because searching for the victim while armed corroborates his criminal purpose. <!-- correct -->
(c) Guilty under the proximity test because driving for two hours placed him dangerously near completion, but not guilty under the MPC because he did not actually fire the weapon at Vance.
(d) Not guilty under both tests because the police intervention was an independent intervening cause that prevented him from crossing the legal threshold beyond mere preparation into a criminal attempt.
(e) Guilty under the substantial step test, but only if the prosecution proves that Vance was actually in the neighborhood at the exact time Darius was actively searching for him.

**Answer:** (b)

**Explanation:** The correct answer is (b). Under the common law proximity test, Darius is not guilty because he had not yet found Vance and was not "dangerously near" completion. Under the MPC substantial step test, searching for the contemplated victim while armed satisfies the actus reus. (a) is wrong because a confession of mens rea does not cure a deficient actus reus. (c) reverses the tests' thresholds; the MPC is the easier standard to meet. (d) is wrong because police intervention does not erase liability if the threshold was already crossed. (e) is wrong because actual presence of the victim is not required for a substantial step.
