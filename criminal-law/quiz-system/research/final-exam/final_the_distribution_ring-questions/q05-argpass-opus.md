**Q5.** During the stash house raid, Trey waited in the running car, genuinely believing the plan was only to intimidate rivals and steal product. He is charged under federal law as an accomplice to Silas's armed robbery (a robbery involving a firearm). Can Trey be convicted as an accomplice to the armed robbery?

(a) Yes, because by driving the getaway car, he provided actual assistance that facilitated the offense, which satisfies both the actus reus and the specific intent mens rea requirements for accomplice liability.
(b) Yes, because he learned about the firearm when Silas re-entered the car after the shooting, and he subsequently chose to continue driving the getaway vehicle, thereby ratifying the armed robbery.
(c) No, because he did not have advance knowledge that a firearm would be used at a time when he had a meaningful opportunity to walk away from the criminal enterprise. <!-- correct -->
(d) No, because his participation solely as a getaway driver is automatically classified as an accessory after the fact, which legally precludes any liability as an accomplice to the underlying robbery.
(e) Yes, because under the natural and probable consequences doctrine, the use of a firearm is categorically foreseeable in any drug-related theft or intimidation plan, establishing his liability for the weapon.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** The correct answer is (c). Under the *Rosemond* standard for federal armed offenses, an accomplice must have advance knowledge of the full scope of the crime (including firearm use) while still having a meaningful opportunity to walk away. Trey only learned of the gun afterward. (a) is wrong because actus reus alone is insufficient; Trey lacked the specific mens rea regarding the firearm. (b) is wrong because learning of the gun *after* the shooting means Trey had no meaningful opportunity to withdraw. (d) is wrong because a getaway driver can be an accomplice if they possess advance knowledge. (e) is wrong because accomplice liability for the target offense requires purpose/advance knowledge, not merely foreseeability.
