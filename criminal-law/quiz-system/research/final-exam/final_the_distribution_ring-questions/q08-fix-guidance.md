## Issue 1 — grounding

**Q8.** Ian is charged with felony murder for the guard's death. Which of the following is the most accurate statement?

(a) Ian is guilty of felony murder. The guard's death occurred during the commission of a dangerous felony, which triggers the rule regardless of whether Ian acted with malice. <!-- correct -->
(b) Ian is not guilty of felony murder. He shot the guard intentionally after dropping his bat, meaning he must be charged exclusively with premeditated first-degree murder instead.
(c) Ian is guilty of felony murder. The guard was an active participant in a rival drug conspiracy, which automatically triggers the doctrine in any territorial dispute.
(d) Ian is not guilty of felony murder. The guard drew his weapon first, which acts as an intervening cause that breaks the causal chain required for the rule.
(e) Ian is guilty of felony murder. He secretly brought a concealed handgun to the scene, which automatically elevates any underlying property crime to a capital offense.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** The felony murder rule holds a defendant strictly liable for any death caused during the commission or attempted commission of a qualifying dangerous felony, such as robbery. (b) is wrong because felony murder acts as an alternative pathway to first-degree murder without requiring proof of premeditation. (c) is wrong because the victim's status as a rival criminal does not automatically trigger the doctrine; the underlying felony does. (d) is wrong because a victim's lawful attempt to defend property does not break the causal chain of felony murder. (e) is wrong because the robbery itself is the qualifying felony, regardless of whether a hidden handgun elevates the property crime charge.
