**Q2.** Assume George, Helen, and Ian are charged with conspiracy to defend the turf. Have they satisfied the agreement element?

(a) Yes, they successfully formed a conspiracy. Their coordinated silent nodding and taking the money from the table is sufficient to infer a mutual understanding to defend the turf. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, they successfully formed a conspiracy. Felix's explicit statement about defending the turf automatically binds all listeners into a conspiratorial agreement regardless of their reaction.
(c) No, they did not form a conspiracy. An enforceable agreement requires an explicit verbal commitment from each participant before criminal liability can formally attach.
(d) No, they did not form a conspiracy. Mere presence at a meeting where crimes are discussed does not constitute participation without a signed contract or written pledge.
(e) Yes, they successfully formed a conspiracy. A conspiracy to commit violent acts is legally presumed whenever three or more individuals gather with a known crew leader.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** A conspiracy agreement can be inferred from coordinated conduct that lacks a plausible innocent explanation, such as silently nodding and accepting payment to defend turf (*Alvarez*). (b) is wrong because unilateral statements do not bind silent listeners without some reciprocal indication of assent. (c) is wrong because courts routinely infer agreements from conduct; explicit verbal commitments are not required. (d) is wrong because taking the money goes beyond mere presence and establishes active participation. (e) is wrong because there is no automatic legal presumption of conspiracy based solely on gathering with a crew leader.
