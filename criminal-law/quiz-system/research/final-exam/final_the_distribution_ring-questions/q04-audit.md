**Q4.** The prosecution charges George with attempted murder. Compare George's liability under the dangerous proximity test and the substantial step test.

(a) George is guilty under the MPC substantial step test but likely not guilty under the dangerous proximity test, because he was still searching for a victim. <!-- correct -->
(b) George is guilty under the dangerous proximity test, because acquiring the handgun and driving three blocks satisfied the requirement of coming dangerously close.
(c) George is not guilty under either test, because he had not yet spotted a rival lookout, making his actions mere preparation under both standards.
(d) George is guilty under both tests, because his specific intent to kill a rival lookout overrides the standard actus reus requirement for attempted murder.
(e) George is not guilty under the substantial step test, because driving with a broken taillight was not strongly corroborative of his stated criminal purpose.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under the MPC substantial step test, cruising to find a victim is "searching for the contemplated victim" and satisfies the actus reus. Under the common law dangerous proximity test, George had not come dangerously close to completion because he had not found a target yet (analogous to Rizzo). (b) is wrong because driving around without finding the victim fails the dangerous proximity test. (c) is wrong because under the MPC, searching is a substantial step, not mere preparation. (d) is wrong because specific intent does not waive the actus reus element. (e) is wrong because searching for a victim with a loaded gun strongly corroborates his intent, regardless of the taillight.
