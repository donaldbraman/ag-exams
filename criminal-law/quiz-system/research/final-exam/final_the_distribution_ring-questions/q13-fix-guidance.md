**Q13.** Ten minutes later, Helen lets Ian hide in her apartment and gives him a change of clothes. What is her liability for this specific conduct?

(a) She is guilty of full accomplice liability. Providing material support and clothing to the shooter after the fact retroactively establishes her intent to promote the underlying murder.
(b) She is guilty as an accessory after the fact. She provided tangible aid to help Ian evade capture by the police after the substantive crime was already completed. <!-- correct -->
(c) She is not guilty of any additional crime. Her prior role as a lookout during the robbery legally merges with any subsequent harboring offenses committed later that night.
(d) She is guilty of conspiracy to commit murder. Providing a change of clothing conclusively demonstrates a retroactive agreement to the killing of the rival guard.
(e) She is not guilty of any harboring offense. Letting someone into an apartment is a purely passive act that lacks the affirmative criminal intent required for post-crime liability.

**Answer:** (b)

**Explanation:** An accessory after the fact is someone who knowingly provides aid to a felon after the crime is complete to help them evade capture. Giving Ian a change of clothes and a place to hide satisfies these elements. (a) is wrong because post-crime assistance does not retroactively establish accomplice liability for the completed murder. (c) is wrong because the prior role as a lookout is a separate actus reus from the subsequent harboring offense; they do not merge. (d) is wrong because providing clothing after a crime does not establish a prior agreement to commit the killing. (e) is wrong because knowingly hiding a fugitive is an affirmative act of obstruction, not passive presence.
