**Q2.** The prosecution charges George, Helen, and Ian with conspiracy to defend the drug turf by unlawful means.

(a) Guilty, because their silent nods and acceptance of the money constitute coordinated conduct from which a mutual understanding can be inferred. <!-- correct -->
(b) Not guilty, because they did not explicitly state their verbal assent to Felix's proposed plan to defend the turf by any means.
(c) Not guilty, because the unilateral approach to conspiracy requires at least one party to be a government agent or informant.
(d) Guilty, because mere presence at a meeting where a crime is proposed automatically establishes a conspiratorial agreement under the MPC.
(e) Guilty, because Wharton's Rule mandates that any crime involving multiple participants be charged as a conspiracy regardless of explicit agreement.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Conspiratorial agreement is rarely proven by explicit words; it can be inferred from coordinated conduct with no plausible innocent explanation. Nodding and taking the money in response to Felix's proposal establishes mutual understanding. (b) is wrong because explicit verbal assent is not required. (c) is wrong because the unilateral approach applies when one party feigns agreement, but here there is actual multilateral agreement. (d) is wrong because mere presence without coordinated conduct is insufficient. (e) is wrong because Wharton's Rule applies to inherently bilateral crimes (like dueling), not a turf defense conspiracy.
