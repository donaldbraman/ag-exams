**Q13.** Assume Ian committed the robbery and homicide. The prosecution charges Helen as an accessory after the fact for letting Ian into her apartment and giving him clothes.

(a) Guilty, because she provided assistance to Ian with the intent to help him avoid capture after the robbery and homicide were complete. <!-- correct -->
(b) Guilty, because her prior role as a lookout automatically establishes her continuing liability as an accessory after the fact for the escape.
(c) Not guilty, because providing a change of clothes does not satisfy the substantial step requirement for actionable post-crime assistance.
(d) Not guilty, because she and Ian were part of the same original conspiracy, precluding a separate accessory charge for the same transaction.
(e) Not guilty, because she did not use force or deception against law enforcement to actively shield Ian from physical discovery.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Accessory after the fact is a separate, lesser offense for someone who provides assistance to a principal with the intent to help them avoid capture after the target crime is fully completed. Helen giving Ian shelter and a change of clothes satisfies this. (b) is wrong because prior complicity in the target offense does not automatically create liability for the separate offense of post-crime assistance. (c) is wrong because providing clothing and shelter is the classic form of actionable post-crime assistance. (d) is wrong because being in an original conspiracy does not legally preclude an accessory after the fact charge for subsequent cover-up efforts. (e) is wrong because force or deception against law enforcement are not required elements.
