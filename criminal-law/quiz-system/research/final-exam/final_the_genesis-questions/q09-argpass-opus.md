**Q9.** Avon is charged with possessing the drug sales ledger and the keys to the restricted manufacturing section. The items were found in a locked box in Avon's office. Can the prosecution establish that Avon possessed these items?

(a) Yes, because finding the items in a locked box inside a CEO's personal office establishes his awareness and his exclusive power and intent to control them. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because a corporate officer is presumed by law to have actual physical possession of any physical object located anywhere within the corporate headquarters building.
(c) No, because placing the items inside a locked storage box proves that Avon intended to legally abandon them and relinquish his dominion and ongoing control.
(d) No, because legal constructive possession cannot be established unless the defendant is physically touching the items at the exact moment the police warrant is executed.
(e) No, because Marlowe was the official regional manager of the warehouse, meaning Marlowe legally retained exclusive constructive possession over the facility's keys and the ledger.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. Constructive possession requires the power and intent to exercise dominion and control over an object. Items found in a locked box inside a defendant's personal office strongly support the inference that the defendant was aware of them and retained exclusive control. (b) is wrong because corporate officers are not legally presumed to physically possess every object in a headquarters; the state must prove individual dominion and control. (c) is wrong because locking items in a secure box demonstrates an intent to preserve control, not abandon them. (d) is wrong because constructive possession is specifically defined as having control without actual, concurrent physical touching. (e) is wrong because multiple people can share constructive possession, and keeping the items in Avon's office establishes his independent control regardless of Marlowe's status.
