**Q10.** Can Avon be charged with possession of the 5 kilograms of cocaine found in the warehouse lockbox?

(a) Guilty, because as CEO, he holds strict liability for all physical items stored on the corporate premises.
(b) Guilty, because his role and directives show he had the power and intent to exercise control over the drugs. <!-- correct -->
(c) Not guilty, because he was not physically present at the warehouse when the cocaine was discovered by investigators.
(d) Not guilty, because the drugs were secured inside a locked box that he did not personally possess the key for.
(e) Not guilty, because the drugs belonged to the corporation as an entity rather than to Avon as an individual.

**Answer:** (b)

**Explanation:** (b) is correct because constructive possession requires the power and intent to exercise dominion and control over an object. Avon's status as syndicate boss and his text message ordering the courier to pick up the drugs demonstrate his dominion and control. (a) fails because possession requires mens rea (knowledge/intent), not strict liability. (c) fails because constructive possession was developed specifically to assign liability when the defendant is not physically present. (d) fails because direct physical access (a key) is not required if he controls the people who do have access. (e) fails because individuals can constructively possess illicit items hidden within corporate property.
