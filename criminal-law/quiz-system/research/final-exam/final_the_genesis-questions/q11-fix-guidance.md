**Q11.** Are Avon and Marlowe guilty of conspiracy to distribute narcotics?

(a) Not guilty, because an agreement must be formally documented in writing to satisfy the conspiracy actus reus.
(b) Not guilty, because Marlowe only explicitly agreed to set up logistics, not to distribute the narcotics themselves.
(c) Guilty, because Avon's instruction and Marlowe's compliance establish a bilateral agreement to commit the illicit distribution target offense. <!-- correct -->
(d) Guilty, because Avon unilaterally intended to distribute narcotics, which is sufficient under the common law conspiracy standard.
(e) Guilty, because conspiracy is a strict liability offense when it involves inherently dangerous activities like drug trafficking.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** (c) is correct because conspiracy requires an agreement to commit a crime. Avon's directive to "set up the drug operation... off the books" and Marlowe's execution of that order establish the requisite meeting of the minds and bilateral agreement to commit the target offense. (a) fails because tacit or implied verbal agreements are fully sufficient for conspiracy. (b) fails because agreeing to handle the logistics of a drug operation constitutes agreeing to participate in the distribution conspiracy. (d) fails because common law conspiracy requires a bilateral agreement, not unilateral intent. (e) fails because conspiracy is a specific intent crime, never strict liability.
