**Q13.** If the jurisdiction rejects the Pinkerton doctrine, can Avon be convicted as an accomplice to Marlowe's federal chemical storage felony?

(a) Guilty, because his statement "I don't care how you process it" demonstrates a conscious object to store illegal chemicals.
(b) Guilty, because any degree of criminal negligence regarding the chemicals is sufficient to establish accomplice liability under common law.
(c) Not guilty, because his indifference to the processing methods establishes mere knowledge rather than the specific purpose to facilitate the offense. <!-- correct -->
(d) Not guilty, because an employer can never be held as an accomplice to an employee's independent criminal actions.
(e) Not guilty, because the chemical storage offense was not successfully completed due to the ensuing fire and warehouse destruction.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** (c) is correct because accomplice liability generally requires the specific purpose (conscious object) to facilitate the underlying crime. Avon's statement ("I don't care how you process it") shows indifference or knowledge of a risk, not a purpose to commit a federal chemical storage violation. (a) fails because "I don't care" explicitly negates a conscious object/purpose. (b) fails because negligence is insufficient for accomplice liability, which requires purpose. (d) fails factually; employers are routinely held as accomplices if they purposefully facilitate the crime. (e) fails because the crime of storing the chemicals without a permit was completed the moment they were stored.
