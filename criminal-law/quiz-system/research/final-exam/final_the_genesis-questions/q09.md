**Q9.** The prosecution charges Avon under RICO. Do Barksdale Logistics and the illicit distribution network satisfy the "enterprise" element?

(a) Yes, because the combination of the legal corporation and the illicit distribution network constitutes an ongoing organization. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because any registered corporation automatically satisfies the enterprise requirement regardless of its specific operational activities.
(c) No, because a RICO enterprise must be an exclusively illegal organization entirely distinct from any legitimate business.
(d) No, because the government must prove the enterprise successfully corrupted public officials to qualify under the statute.
(e) No, because an individual acting as a CEO cannot also be charged as a participant in the enterprise.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because a RICO "enterprise" includes any individual, partnership, corporation, or group of individuals associated in fact. Blending a legitimate corporate front (Barksdale Logistics) with an illicit distribution network perfectly satisfies the requirement of an ongoing organization functioning as a continuing unit. (b) fails because the corporation must be involved in a pattern of racketeering activity; merely being registered is insufficient. (c) fails because RICO expressly applies to legitimate businesses used for illicit purposes. (d) fails because public corruption is not an element of a RICO enterprise. (e) fails because an individual (Avon) can be the "person" employed by or associated with the corporate "enterprise."
