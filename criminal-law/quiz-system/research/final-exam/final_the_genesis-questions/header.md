# Scenario: Barksdale Logistics

### Facts

1. Avon is the CEO of "Barksdale Logistics Corp.", a legally registered commercial storage corporation.
2. Avon and his regional manager, Marlowe, enter an agreement to lease an abandoned warehouse under Barksdale Logistics Corp.'s name as an overflow storage facility. They secretly restrict access to one section to manufacture illicit drugs.
3. Avon delegates all day-to-day management and safety monitoring of the warehouse to Marlowe and never visits the site.
4. A city ordinance makes it a strict-liability misdemeanor to operate an unregistered storage facility. Additionally, a federal statute imposes a 5-year felony sentence on anyone who "knowingly stores hazardous chemicals without a permit."
5. Marlowe stores "Acetone-FX" in the warehouse without a permit. He genuinely but mistakenly believes Acetone-FX is a harmless, non-flammable water-based cleaner, completely unaware of its highly volatile chemical properties.
6. Stringer, an independent chemical supplier, sells the Acetone-FX to Marlowe. Stringer knows Marlowe uses the warehouse for illicit purposes, but Stringer charges normal market rates and has no stake in Marlowe's venture.
7. Marlowe hires Wallace, an hourly worker, to mix the Acetone-FX. Wallace has a documented seizure disorder. His physician recently explicitly warned him that inhaling strong chemical fumes will trigger immediate, severe involuntary convulsions. Desperate for money, Wallace agrees to do the mixing anyway.
8. While Wallace is mixing the Acetone-FX in an open vat, he inhales the fumes. He immediately suffers a massive convulsion, losing consciousness. His arms thrash involuntarily, knocking the vat off the table.
9. The spilled Acetone-FX pools around a nearby space heater, instantly igniting a massive chemical fire that engulfs the warehouse.
10. Inspector Greggs, a city health inspector passing by the facility, runs inside to rescue anyone trapped and is killed by a collapsing roof beam.
11. Under the jurisdiction's criminal code, manufacturing illicit drugs is an enumerated inherently dangerous felony.
12. After the fire, investigators execute a search warrant at Barksdale Logistics corporate headquarters. They find a locked box in Avon's office containing the keys to the restricted manufacturing section of the warehouse and a ledger of drug sales.

### Stem 1: Immediate Actors

The fire at the unregistered warehouse left Inspector Greggs dead. Before we look at the corporate hierarchy, I need you to evaluate the immediate liability of the guys on the ground—Wallace, Marlowe, and the supplier Stringer—for the fire, the death, and the regulatory storage violations.
