**Q2.** Assume that, whether or not the initial fire is attributed to Wallace, he is charged with criminal homicide for Inspector Greggs's death. The prosecution argues that Wallace acted with the requisite mens rea. Which of the following is the most likely grading of Wallace's culpability?

(a) Depraved heart murder, because any chemical manufacturing automatically manifests an extreme indifference to the value of human life under modern homicide codes.
(b) Manslaughter, because his conscious decision to mix the chemicals despite a medical warning that doing so would trigger immediate convulsions constituted recklessness. <!-- correct -->
(c) Felony murder, because his agreement to mix the chemicals constituted a conspiracy, holding him strictly liable for all resulting deaths in the warehouse.
(d) Criminally negligent homicide, because a reasonable person would have realized the risk, but there is no evidence Wallace himself was subjectively aware of it.
(e) No criminal homicide, because a worker strictly following an employer's directions lacks the necessary specific intent required to cause a foreseeable death.

**Answer:** (b)

**Explanation:** (b) is correct. Manslaughter requires recklessness (a conscious disregard of a substantial and unjustifiable risk). Wallace consciously disregarded the explicit medical warning that inhaling fumes would cause convulsions while handling chemicals, satisfying the standard. (a) is wrong because depraved heart murder requires extreme indifference to human life, and merely risking a seizure does not automatically meet this higher threshold. (c) is wrong because conspiracy liability (*Pinkerton*) applies to co-conspirators, but Wallace merely agreed to mix chemicals, not to manufacture illicit drugs. (d) is wrong because Wallace was subjectively aware of the risk due to the explicit physician warning, elevating his culpability from negligence to recklessness. (e) is wrong because following orders is not a defense to homicide, and specific intent is not required for manslaughter.
