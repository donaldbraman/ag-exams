**Q11.** Federal prosecutors charge Avon Commercial Properties Inc. under RICO. Avon argues that the corporation cannot be a RICO enterprise because it holds legitimate board meetings and processes valid rent checks. Can the corporation serve as a RICO enterprise?

(a) Yes, because a legitimate legal entity can be a RICO enterprise when its assets or structure are utilized to shield an illicit network. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, but only if the prosecution proves that the corporation's board members explicitly voted to authorize the drug manufacturing.
(c) No, because a RICO enterprise must be an association-in-fact created exclusively for illegal purposes.
(d) No, because prosecuting a legally registered corporation under RICO violates the corporate charter protections of state law.
(e) No, because the corporation is the defendant, and under RICO, the defendant and the enterprise must be identical in all respects.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct. A RICO enterprise can be a perfectly legitimate legal entity (like a corporation) that is used as a vehicle or shield for a pattern of racketeering activity. The enterprise does not need to be exclusively criminal. (b) is incorrect; formal corporate authorization is not required to establish that the enterprise was used for racketeering. (c) is wrong because RICO explicitly covers legitimate legal entities, not just informal criminal associations-in-fact. (d) is false; federal RICO supersedes state corporate charter protections under the Supremacy Clause. (e) misstates the law; under § 1962(c), the defendant and the enterprise must actually be distinct, not identical.
