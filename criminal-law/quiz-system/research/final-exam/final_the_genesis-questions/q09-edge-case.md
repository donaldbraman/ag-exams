**Q9.** Stringer is charged as an accomplice to Marlowe's drug manufacturing. He sold Acetone-FX to Marlowe at standard market rates, knowing Marlowe would use it to make drugs. Is Stringer guilty as an accomplice under the traditional common law rule?

(a) Yes, because he provided material assistance by supplying a necessary chemical for the manufacturing process.
(b) Yes, because his knowledge of Marlowe's criminal purpose is sufficient to establish the mens rea for accomplice liability.
(c) No, because selling goods at standard market prices does not constitute an actus reus of aiding or abetting.
(d) No, because he lacked the purpose to facilitate the drug manufacturing, as he had no stake in the profits and charged standard rates. <!-- correct -->
(e) No, because he did not personally participate in the actual physical mixing of the chemicals at the warehouse.

**Answer:** (d)

**Explanation:** (d) is correct. Under the traditional common law rule, accomplice liability requires the specific intent (purpose) to facilitate the commission of the crime. Merely supplying goods at standard market rates with knowledge of their intended illegal use does not establish purpose. (a) is incorrect because it ignores the mens rea requirement entirely. (b) is wrong because knowledge alone is insufficient for accomplice liability; purpose is required. (c) is false because supplying materials certainly constitutes the actus reus of aiding; the failure is in the mens rea. (e) is incorrect because physical presence or personal participation in the final act is not required for an accomplice.
