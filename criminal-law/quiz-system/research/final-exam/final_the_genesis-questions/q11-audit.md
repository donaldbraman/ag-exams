**Q11.** Avon is charged with conspiracy to distribute narcotics. Does his interaction with Marlowe satisfy the agreement element?

(a) Yes, because Avon's instruction to set up the drug operation and Marlowe's subsequent actions constitute an agreement to commit the offense. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because any formal employer-employee relationship automatically satisfies the mutual agreement requirement for a criminal conspiracy under the common law.
(c) No, because Avon did not specify the exact methods Marlowe should use to process the narcotics at the commercial warehouse location.
(d) No, because Marlowe's purchase of hazardous processing chemicals was a unilateral act falling entirely outside the scope of the conspiracy agreement.
(e) No, because Avon and Marlowe did not explicitly state their agreement to the illicit plan in writing or in a recorded conversation.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** The agreement element of a conspiracy does not require a formal contract; it can be inferred from a concert of action. Avon explicitly directed Marlowe to "set up the drug operation," and Marlowe subsequently procured chemicals to do so (Facts 2 and 4). This exchange and the subsequent coordinated actions manifest a mutual agreement to commit the target offense of drug distribution. (b) is wrong because an employer-employee relationship does not automatically establish an agreement to commit a crime without mutual intent. (c) is wrong because conspirators do not need to agree on every specific detail or method to form a criminal agreement. (d) is wrong because procuring the chemicals was an overt act in furtherance of the overarching agreed-upon operation. (e) is wrong because an agreement can be implicit and need not be formalized in writing.
