**Q2.** Assume Wallace is charged with unintentional homicide for Greggs's death. Under the Model Penal Code, which of the following accurately describes the mental state analysis for determining his level of liability?

(a) He is guilty of manslaughter if he consciously disregarded a substantial risk of death, but only negligent homicide if he merely should have been aware of it. <!-- correct -->
(b) He is guilty of murder because any unintentional death resulting from the manufacture of illicit drugs automatically satisfies the extreme indifference to human life standard.
(c) He is guilty of manslaughter regardless of his actual awareness, because participating in an illicit drug operation imposes strict liability for any resulting physical harm.
(d) He is guilty of negligent homicide because his desperate financial situation provides a valid legal justification that mitigates his conscious disregard of the medical warning.
(e) He is not guilty of any homicide offense because the chemical fire was physically ignited by the space heater, acting as an independent superseding cause.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** (a) is correct because the MPC differentiates homicide grades based on actual awareness: consciously disregarding a substantial risk of death constitutes recklessness (manslaughter), while failing to perceive a risk one should have perceived constitutes negligence (negligent homicide). (b) is wrong because an unintentional death does not automatically satisfy the extreme indifference standard without evaluating the specific degree of risk and the defendant's mental state. (c) is wrong because the MPC rejects strict liability for homicide. (d) is wrong because financial desperation does not legally excuse risking the lives of others. (e) is wrong because the space heater was a passive condition, not an independent superseding cause that relieves him of liability.
