## Issue 1 — grounding

**Q7.** The State charges Arthur with illegal possession of the Solv-X drums found in Unit 4B. Arthur argues he cannot be convicted because he never visited the site or physically touched the drums. How will a court likely rule?

(a) Arthur is not guilty of possession because the voluntary act requirement mandates actual physical handling. Without direct contact, the actus reus for possession is completely absent.
(b) Arthur is guilty of constructive possession because he held a key to the unit and shared in the venture's profits. This establishes his power and intent to exercise dominion and control over the premises. <!-- correct -->
(c) Arthur is not guilty of possession because his explicit prohibition against flammable agents negates the necessary mens rea. He lacked knowledge of the specific contraband present.
(d) Arthur is guilty of actual possession because the actions of a co-conspirator are physically imputed to all other members. The *Pinkerton* doctrine eliminates the distinction between actual and constructive possession.
(e) Arthur is guilty of joint possession because modern penal codes impose strict liability on syndicate leaders. This liability extends to any illegal items stored within properties they indirectly finance.

**Answer:** (b)

**Explanation:** Option (b) is correct because physical contact is not required for possession. Constructive possession is established when a person has the power and intent to exercise dominion and control over an item, either directly or through others. Holding a key and managing the venture satisfies this standard. Option (a) is incorrect because the law universally recognizes constructive possession as satisfying the actus reus requirement. Option (c) is incorrect because, while Arthur forbade flammable agents, he still constructively possessed the inventory of the lab he co-managed, even if he mistook its exact chemical nature. Option (d) is incorrect because *Pinkerton* imputes substantive offenses, but the legal distinction between actual and constructive possession remains relevant. Option (e) is incorrect because there is no blanket strict liability possession rule for syndicate leaders.

**Grounding:** Constructive possession doctrine (power and intent to exercise dominion and control)
