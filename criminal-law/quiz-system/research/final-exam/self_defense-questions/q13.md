**Q13.** The District Attorney justifies the 25-to-life sentence as necessary "to express society's condemnation of vigilante violence." This justification is most closely aligned with which theory of punishment?

(a) Specific deterrence, because the lengthy sentence will physically prevent Alex from committing future acts of vigilante violence against other neighbors in his residential community.
(b) Rehabilitation, because the sentence provides a highly structured environment for Alex to correct the behavioral deficits and anger management issues that led to the shooting.
(c) The expressive theory, because the punishment serves as a public communicative act that clearly signals the community's denunciation of the severe moral violation. <!-- correct -->
(d) Restorative justice, because the lengthy sentence formally acknowledges the profound emotional harm inflicted upon the victim's immediate family and the broader neighborhood.
(e) The classical deterrence model, because it perfectly calculates the exact quantum of pain necessary to offset the subjective pleasure derived from the criminal act.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** The expressive theory of punishment, popularized by Joel Feinberg, views criminal sanctions not merely as deprivations, but as communicative acts through which society formally signals its moral condemnation and denunciation of the offense. The DA's explicitly stated goal to "express society's condemnation" (Fact 12) perfectly tracks this theoretical rationale. Option (a) is incorrect because specific deterrence aims to physically prevent the specific offender from reoffending, not to send a moral message to society. Option (b) is incorrect because rehabilitation focuses on treating and reforming the offender rather than expressing condemnation. Option (d) is incorrect because restorative justice focuses on healing relationships between the offender, victim, and community through accountability, rather than unilateral state condemnation. Option (e) is incorrect because classical deterrence is an economic calculus aimed at preventing general crime by manipulating incentives, not communicating moral outrage.
