# Scenario: Front Lawn Escalation

### Facts

1. Alex and his neighbor Blake are involved in a bitter property line dispute. Alex genuinely believes the patch of front lawn separating their houses belongs to him because the County Clerk recently provided him with an official, stamped plat map showing it on his side of the line (though the clerk's printout was legally erroneous).
2. Alex texts his friend Chris: "Let's grab baseball bats and go over to Blake's lawn to scare him into dropping the lawsuit." Chris replies: "I'm in, let's go." They grab bats and walk over.
3. Halfway there, Chris stops, drops his bat on the sidewalk, and says to Alex: "I'm going home. This is stupid, don't do anything crazy." Chris turns and walks away.
4. Chris walks home and goes to sleep, doing nothing else to stop Alex or warn authorities.
5. Alex continues alone, stepping onto the disputed lawn. Blake steps out of his house and yells, "Get off my property, you worthless piece of trash!"
6. Blake angrily reaches into his jacket pocket. Alex knows Blake previously told another neighbor, "I always carry a revolver to deal with trespassers."
7. Enraged by the insult and terrified that Blake is drawing a weapon, Alex wordlessly drops the baseball bat on the grass, instantly pulls his own permitted handgun, and shoots Blake once in the chest, intending to kill him. Alex had an unobstructed path to safely run back to the public street.
8. Alex sees Blake bleeding heavily on the lawn. Realizing Blake is unarmed (Blake had reached for a cell phone), Alex panics, runs back to his own house, and locks the door without calling 911.
9. Blake bleeds on the lawn for 45 minutes before a passing delivery driver calls emergency services.
10. At the hospital, Dr. Davis, the attending ER physician, administers the wrong blood type to Blake. Dr. Davis explicitly ignored a bright red allergy/blood-type warning bracelet on Blake's wrist because he was distracted looking at his phone to make personal plans.
11. Blake dies from the transfusion reaction, not the gunshot wound (which medical experts testify was easily survivable if treated correctly).

### Stem 1: Initial Charges

We are drafting the initial charges against Alex and Chris based on the front-lawn confrontation. First, analyze Chris's liability for the conspiracy and the shooting. Second, evaluate Alex's potential mistake-of-law defense and how his belief about the property line impacts his duty to retreat. Third, evaluate Alex's self-defense and mitigation claims (including provocation and EED) for the shooting itself. Assume traditional common law applies unless I specify the MPC or a specific state doctrine.
