**Q1.** Assume Chris is charged with conspiracy to commit assault. Under the Model Penal Code, is Chris guilty of the conspiracy charge?

(a) Guilty, because his statement to Alex and departure failed to thwart the success of the planned conspiracy. <!-- correct -->
(b) Guilty, because a completed conspiracy agreement can never be undone even if the target offense is thwarted.
(c) Not guilty, because his statement "I'm out" effectively communicated his withdrawal to his sole co-conspirator.
(d) Not guilty, because turning around and going home constituted an overt act of abandonment before the assault.
(e) Not guilty, because his departure objectively demonstrated a complete and voluntary renunciation of the criminal purpose.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under the MPC, the affirmative defense of renunciation for a conspiracy requires that the actor successfully thwart the commission of the target offense under circumstances manifesting a complete and voluntary renunciation of their criminal purpose. Because Chris only walked away and did nothing to stop Alex (Fact 3), he failed to thwart the assault and remains guilty of the conspiracy itself. Option (b) is incorrect because the MPC expressly provides a renunciation defense if the actor thwarts the crime. Option (c) is incorrect because mere communication of withdrawal cuts off future liability for co-conspirator crimes but does not erase the underlying conspiracy charge. Option (d) is incorrect because an overt act of abandonment without thwarting is insufficient under the MPC. Option (e) is incorrect because voluntary departure alone does not satisfy the MPC's thwarting requirement.
