## Scenario: Institutional Corruption

### Facts

1. Police officers observe Silas driving with a cracked taillight. Internal police communications later reveal the officers stopped Silas purely on an unsupported hunch of drug activity, using the traffic violation solely as a pretext.
2. Silas paid Elena $500 for a simple three-mile ride to a warehouse, a payment well above the market rate. Elena knew the cargo Silas was carrying consisted of illegal chemicals, but she did not share his purpose.
3. When police try to stop them, Silas accelerates to 90 mph, weaving erratically through a crowded residential neighborhood. He loses control of his vehicle, jumps the curb, and strikes a pedestrian, causing immediate fatal injuries. Evading police at high speeds is specifically designated as a felony under state law.
4. Silas abandons his vehicle and flees on foot to a nearby suspected stash house. Pursuing officers breach the front door and find Silas in the living room. He had just poured gasoline over 50 kilograms of packaged cocaine and was holding a lighter in his trembling hand. Officers tackle him before he can ignite it. In Silas's pocket, they find the only key to the locked bedroom where more drugs are stored.
5. During pretrial motions, defense attorney Elias Thorne files a selective prosecution discovery motion, presenting leaked statistical data proving that the DA's charging policies have a 95% disparate impact on minority defendants.
6. During jury selection, DA Claire Miller uses her peremptory challenges to strike all five Black prospective jurors. When Thorne raises a challenge, Miller states for the record: "These jurors reside in neighborhoods with high drug-crime rates, making them inherently biased against police testimony."
7. During his closing argument, Thorne urges the jury to acquit Silas of all charges to send a democratic protest message against a racially biased system, explicitly relying on Paul Butler's framework despite Silas being charged with a violent felony.
8. The judge instructs the jury that "If you acquit contrary to the evidence, you will be held in contempt of court and sent to jail."

### Stem 1: The Trial of Silas

Silas's trial for drug and homicide offenses is underway, testing the limits of prosecutorial discretion and jury authority.
