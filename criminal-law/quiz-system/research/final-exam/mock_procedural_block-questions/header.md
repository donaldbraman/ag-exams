# Scenario: Cartel Crossroads

### Facts

1. Leo Vargas is a high-level manager for the Silver Cartel, an ongoing, structured organization with a formal hierarchy. The investigation established that Leo and his subordinate, Sal, expressly agreed to transport 50 kilograms of fentanyl.
2. Leo hired Mike, an independent mechanic, to build a hidden compartment in Sal's car. Mike actually believed the compartment was for smuggling untaxed cigarettes, not fentanyl, and he charged his standard flat rate with no stake in the cartel's success.
3. Leo retained the secondary keys to the vehicle and GPS tracking access. When dispatching Sal, Leo told him: "Take the shipment straight to the safehouse. No guns, no violence. But if you fail to deliver this today, your family will pay the price tomorrow."
4. En route, Sal was pulled over by Officer Brady, who was driving an unmarked car and wearing plainclothes. Internal police communications revealed Brady initiated the stop because Sal was a young Hispanic male driving a high-end vehicle in a predominantly white neighborhood. However, Brady used Sal's failure to signal a lane change as the objective legal justification.
5. Sal fires at the plainclothes officer whom he believes is a hitman; Victor was walking his dog on the crowded sidewalk directly behind the officer.
6. Following Sal's arrest, DA Paula offered him a standard five-year plea deal for drug transport. When Sal refused, Paula threatened to use overlapping state statutes to charge him with a specialized aggravated trafficking offense carrying a mandatory 25-year sentence, which she had probable cause to support.
7. To protect his client Leo from Sal's potential cooperation, defense attorney Frank Felson met with DA Paula and offered her a lucrative partnership at his law firm if she intentionally derailed Sal's trial.
8. Accepting the law firm offer, DA Paula acted during the judicial phase of Sal's trial preparation, instructing the police evidence custodian to "accidentally incinerate" the dashcam footage from Officer Brady's cruiser. Paula later claimed absolute civil immunity for this act.
9. During jury selection for Sal's trial, DA Paula used peremptory challenges to strike all six Hispanic prospective jurors. When challenged by the judge, Paula stated, "I struck them because they expressed skepticism of police testimony," though the record showed three of them never spoke.
10. To further protect Leo from underlying state drug charges, Frank met with State Senator Vance and asked Vance to sponsor a bill rewriting the state's drug trafficking code to exclude fentanyl analog mixtures.
11. Senator Vance, whose legislative office receives over $10,000 in federal funding annually, successfully shepherded the bill into law. One month later, Frank sent a $25,000 check to Vance's consulting firm; notably, there had been no prior agreement, understanding, or promise of payment when Frank initially met with the Senator.
12. At the conclusion of Sal's trial, the Judge instructed the jury: "There is no such thing as valid jury nullification. If you acquit the defendant contrary to the evidence, you will violate your oath and the law."

### Stem 1: The Drug Run and Shootout

Assess the substantive criminal liability arising from the cartel's drug transport, Mike's involvement, and Sal's shootout with the plainclothes officer.
