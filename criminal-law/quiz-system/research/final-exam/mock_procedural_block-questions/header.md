# Scenario: Institutional Corruption

### Facts

1. Police officers observe Silas driving a vehicle with a cracked taillight. Internal police communications later reveal the officers stopped Silas purely on an unsupported hunch of drug activity, using the traffic violation solely as a pretext.
2. Instead of pulling over, Silas accelerates to 90 mph, weaving erratically through a crowded residential neighborhood. He loses control of his vehicle, jumps the curb, and strikes a pedestrian, causing immediate fatal injuries. Evading police at high speeds is specifically designated as a felony under state law.
3. Earlier that day, Silas had paid Elena $500 for a simple three-mile ride to a warehouse. The payment was well above the market rate, and Elena knew the cargo consisted of illegal chemicals, though she merely provided the transportation.
4. After the crash, Silas abandons his vehicle and flees on foot to a nearby suspected stash house. Pursuing officers breach the front door and find Silas in the living room. He had just poured gasoline over 50 kilograms of packaged cocaine and was holding a lighter in his trembling hand. Officers tackle him before he can ignite it. In Silas's pocket, officers find the only key to the locked bedroom where the drugs had been stored.
5. During jury selection for Silas's trial, District Attorney Claire Miller uses her peremptory challenges to strike all five Black prospective jurors. When defense attorney Elias Thorne raises a challenge, Miller states for the record: "These jurors reside in neighborhoods with high drug-crime rates, making them inherently biased against police testimony."
6. During pretrial motions, Thorne presents leaked statistical data proving that the DA's charging policies have a 95% disparate impact on minority defendants.
7. In his closing argument, Thorne explicitly urges the jury to acquit Silas of all charges to send a democratic protest message against a racially biased system, arguing for race-conscious jury nullification based on Paul Butler's framework, despite Silas being charged with a violent felony murder.
8. Subpoenaed digital logs reveal that during trial preparation, DA Miller discovered a wiretap recording indicating the cocaine actually belonged to a rival syndicate. To protect her conviction rate against Silas, Miller intentionally deleted the recording from the evidentiary database.
9. Encrypted texts reveal that prior to trial, Silas and Thorne formed an explicit agreement to use cartel funds to illegally obstruct the legal proceedings.
10. Surveillance footage confirms Thorne met with State Senator Marcus Vance. They explicitly agreed Vance would sponsor the "Evidence Integrity Act" to retroactively exclude stash-house evidence. In exchange, Thorne delivered $20,000 in cash, which he characterized as a "campaign contribution," paid three days before the bill's introduction.
11. Realizing the legislation might stall, Thorne independently paid trial judge Mercer $10,000 to suppress key evidence. Silas was generally aware Thorne was using "aggressive tactics" but did not know Thorne planned to bribe the judge.
12. Financial forensic audits establish that Thorne's law firm has operated for a decade as an ongoing, structured organization. The firm's primary, coordinated purpose has been facilitating bribes, witness tampering, and money laundering for multiple cartel lieutenants.
13. Following massive media coverage of the corruption, the city council debates decriminalizing minor drug offenses. Transcripts of the debate show that highly numerate council members who previously opposed decriminalization used their advanced statistical skills to selectively reinterpret the crime data, concluding the exact opposite of what the statisticians' report explicitly stated.

### Stem 1: Trial of Silas

The criminal trial of Silas begins, focusing on the traffic stop, the pedestrian death, the drug charges, and the courtroom maneuvers by both the prosecution and the defense.

### Stem 2: Special Counsel Investigation

Now that Silas is convicted, the State Attorney General has appointed us as Special Counsel to investigate Thorne's pretrial maneuvers and DA Miller's conduct. Review the investigation files to evaluate the liability of the conspirators, the extent of institutional corruption, and the subsequent policy fallout.
