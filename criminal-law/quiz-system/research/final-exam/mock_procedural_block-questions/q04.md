**Q4.** Assume that Elena is charged as an accomplice to Silas's drug possession because she drove him to the warehouse knowing he was transporting illegal chemicals, for an above-market fee. Under the traditional rule for accomplice liability, is Elena guilty?

(a) Not guilty, because her mere knowledge of his criminal activity is insufficient; she must have acted with the specific purpose of facilitating the crime. <!-- correct -->
(b) Guilty, because she provided material assistance to Silas while having actual knowledge of his illegal purpose and the chemical cargo.
(c) Guilty, because accepting an above-market fee legally transforms her into a principal in the first degree for any subsequent criminal acts.
(d) Not guilty, because she did not personally handle or transport the illegal chemicals during the drive to the targeted warehouse location.
(e) Not guilty, because driving a vehicle is an inherently lawful act that cannot ever form the basis of criminal accomplice liability.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under traditional accomplice liability, a defendant must act with the purpose of promoting or facilitating the offense; mere knowledge that assistance will be used for a crime is insufficient. (b) is incorrect because it applies a knowledge standard, which the traditional common law rule rejects for accomplice liability. (c) is incorrect because accepting a fee might show motive or intent, but does not magically transform an accomplice into a principal in the first degree. (d) is incorrect because accomplice liability explicitly holds actors responsible for the principal's physical acts. (e) is incorrect because facially lawful acts (like driving) can form the actus reus of accomplice liability if done with the requisite purpose.
