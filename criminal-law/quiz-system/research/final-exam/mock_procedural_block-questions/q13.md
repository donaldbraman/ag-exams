**Q13.** Assume Senator Vance is federally charged under 18 U.S.C. § 666 regarding the $20,000 cash payment from Thorne. Under *Snyder v. United States*, how should the payment be classified?

(a) It is a bribe, because the payment was agreed to and made before Vance sponsored the legislation, demonstrating a corrupt exchange to influence a future official act. <!-- correct -->
(b) It is a bribe, because any exchange of cash between a private defense lawyer and a public politician is classified as a per se bribe under federal law.
(c) It is a gratuity, because Thorne explicitly characterized the payment as a "campaign contribution" rather than a direct personal payment for political services.
(d) It is a gratuity, because Senator Vance had not yet successfully passed the legislation when he received the money from the defense attorney.
(e) It is a legal campaign contribution, because the Supreme Court explicitly protects cash payments to state legislators from federal corruption prosecution.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under *Snyder v. United States*, § 666 criminalizes bribes (payments agreed to or made before an official act to influence it) but not gratuities (payments made after an act as a reward). Because Thorne paid Vance before the bill's introduction to secure its sponsorship, the payment functions as a corrupt bribe. (b) is incorrect because there is no per se rule making all cash exchanges bribes; timing and corrupt intent are required. (c) is incorrect because a corrupt pre-act payment cannot be immunized merely by slapping the label "campaign contribution" on it. (d) is incorrect because bribery only requires attempting to influence an official act; the ultimate success of the legislation is irrelevant. (e) is incorrect because while federalism limits the scope of federal corruption laws (e.g., excluding gratuities), it does not protect state legislators who engage in explicit quid pro quo bribery.
