**Q10.** The federal government discovers that DA Paula accepted a lucrative law firm partnership from Frank in exchange for intentionally derailing Sal's trial. She is subsequently indicted under 18 U.S.C. § 1346. Is DA Paula guilty of honest-services fraud for accepting the law firm partnership?

(a) Yes, because accepting a lucrative partnership in exchange for intentionally derailing a trial is a core bribery scheme properly criminalized under *Skilling*. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because any undisclosed conflict of interest held by a public prosecutor automatically satisfies the statutory elements of federal honest-services fraud.
(c) No, because a partnership offer constitutes intangible professional advancement rather than the tangible "money or property" required by the mail fraud statute.
(d) No, because *Skilling* limited honest-services fraud exclusively to kickback schemes involving government contracts, which are not present in this criminal scenario.
(e) No, because the legal profession is regulated by state ethical standards, completely exempting attorneys from federal criminal prosecution for trial misconduct.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under *Skilling v. United States*, the federal honest-services fraud statute (18 U.S.C. § 1346) is strictly limited to schemes involving bribes and kickbacks. Accepting a lucrative partnership in exchange for an official act (derailing a trial) constitutes a core bribery scheme falling squarely within the statute's scope. (b) is wrong because *Skilling* expressly held that undisclosed conflicts of interest do not satisfy the statute. (c) is incorrect because the honest-services fraud statute was specifically enacted to criminalize the deprivation of the intangible right to honest services, bypassing the "money or property" requirement. (d) fails because *Skilling* covers any bribe or kickback involving a public official, not just government contracts. (e) is wrong because state ethical regulation does not exempt attorneys or prosecutors from federal criminal bribery and fraud statutes.
