## Issue 1 — audit

**Q1.** The prosecution charges Mike as an accomplice to the cartel's transportation of 50 kilograms of fentanyl. Mike argues that he was simply operating his independent mechanic business and was unaware of the specific drug involved. Is Mike guilty as an accomplice to the transportation of fentanyl?

(a) Yes, because his knowledge that the compartment would be used for illicit smuggling satisfies the mental state for accomplice liability.
(b) Yes, because constructing a hidden compartment is an inherently criminal act that implies purpose to aid any subsequent drug offense.
(c) No, because charging his standard flat rate and lacking knowledge of the fentanyl demonstrate he did not have the purpose to aid that crime. <!-- correct -->
(d) No, because independent contractors cannot be held liable as accomplices unless they share directly in the profits of the criminal enterprise.
(e) No, because building the compartment was merely a preparatory act that did not directly cause the successful transportation of the drugs.

**Answer:** (c)

**Explanation:** Under *Lauria*, an independent contractor providing standard goods or services at a standard rate lacks the purpose required for accomplice liability unless they have a stake in the venture, which Mike did not. Furthermore, because Mike believed he was facilitating cigarette smuggling, he made a mistake of fact regarding the transportation of fentanyl, defeating the required mens rea for the target offense. (a) fails because knowledge alone without a stake is insufficient for accomplice liability when providing standard services. (b) fails because building a compartment does not automatically prove purpose to aid this specific drug offense. (d) is wrong because formal induction into the enterprise or profit-sharing is not legally required for accomplice liability if purpose exists. (e) is wrong because accomplice liability requires aiding the offense, not necessarily committing a substantial step, which is an attempt element.

**Tags:** chapters: [10, 18], topics: [accomplice-purpose-vs-knowledge, mistake-of-fact], difficulty: medium, cognitive: application

**Grounding:** Chapter 18 (Accomplice Liability) and Chapter 10 (Mistake of Fact)
