**Q3.** Does the Silver Cartel satisfy the "enterprise" element under RICO?

(a) It constitutes an enterprise because it is an ongoing organization functioning as a continuing unit with a formal hierarchy. <!-- correct -->
(b) It constitutes an enterprise because any agreement to transport illegal drugs automatically satisfies the enterprise requirement.
(c) It does not constitute an enterprise because there is no evidence of a legally chartered corporate entity being utilized.
(d) It does not constitute an enterprise because the underlying acts were strictly related to drug trafficking rather than extortion.
(e) It does not constitute an enterprise because Sal's eventual detour to shoot an officer broke the continuing unit requirement.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under *United States v. Boyle*, an association-in-fact enterprise requires a purpose, relationships among those associated with the enterprise, and longevity sufficient to permit these associates to pursue the enterprise's purpose. The Silver Cartel is an ongoing, structured organization functioning as a continuing unit with a formal hierarchy, satisfying this structural requirement. (b) is wrong because a mere agreement to transport drugs is a conspiracy, and *Boyle* requires an enterprise structure distinct from the pattern of racketeering activity itself. (c) is wrong because RICO expressly covers illegitimate associations-in-fact, not just chartered corporations. (d) is wrong because drug trafficking is a predicate racketeering activity under RICO. (e) is wrong because individual deviations by members do not dissolve the overarching enterprise structure.
