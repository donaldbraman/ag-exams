**Q15.** Assume the government indicts Thorne's law firm under the Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations (RICO) Act. Does the law firm qualify as a RICO "enterprise"?

(a) Yes, because the law firm functions as an ongoing, structured organization with a coordinated purpose of facilitating illegal activities over a substantial period. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because any business entity that generates more than ten thousand dollars in annual revenue automatically qualifies as a criminal enterprise under RICO.
(c) Yes, because the law firm was established specifically to violate the Hobbs Act by extorting clients and laundering money for various criminal cartels.
(d) No, because RICO enterprises are strictly limited to informal associations-in-fact, and explicitly exclude legally incorporated entities like traditional law firms.
(e) No, because Thorne is a licensed attorney, and professional legal service firms are statutorily exempt from federal racketeering and enterprise liability.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** A RICO enterprise requires an ongoing organization with a structure and a coordinated purpose. Thorne's law firm operates as a structured organization coordinating bribes and money laundering, satisfying the enterprise element. (b) is incorrect because enterprise status depends on structure and coordinated activity, not an arbitrary revenue threshold. (c) is incorrect because a RICO enterprise does not need to be established *specifically* to violate the law; legitimate businesses operated in a corrupt manner qualify. (d) is incorrect because RICO explicitly covers legally incorporated entities (corporations, partnerships, law firms) as well as informal associations-in-fact. (e) is incorrect because there is no professional or statutory exemption shielding lawyers or law firms from RICO liability.
