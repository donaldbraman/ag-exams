**Q14.** Assume the prosecution seeks to hold Silas liable for Thorne's independent $10,000 bribe to Judge Mercer under the *Pinkerton* doctrine. Silas was generally aware Thorne used "aggressive tactics" but did not know Thorne planned to bribe the judge. Can Silas be held liable for the bribe?

(a) Yes, because the bribe was committed in furtherance of the obstruction conspiracy and was a reasonably foreseeable consequence of authorizing "aggressive tactics" with cartel funds. <!-- correct -->
(b) Yes, because under the *Pinkerton* doctrine, a conspirator is held strictly liable for every single crime committed by an associate during their natural lifetime.
(c) Yes, because Silas's prior physical presence at the drug stash house firmly established his permanent status as a principal in the first degree.
(d) No, because Silas completely lacked the specific, advance knowledge of Thorne's independent plan to offer a financial bribe to Judge Mercer.
(e) No, because the *Pinkerton* doctrine is exclusively restricted to violent felonies committed during a conspiracy, and does not apply to white-collar offenses like bribery.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** Under the *Pinkerton* doctrine, a conspirator is liable for all substantive crimes committed by co-conspirators if those crimes are in furtherance of the conspiracy and are reasonably foreseeable. Given the conspiracy to illegally obstruct the proceedings using cartel funds, bribing the judge was a foreseeable tactic. (b) is incorrect because *Pinkerton* liability is bounded by foreseeability and furtherance of the conspiracy, not strict lifetime liability. (c) is incorrect because his presence at the stash house relates to the drug charges, not the subsequent obstruction conspiracy. (d) is incorrect because *Pinkerton* specifically allows liability without advance knowledge or direct participation, provided the act is foreseeable. (e) is incorrect because *Pinkerton* applies to all foreseeable crimes within the conspiracy's scope, including non-violent white-collar offenses.
