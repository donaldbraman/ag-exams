**Q16.** Assume that during the city council debate, highly numerate council members selectively reinterpreted statistical crime data to support their prior opposition to decriminalization. According to the "numeracy paradox," why does this occur?

(a) Because individuals with higher analytical abilities are better equipped to selectively interpret data to protect their preexisting moral and political commitments. <!-- correct -->
(b) Because highly numerate individuals inherently lack the scientific curiosity required to understand and contextualize complex statistical reports on crime.
(c) Because statistical reports on crime rates are intentionally falsified by researchers to manipulate public policy and confuse elected political representatives.
(d) Because advanced analytical skills allow politicians to objectively uncover the true, hidden meaning of the data, which always opposes drug decriminalization.
(e) Because individuals with higher numeracy rely exclusively on emotional heuristics rather than reviewing the quantitative data provided in statistical reports.

**Answer:** (a)

**Explanation:** The numeracy paradox reveals that individuals with high quantitative reasoning skills show greater political polarization on contested issues because they use their analytical capacity as a tool to selectively dissect and interpret data in ways that confirm their prior beliefs. (b) is incorrect because numeracy and scientific curiosity are distinct traits; high numeracy does not inherently preclude curiosity. (c) is incorrect because the paradox describes cognitive bias in interpreting valid data, not a conspiracy of falsified research. (d) is incorrect because it falls into the trap of assuming motivated cognition is objective truth-seeking rather than selective interpretation. (e) is incorrect because highly numerate individuals do engage with the quantitative data; they just deploy their analytical skills to reach their preferred conclusions rather than relying on pure emotion.
