**Summary Verdict**: Needs Revision. The chapter map accurately reflects the source texts and contains excellent hypo seeds, but requires revision to include two key concepts explicitly taught in the slides and to fix widespread deviations from the required trap formatting.

### Omissions
While the map captures the broad strokes of the chapter well, it omits two explicit, named concepts heavily emphasized in the Slide Digest:
1. **Bentham's Formula (`G < Pr × Pen`)**: The Slide Digest notes that the slides formally teach deterrence as an equation (Slide: "Bentham's Formula"), but the map's `deterrence-rational-actor` refinement only discusses weighing costs and benefits conceptually without mentioning the explicit calculation that students are required to practice.
2. **Marginal Deterrence**: The Slide Digest explicitly notes that "marginal deterrence" is a named concept taught in class (e.g., "equal penalties for theft and murder eliminate the disincentive to kill witnesses"). While the map mentions that punishment shouldn't be "too expensive relative to benefit" (`deterrence-four-limits`), it fails to capture marginal deterrence by name, which is a classic exam application.
