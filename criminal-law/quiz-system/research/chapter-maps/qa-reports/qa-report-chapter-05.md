Here are several examples of non-conforming traps and how they must be fixed:

*   **legality-no-retroactivity**: "Students apply legality only to newly enacted statutes because they forget that retroactive judicial expansion of a statute's scope is also a legality violation." 
    *   *Fix*: "...because they confuse prospective legislative enactments with the prohibition on retroactive judicial expansions."
*   **vagueness-as-applied-vs-facial**: "Students raise a facial vagueness challenge when an as-applied challenge is stronger because they default to the broader argument without analyzing whether the defendant's conduct specifically lacked notice."
    *   *Fix*: "...because they confuse facial unconstitutionality with as-applied unconstitutionality."
*   **lenity-genuine-ambiguity-required**: "Students invoke lenity whenever there is any possible narrower reading because they treat it as a trump card rather than a tiebreaker."

*   **bearden-circumvention-fees-not-fines**: "Students conclude Bearden ended debtors' prisons because they remember the constitutional holding without studying how the decision was formally preserved but substantively circumvented."
