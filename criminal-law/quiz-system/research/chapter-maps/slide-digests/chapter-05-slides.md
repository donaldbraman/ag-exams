### Slide 6
-   **Content:** Title "Limiting Doctrines". Bullet points defining:
    -   **Desuetude:** No use of outdated laws for selective enforcement
    -   **Legality:** Law must be clear, ascertainable and non-retrospective
    -   **Vagueness:** Statutes must be clear enough that an ordinary person will know whether their conduct is criminal or not.
    -   **Lenity:** Where ambiguity exists in the interpretation of a criminal statute, courts should read the statute to support narrow enforcement.
-   **Doctrine:** This slide directly lists and defines the core doctrines discussed in the chapter under "Doctrines That Constrain Criminal Law Interpretation," which are mechanisms for limiting how courts interpret and apply criminal statutes.
-   **Discussion:** The professor would review each doctrine, asking students to explain its purpose and how it might apply to the "common scold" problem or other hypothetical scenarios. "Which of these doctrines did the *Palendrano* court use?"
-   **Practiced:** Desuetude, legality, vagueness, lenity, statutory interpretation.
