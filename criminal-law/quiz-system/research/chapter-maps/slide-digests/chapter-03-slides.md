## Slide-by-Slide Analysis

### Part I: The Core of Criminal Law

---

#### Slide: "What Should Criminal Law Punish?" (Slide 1)
- **Content:** Opens by asking students what a "criminal case" looks like, then names the three families of core offenses: crimes against persons, crimes against property, and fraud/deception.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Three-family taxonomy of core offenses; the intuitive "core" of criminal law.
- **Chapter map keys:** `three-offense-families`, `core-offense-consensus`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Broad Consensus?" (Slide 2)
- **Content:** Cross-cultural studies show people rank offenses similarly in seriousness — but the consensus is "shallower than it appears."
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Surface-level consensus on offense labels vs. underlying disagreement; naturalist account of criminal law.
- **Chapter map keys:** `core-offense-consensus`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Disputes at the Margins" (Slide 3)
- **Content:** Illustrates contested edges of "murder" (defense of property, sleeping abuser, corporate opioid marketing) and contested enforcement gaps (wage theft vs. street theft).
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Boundary cases within accepted offense categories; corporate manslaughter; wage theft non-prosecution; consensus at the category level vs. the boundary.
- **Chapter map keys:** `core-offense-consensus`, `three-offense-families`
- **Practiced:** Yes
- **Note:** Directly activates the "pivot fact" in `core-offense-consensus` — the corporation that markets opioids collapses the simple consensus framing.

---

#### Slide: "The Clear Cases Are Clear" (Slide 4)
- **Content:** Uses the Dan Markel murder (hired killer) as an uncontested case; frames the semester as working within the agreed framework, not at its foundations.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** The uncontested core; where most of criminal law doctrine lives.
- **Chapter map keys:** `core-offense-consensus`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "But Here's What Casebooks Don't Tell You" (Slide 5)
- **Content:** Announces the pivot: "core" offenses are not what the system mostly does; enforcement activity is elsewhere.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Gap between doctrinal curriculum and enforcement reality; introduces system-involvement as the hidden majority.
- **Chapter map keys:** `system-involvement-scale`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

### Part II: What We Actually Enforce

---

#### Slide: "What Percentage of Arrests Are for Violent Crimes?" (Slide 6)
- **Content:** Polling question — students guess the percentage of violent crime arrests.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Student prior belief exercise; sets up the evidence-practice gap.
- **Chapter map keys:** `system-involvement-scale`
- **Practiced:** Yes (active recall prompt)

#### Slide: "The Answer" (Slide 7)
- **Content:** FBI UCR 2024 arrest table — violent crimes 5.6%, property 12.1%, fraud 2.1%, drug offenses 10.4%, "other offenses" ~60%.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Quantitative breakdown of arrest categories; violent + property = under 18%; the 60% "other" category.
- **Chapter map keys:** `system-involvement-scale`, `evidence-grade-hierarchy`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "What's in 'Other Offenses'?" (Slide 8)
- **Content:** Lists the specific system-involvement categories: FTA, bench warrant arrests, driving on suspended license, probation violations, disorderly conduct. Names them as "offenses against the system itself."
- **Doctrinal concepts:** System-involvement offenses defined and enumerated; distinction between offenses against persons/property vs. offenses against the system.
- **Chapter map keys:** `system-involvement-scale`, `three-offense-families`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "The Mismatch" (Slide 9)
- **Content:** Frames the policy puzzle: if criminal law's mission is public safety from violence and theft, why do those categories represent less than a fifth of enforcement?
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Evidence-practice gap framing; institutional legitimacy question; purpose vs. practice of criminal enforcement.
- **Chapter map keys:** `system-involvement-scale`, `bayesian-policy-standard`

#### Slide: "Today's Question" (Slide 10)

### Part III: System-Involvement Offenses

---

#### Slide: "Failure to Appear" (Slide 11)
- **Content:** Presents the policy argument for criminalization vs. the evidence: text reminders reduce FTA by 36% (30,000 fewer warrants), cost under $1 per person; cash bail shows no effect; criminalization has no experimental support.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** FTA reminders evidence; cash bail failure as FTA deterrent; evidence-based vs. criminalization approach.
- **Chapter map keys:** `fta-reminders-evidence`, `cash-bail-fta-failure`, `bayesian-policy-standard`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "FTA: Practice vs. Evidence" (Slide 12)
- **Content:** Most jurisdictions still criminalize rather than remind; 61% of New Mexico warrants are for FTA.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Evidence-practice gap for FTA; scale of the problem.
- **Chapter map keys:** `fta-reminders-evidence`, `system-involvement-scale`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Driver's License Suspension" (Slide 13)
- **Content:** Policy argument for debt-based suspension vs. evidence: no correlation with collection; California collection increased 8.9% after ending suspension; job loss; creates new crime.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Debt-based license suspension ineffectiveness; suspension creates the offense of driving while suspended; California AB 103 natural experiment.
- **Chapter map keys:** `debt-suspension-ineffective`, `bayesian-policy-standard`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "License Suspension: Racial Disparities" (Slide 14)
- **Content:** North Carolina data: Black drivers 21% of adult population, 54% of DWS charges; 25 states reformed, none reversed.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Racial disparities produced by debt-based suspension; structural generation of a racially disparate offense.
- **Chapter map keys:** `debt-suspension-racial-disparity`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Technical Violations" (Slide 15)
- **Content:** Policy argument vs. evidence: 116-study meta-analysis shows custodial sanctions produce null effects or slight increases in reoffending; HOPE failed to replicate; 40% of prison admissions are technical violations; $10 billion annually with no safety return.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Technical violations incarceration null effect; HOPE program failure; supervision violations scale; evidence quality (meta-analysis vs. single-site study).
- **Chapter map keys:** `technical-violations-incarceration`, `hope-program-failure`, `supervision-violations-scale`, `evidence-grade-hierarchy`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "The HOPE Cautionary Tale" (Slide 16)
- **Content:** HOPE showed remarkable initial Hawaii results; 30+ states adopted it; rigorous replication found no effect and more violations than control.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Failed replication; single-site vs. multi-site RCT; the epistemology of policy evidence.
- **Chapter map keys:** `hope-program-failure`, `evidence-grade-hierarchy`
- **Practiced:** Yes
- **Note:** This slide directly operationalizes the `hope-program-failure` trap: students are primed with the promising initial result before being shown the failed replication.

---

#### Slide: "Homelessness" (Slide 17)
- **Content:** Camping ban policy argument vs. evidence: nationwide study shows no significant reduction; Housing First reduces homelessness 88%; post-*Grants Pass* citations surged but homelessness rose 18%.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Criminalization of homelessness ineffectiveness; Housing First evidence; *Grants Pass* aftermath.
- **Chapter map keys:** `criminalization-homelessness-ineffective`, `housing-first-evidence`, `grants-pass-context`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Post-*Grants Pass*" (Slide 18)
- **Content:** Table of citation surges (San Francisco 500%, Stockton 1,400%); national homelessness reached 770,000 in 2024, highest on record.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Empirical aftermath of *Grants Pass*; citation surge ≠ homelessness reduction; scale of the housing crisis.
- **Chapter map keys:** `grants-pass-context`, `criminalization-homelessness-ineffective`
- **Practiced:** Yes

### Part IV: Ferguson

---

#### Slide: "Ferguson: Aberration or Pattern?" (Slide 19)
- **Content:** The DOJ found: Finance Director requested 10% revenue increase; 90,000 citations for population of 21,000; 32,975 arrest warrants in one year for nonviolent offenses.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Ferguson as revenue-extraction system; revenue targets as structural incentive; warrant machine mechanics.
- **Chapter map keys:** `ferguson-revenue-machine`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "The Debt Cascade" (Slide 20)
- **Content:** Traces the debt cascade: missed court date → $125 FTA charge → warrant → more fees → jail → more fees; each new charge creates new failure opportunities.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Debt cascade mechanics; compounding fees as structural trap; how system-involvement offenses generate more system-involvement.
- **Chapter map keys:** `ferguson-revenue-machine`, `keilee-fant-story`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Keilee Fant" (Slide 21)
- **Content:** Certified nursing assistant; jailed over a dozen times for unpaid traffic tickets; held 50 days without soap, shower, or change of clothes; died during the litigation.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Keilee Fant case; human consequences of the debt cascade; inability to pay as the driver of system involvement (not culpability).
- **Chapter map keys:** `keilee-fant-story`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Ferguson: Racial Disparities" (Slide 22)
- **Content:** Table: Black population 67%; vehicle stops 85% Black; citations 90%; arrests 93%; 2013 warrants 92%.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Racial disparities in Ferguson enforcement; documented disparity across every enforcement category.
- **Chapter map keys:** `ferguson-racial-disparities`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Reform Came Only After..." (Slide 23)
- **Content:** National attention, DOJ intervention, a shooting and federal investigation were required for reform; the affected community knew and had known for years.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Structural persistence of the revenue machine; political powerlessness of affected communities; reform only after catastrophic external pressure.
- **Chapter map keys:** `ferguson-revenue-machine`, `ferguson-racial-disparities`
- **Practiced:** Yes

### Part V: The Pattern

---

#### Slide: "Evidence vs. Practice" (Slide 24)
- **Content:** Summary table: FTA (evidence favors reminders, practice = criminalization); License Suspension (evidence favors ending debt-based, practice = half still suspend); Technical Violations (evidence favors treatment, practice = incarceration); Homelessness (evidence favors Housing First, practice = criminalization).
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Evidence-practice gap across all four system-involvement categories; systematic pattern of ignoring evidence.
- **Chapter map keys:** `fta-reminders-evidence`, `debt-suspension-ineffective`, `technical-violations-incarceration`, `criminalization-homelessness-ineffective`, `bayesian-policy-standard`
- **Practiced:** Yes
- **Note:** This is a high-value synthesis slide that operationalizes `bayesian-policy-standard` — all four cells follow the same structure: what do the odds favor vs. what do we actually do?

---

#### Slide: "Why Doesn't Practice Follow Evidence?" (Slide 25)
- **Content:** Five explanations: (1) revenue incentives, (2) political symbolism, (3) institutional inertia, (4) asymmetric burden of proof, (5) political powerlessness of affected populations.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Structural explanations for the evidence-practice gap; five distinct mechanisms; institutional analysis of criminal enforcement.
- **Chapter map keys:** `bayesian-policy-standard`, `ferguson-revenue-machine`, `system-involvement-scale`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "But There's Something Deeper" (Slide 26)
- **Content:** All five explanations are true — but there's something else affecting everyone in the room. Transition to motivated cognition.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Preview of motivated cognition as the deeper structural explanation; personal implication for students.
- **Chapter map keys:** `motivated-cognition-mechanism`
- **Practiced:** Yes

### Part VI: Motivated Cognition

---

#### Slide: "What Is Motivated Cognition?" (Slide 27)
- **Content:** Defines motivated cognition: strong moral commitments cause factual beliefs to fall in line; confirmatory studies seem rigorous, challenging studies seem flawed; does not feel like bias — feels like clear thinking.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Motivated cognition mechanism; confirmation bias; asymmetric skepticism; the subjective experience of motivated reasoning as "clear thinking."
- **Chapter map keys:** `motivated-cognition-mechanism`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "The Troubling Finding" (Slide 28)
- **Content:** Smarter people are MORE susceptible. Higher numeracy → greater political polarization. More numerate partisans are better at selectively interpreting data.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Numeracy paradox; expertise amplifies motivated cognition rather than correcting it; Kahan et al. finding.
- **Chapter map keys:** `numeracy-paradox`, `motivated-cognition-mechanism`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "One Trait Reduces Polarization" (Slide 29)
- **Content:** Science curiosity — intrinsic motivation to seek information for the pleasure of learning — reduces polarization; science-curious partisans treat surprising findings as interesting puzzles.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Science curiosity as partial remedy for motivated cognition; curiosity vs. numeracy distinction; treating challenging evidence as interesting rather than threatening.
- **Chapter map keys:** `science-curiosity-remedy`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "From the Inside" (Slide 30)
- **Content:** Gives voice to motivated cognition in first person: "Maybe the studies are flawed. Maybe the researchers have an agenda." — framing this as selective skepticism in service of prior beliefs.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Phenomenology of motivated reasoning; distinction between genuine skepticism and selective skepticism; lived experience vs. mechanism.
- **Chapter map keys:** `motivated-cognition-mechanism`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

### Part VII: The Flip

---

#### Slide: "You Have It Too" (Slide 31)
- **Content:** Students did well critiquing law-and-order arguments; evidence on system-involvement offenses is clear. Now flip it.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Self-application of motivated cognition; the class's own motivated reasoning on the left side of the debate; transition to contested domains.
- **Chapter map keys:** `motivated-cognition-mechanism`, `contested-domains-hollowed-common-ground`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Contested Domains" (Slide 32)
- **Content:** Lists contested domains: abortion, firearms, gambling, drugs. Both sides claim "the science" supports them; debates never resolve.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Contested domains where motivated cognition operates on both sides; the parallel epistemic problem for progressives and conservatives.
- **Chapter map keys:** `motivated-cognition-mechanism`, `cultural-cognition-guns`, `contested-domains-hollowed-common-ground`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "The Question for You" (Slide 33)
- **Content:** When you read a confirming study, how carefully do you scrutinize the methodology? Is it the same scrutiny as for a challenging study? If honest — probably not.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Personal application of the numeracy paradox; asymmetric methodological scrutiny as the diagnostic test for motivated cognition.
- **Chapter map keys:** `numeracy-paradox`, `motivated-cognition-mechanism`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

### Part VIII: Finding Common Ground

---

#### Slide: "Human-Centered Design" (Slide 34)
- **Content:** HCD methodology: (1) consult affected communities, (2) prototype and test, (3) measure agreed outcomes, (4) iterate. Frame as "does this reduce harm?" not "whose values should prevail?"
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Human-Centered Design as an evidence-based escape from culture war; outcome-focused vs. value-focused framing; HCD as the chapter's proposed methodology.
- **Chapter map keys:** `bayesian-policy-standard` (HCD operationalizes the Bayesian standard), `contested-domains-hollowed-common-ground`
- **Practiced:** Yes
- **Note:** HCD is not a separate refinement key in the chapter map, but it is the methodological application of `bayesian-policy-standard` and the chapter's answer to `contested-domains-hollowed-common-ground`.

---

#### Slide: "Abortion: The Shared Goal" (Slide 35)
- **Content:** The debate focuses on legality; almost no one focuses on reducing the need. Other countries achieve lower abortion rates through comprehensive sex ed, contraception access, pre/post-natal support, paid leave.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** HCD applied to abortion; hollowed-out common ground (reducing abortion rates); shared goal vs. contested means.
- **Chapter map keys:** `contested-domains-hollowed-common-ground`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Firearms: The Crisis We Ignore" (Slide 36)
- **Content:** Debate focuses on mass shootings, self-defense, Second Amendment. Most firearm deaths are suicides — 55-60% of all gun deaths.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Cultural cognition of guns; the contested framing vs. the factual distribution of harm; suicide as the dominant harm mechanism.
- **Chapter map keys:** `cultural-cognition-guns`, `contested-domains-hollowed-common-ground`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "The Non-Divisive Crisis" (Slide 37)
- **Content:** Conservatives and liberals both want fewer suicides; gun owners and non-owners both have struggling family members. Proposed pilots: safe storage, crisis intervention, lethal means counseling, mental health access.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** HCD applied to guns; common ground on suicide prevention (vs. culture war on ownership); evidence-based suicide prevention pilot programs.
- **Chapter map keys:** `contested-domains-hollowed-common-ground`, `cultural-cognition-guns`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Drugs: Real Harm" (Slide 38)
- **Content:** Statistics: 105,000 overdose deaths in 2023; 800,000 opioid deaths since 1999; 178,000 alcohol deaths annually; overdose is leading cause of death under 50. Question is not whether drugs are harmful but what policies reduce harm.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Magnitude of drug harms; harm-reduction framing; shifting from criminalization question to effectiveness question.
- **Chapter map keys:** `criminalization-drug-use-weak`, `mat-evidence-gap`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "What the Evidence Says Works" (Slide 39)
- **Content:** MAT reduces overdose deaths; naloxone 27-46% reduction; needle exchange reduces disease without increasing use; criminal penalties show weak deterrence evidence. Portugal decriminalized 2001: use didn't increase, deaths fell.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** MAT evidence base; naloxone distribution effectiveness; needle exchange; criminalization-drug-use-weak; Portugal natural experiment.
- **Chapter map keys:** `mat-evidence-gap`, `criminalization-drug-use-weak`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "Why Don't We Do What Works?" (Slide 40)
- **Content:** Moral commitments shape factual beliefs; institutional inertia; political incentives; motivated cognition affects advocates too; Oregon Measure 110: decriminalization without infrastructure is incomplete.
- **Doctrinal concepts:** Application of motivated cognition to drug policy advocates; Oregon implementation failure; the implementation evidence requirement.
- **Chapter map keys:** `motivated-cognition-mechanism`, `oregon-implementation-failure`
- **Practiced:** Yes

---

#### Slide: "HCD in Action" (Slide 41)
- **Content:** Three programs: LEAD (58% less likely to be arrested after enrollment); Denver STAR (health workers instead of police; reduced criminal charges, no crime increase); Rhode Island MAT (61% drop in post-release overdose deaths).
- **Doctrinal concepts:** LEAD deflection evidence; Denver STAR alternative response; Rhode Island MAT in corrections; HCD as working model.
- **Chapter map keys:** `lead-deflection`, `mat-evidence-gap`
- **Practiced:** Yes
