## Element Verification

### Prima Facie Elements
- [x] **Reasonable belief in necessity** — Confirmed. Chapter Part I: "The defendant must believe that defensive force is needed, and that belief must be reasonable." NY Penal Law § 35.15(1): "reasonably believes."
- [x] **Reasonable belief in deadly threat (for deadly force)** — Confirmed. § 35.15(2)(a): "reasonably believes that such other person is using or about to use deadly physical force." Chapter Part I.
- [x] **Proportionality** — Confirmed. Chapter Part I: "The force used in self-defense must be roughly proportional to the force threatened. Force capable of causing death or serious bodily injury is justified only against a threat of death or serious bodily injury."
- [x] **Imminence** — Confirmed. Chapter Part I: "The threatened harm must be immediate." Core element.
- [x] **Justification classification** — Confirmed. Chapter opening sentence: "Self-defense is the paradigmatic justification: the defendant claims the use of force was *right*."
