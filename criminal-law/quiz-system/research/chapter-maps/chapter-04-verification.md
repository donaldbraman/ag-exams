### Holdings Accuracy

**Duncan v. Louisiana**
- Map: "The Sixth Amendment jury trial right is incorporated against the states via the Fourteenth Amendment because jury trial is fundamental to the American scheme of justice, and applies to serious offenses carrying potential imprisonment of more than six months."
- Chapter: "Because we believe that trial by jury in criminal cases is fundamental to the American scheme of justice, we hold that the Fourteenth Amendment guarantees a right of jury trial in all criminal cases which, were they to be tried in a federal court, would come within the Sixth Amendment's guarantee."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate.

**United States v. Kleinman**
- Map: "A court may instruct a jury that it cannot substitute its sense of justice for its duty to follow the law, but instructions implying jurors could be punished for nullification or that nullification is impossible are erroneous (though subject to harmless error review, not structural error)."
- Chapter: "The court's statement that the jury 'would violate [its] oath and the law if [it] willfully brought a verdict contrary to the law given to [it] in this case,' could be construed to imply that nullification could be punished... Accordingly, we find that the last two sentences of the trial court's nullification instruction were technically erroneous... [applying] harmless error analysis."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate.

**United States v. Dougherty**
- Map: "While juries possess an 'undoubted power' to nullify, they have no right to be informed of this power; courts may decline to give nullification instructions and may take steps to prevent nullification."
- Chapter: "Judge Leventhal, writing for the majority, acknowledged the jury's 'undoubted jury prerogative-in-fact' to nullify, but refused to require that juries be informed of this power."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate.

**State v. Gregory / McCleskey**
- Map: "Statistical evidence showing Black defendants 4.5 times more likely to receive death sentences than similarly situated white defendants."
- Chapter: "*Black defendants were 4.5 times more likely to be sentenced to death than similarly situated white defendants*, even after controlling for variables like number of victims, aggravating factors, and criminal history."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate. Map correctly distinguishes *Gregory* (state constitution, statistical evidence sufficient) from *McCleskey* (federal constitution, individual discriminatory intent required).

**Singer v. United States**
- Map: "A defendant has no right to a bench trial over the government's objection."
- Chapter: "*Singer v. United States* (1965) upheld this arrangement, noting rare exceptions for circumstances involving 'passion, prejudice... public feeling.'"
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate. Map correctly captures both the general holding and the narrow exception.
