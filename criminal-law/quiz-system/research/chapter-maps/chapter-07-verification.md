### Holdings Accuracy

**People v. Newton**
- Map: "Trial court committed prejudicial error by refusing to instruct jury on unconsciousness as complete defense when substantial evidence supported inference Newton was unconscious."
- Chapter: "Where evidence of involuntary unconsciousness has been produced in a homicide prosecution, the refusal of a requested instruction on the subject, and its effect as a complete defense if found to have existed, is prejudicial error."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate.

**People v. James**
- Map: "Trial court erred by refusing to instruct on unconsciousness during guilt phase; psychotic episode defendant may raise unconsciousness even while pleading insanity."
- Chapter: "The evidentiary record included substantial evidence that appellant was unconscious ... and the trial court erred in refusing to instruct the jury on that defense."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate.

**Jacobs v. Commonwealth**
- Map: "Evidence of 'excitable temperament' is not a defense; conscious emotional states do not negate voluntary act."
- Chapter: "The court's reasoning rested on a fundamental principle. Evidence of an excitable temperament was offered not to show that Jacobs was insane or unconscious, but merely that he was 'a man of quick temper...'"
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate.

**Martin v. State**
- Map: "Defendant cannot be convicted when officers forcibly transported him to public location; voluntary appearance is presupposed."
- Chapter: "'Under the plain terms of this statute, a voluntary appearance is presupposed.' A person cannot be convicted of appearing drunk in public when police officers forcibly carried him there."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate.

**Beltz v. State**
- Map: "Voluntary act was continuing to possess after receiving notice; indictment reversed because no notice given."
- Chapter: "the court reversed Beltz's indictment because there was no evidence he had actually received such notice."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate. Map correctly captures both the holding and the actual disposition.

**State v. Ireland**
- Map: "Blackout/unconsciousness is an affirmative defense in Ohio; defendant bears burden of proving by preponderance."
- Chapter: "the court instructed the jury that blackout is an *affirmative defense* and that Ireland bore the burden of proving it by a preponderance of the evidence."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate.

**People v. Decina**
- Map: "Indictment sufficient; voluntary act was conscious decision to drive despite known seizure disorder."
- Chapter: "this defendant knew he was subject to epileptic attacks and seizures that might strike at any time." Court framed relevant conduct as "the conscious choice to get behind the wheel despite a known risk."
- **Status:** ✅ Accurate.

### Pivot Fact Accuracy

**`mpc-voluntary-act-definition`**: Pivot fact is "if the movement was a reflex triggered by a startling stimulus rather than a conscious decision." This is accurate — MPC 2.01(2)(a) excludes reflexes.

**`unconsciousness-complete-defense`**: Pivot fact is "if Newton had not been shot before firing, no unconsciousness inference." Accurate and well-grounded in the chapter's factual discussion.

**`psychotic-break-unconsciousness`**: Pivot fact is "if James had been aware of what he was doing during the episode." Directly supported by the court's discussion: awareness vs. lack of awareness is the distinguishing criterion.

**`jacobs-rule-impulse-no-defense`**: Pivot fact is "if Jacobs had been unconscious rather than enraged, the result might differ." Accurate — the court's holding turns entirely on the distinction between conscious and unconscious states.

**`martin-principle-state-created-actus-reus`**: Pivot fact is "if Martin had walked out of his house of his own accord." Accurate and directly supported by the statute's "voluntary appearance" language.

**`beltz-continuing-possession-voluntary-act`**: Pivot fact correctly identifies the notice issue as the reason the indictment was reversed. Accurate.

**`time-framing-seizure-driving`**: Pivot fact is "if Decina had never been diagnosed with epilepsy." Accurate — foreseeability of the seizure is the doctrinal hinge.

**`burden-minority-defendant`**: Pivot fact "if Ireland's expert testimony had been more compelling." This is functionally accurate but slightly weak as a pivot fact — it describes sufficiency of evidence, not a factual change that would flip the legal rule. More precise pivot would be: "If Ohio classified unconsciousness as negating an element rather than as an affirmative defense, Ireland would win even with the same evidence."

- **Severity:** LOW — does not affect doctrinal accuracy; pivot fact as written is still pedagogically useful.

**Status:** One minor weakness in `burden-minority-defendant` pivot fact.
